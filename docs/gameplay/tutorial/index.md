# Tutorials

Welcome to the MCSR Ranked tutorial section. Whether you are brand new to Minecraft speedrunning or transitioning from casual Any% into the ranked ladder, this section will walk you through everything you need to compete — from your very first seed to advanced optimizations that save seconds per phase.

---

## What Is MCSR Ranked?

MCSR Ranked is a **1v1 competitive format** of Minecraft Java Edition speedrunning. Two players race simultaneously on separate, randomly generated seeds with the same goal: **kill the Ender Dragon as fast as possible**. The first player to receive the credits screen wins the match.

Matches are played on the **MCSR Ranked mod**, which handles matchmaking, seed generation, and result verification automatically. Players earn and lose **Elo rating** based on wins and losses, and the distribution of seed types they receive scales with their Elo bracket.

Key rules to know:
- **Random Seed** — you cannot see or choose your seed in advance.
- **Set Seed is not used** — every run is a fresh, unknown world.
- **Glitchless** — major glitches like credits warping are banned; standard Minecraft mechanics apply.
- **Java Edition only** — typically the current or a recent stable release.

The race is real-time: you must be fast *and* consistent. A clean, moderate run will beat a brilliant but mistake-prone one more often than not.

## The Three Phases of a Run

Every MCSR Ranked run moves through three distinct phases. Understanding these phases is the foundation of all further learning.

### 1. Overworld
The run begins the moment your world loads. Your objective is to gather the resources needed to enter and survive the Nether, primarily **a diamond pickaxe** (or iron tools as a fallback), **enough food**, and **a way into the Nether** (obsidian + flint and steel, or a pre-built Ruined Portal).

The specific path you take through the overworld depends entirely on your **seed type** — the dominant structure that spawns near your start. See the [Overworld Index](./overworld_index.md) for details on each approach.

### 2. Nether
Once through the portal, your goal is to collect **Blaze Rods** from a Nether Fortress and **Ender Pearls** from Piglins via Bartering at a Bastion Remnant. The order in which you tackle these objectives (Bastion first or Fortress first) and the type of Bastion you receive heavily influence your route and timing.

See the [Nether Index](./nether_index.md) for Bastion types, Fortress navigation, and optimal routing.

### 3. Endgame
With Blaze Powder and Ender Pearls in hand, you craft **Eyes of Ender**, use them to locate a Stronghold, navigate to the End Portal room, and kill the Ender Dragon as efficiently as possible.

See the [Endgame Index](./endgame_index.md) for blind travel math, stronghold navigation, and the dragon fight.

## Seed Types at a Glance

Your starting position in the overworld is determined by your **seed type** — a classification based on which key structure spawns closest to you. Each seed type has its own strategy, resource order, and expected timing.

| Seed Type | Key Feature | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| **Village** | A village (possibly with blacksmith) near spawn | Beginner-friendly; very common |
| **Shipwreck** | A shipwreck containing a treasure map and/or supplies | Moderate; requires map reading |
| **Desert Temple** | A desert pyramid containing loot chests | Moderate; TNT trap awareness |
| **Ruined Portal** | A partially built Nether Portal near spawn | Fast but resource-dependent |
| **Buried Treasure** | A chest buried on a beach, found via treasure map | Advanced; requires quick digging |

At lower Elo ratings, Village and Shipwreck seeds are most common. As your Elo increases, more difficult and faster seed types appear more frequently. Full seed distribution percentages by Elo bracket are listed in the [Overworld Index](./overworld_index.md).

## Recommended Learning Order for Beginners

Follow this path to get your first wins as efficiently as possible:

1. **Read this page** — understand the overall flow of a run.
2. **Read the [Overworld Index](./overworld_index.md)** — learn what seed types exist and what resources you always need.
3. **Learn Village seeds first** — they are forgiving and teach you core resource gathering habits. Read the [Village](./overworld/village.md) subpage.
4. **Play 20–30 ranked matches** using only Village strategy. Focus on consistency, not speed.
5. **Read the [Nether Index](./nether_index.md)** — understand Bastion types and basic Fortress routing.
6. **Learn Shipwreck seeds** — the second most common seed type. Read the [Shipwreck](./overworld/shipwreck.md) subpage.
7. **Read the [Endgame Index](./endgame_index.md)** and practice blind throws with Ninjabrain Bot.
8. **Read the [Techniques Index](./technique_index.md)** and begin incorporating efficiency habits.
9. Gradually unlock and learn **Desert Temple**, **Ruined Portal**, and **Buried Treasure** as they appear in your matchmaking.

> **Tip:** Speed comes from consistency, not from copying advanced runners. Master the basics before optimizing splits.

## Recommended Practice Tools

The following tools are widely used by the MCSR Ranked community and will meaningfully accelerate your improvement:

### Ninjabrain Bot
**Ninjabrain Bot** is an overlay application that calculates the stronghold location from your Eye of Ender throws in real time. It reads your game's F3 debug data and tells you exactly how far to travel and in which direction. Using Ninjabrain Bot correctly is essential for consistent sub-10-minute endgame performance.

- Download: [github.com/Ninjabrain1/Ninjabrain-Bot](https://github.com/Ninjabrain1/Ninjabrain-Bot)
- See also: [Blind Travel & Eye Throws](./endgame/blind_travel.md)

### Dibedy Practice Map
The **Dibedy overworld practice map** is a custom Minecraft world that lets you practice individual overworld seed types in isolation. It generates controlled scenarios for Village, Shipwreck, Buried Treasure, and more, so you can repeat the same type of scenario hundreds of times without playing full ranked matches.

- Find it in the **MCSR Ranked Discord** `#resources` channel.

### SeedQueue
**SeedQueue** is a mod that pre-generates seeds in the background and allows you to quickly cycle through them during practice sessions. It is primarily used for single-player speedrun grinding, but is also useful for MCSR Ranked players who want high-volume overworld or nether practice without waiting for world load times.

- Download: [modrinth.com/mod/seedqueue](https://modrinth.com/mod/seedqueue)
