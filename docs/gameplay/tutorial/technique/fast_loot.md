
# Techniques — Fast Loot

> [!IMPORTANT]
> In MCSR Ranked, seconds are the currency of victory. Every chest you loot inefficiently, every item you pick up that you don't need, and every second you spend standing still reorganizing your inventory is time your opponent is spending running forward. Fast looting is not optional — it is a core competency.

## 1. Introduction

### Why Fast Looting Matters

A full MCSR Ranked run typically lasts between **6 and 15 minutes** depending on player skill level. Within that window, a player may interact with **5 to 12 or more chests** across the Overworld, Nether, and Stronghold. The time spent looting each chest — opening it, scanning items, taking what you need, and closing it — adds up enormously.

Consider these comparisons:

- A beginner might spend **20–30 seconds** at a blacksmith chest, manually clicking each item into their inventory one at a time.
- An experienced runner does the same chest in **4–6 seconds** using keyboard shortcuts and pre-planned priority.

That single chest represents a **15–25 second difference**. Across an entire run, inefficient looting can cost **1–3 minutes** — the difference between winning and losing a ranked match at nearly any ELO bracket.

### What "Fast Looting" Actually Means

Fast looting is not about clicking faster. It is about:

- **Knowing what you need before you open the chest** so you can make decisions instantly.
- **Using keyboard shortcuts** instead of slow manual drag-and-drop.
- **Skipping items you don't need** rather than looting everything and sorting later.
- **Keeping moving** — reducing the time you are standing still.

This page covers all of these skills.

## 2. Core Inventory Techniques

These are the keyboard and mouse shortcuts that form the foundation of fast looting. **Master all of these.** They work in chests, crafting tables, furnaces, and your personal inventory.

### Shift + Click

**The single most important inventory technique in the game.**

- **What it does**: Instantly moves an entire stack of items from a chest to your inventory (or vice versa) in one click.
- **How to use**: Hold `Shift` and left-click any item stack.
- **Why it matters**: Eliminates the need to drag items across the screen. One click instead of three.

```
Without Shift+Click:  Click item → Hold → Drag to inventory → Release = 3 actions
With Shift+Click:     Shift + Click = 1 action
```

This alone will cut your chest looting time in half compared to drag-and-drop. If you do nothing else from this guide, learn Shift+Click.

### Number Keys (1–9): Direct Hotbar Assignment

- **What it does**: While hovering your cursor over an item in a chest or inventory, pressing a number key (1–9) **immediately moves that item to the corresponding hotbar slot**.
- **How to use**: Open a chest → hover your mouse cursor over an item (don't click) → press `1` through `9`.
- **Why it matters**: Lets you place critical items directly into your intended hotbar position without any drag-and-drop. Faster and more precise than Shift+Click when hotbar organization matters.

**Example**: You see a bow in a chest. You want it in hotbar slot 3. Hover over the bow → press `3`. Done.

> [!TIP]
> Combine this with a **planned hotbar layout** (see Section 4) so you always know which number key to press for each item type. With practice, this becomes muscle memory.

### Double-Click: Collect All of One Type

- **What it does**: Double-clicking an item in your inventory **collects all stacks of that item type** from anywhere in your inventory into one stack (up to the stack limit).
- **How to use**: Have at least one of an item in your inventory. Double-click it in your inventory screen.
- **Why it matters**: Consolidates split stacks instantly. Useful after looting multiple chests that each gave you a few pieces of the same item (e.g., food, arrows, iron ingots).

> [!NOTE]
> Double-click does **not** pull items from chests — it only consolidates from your own inventory. It also will not work if you have a full stack already.

### Right-Click Drag: Split and Distribute Stacks

- **What it does**: Clicking a stack with the right mouse button picks up **half the stack**. Dragging while holding right-click distributes items evenly across slots you drag over.
- **How to use**: Right-click a stack to pick up half. Drag over empty slots to split it.
- **Why it matters**: Useful for splitting a stack of beds to place them efficiently, distributing food, or splitting crafting materials.

In fast looting scenarios, you'll use right-click drag sparingly — but it's essential for preparing beds before The End or splitting resources.

### Ctrl + Q: Drop Entire Stack

- **What it does**: While hovering over an item in your inventory or a chest, `Ctrl+Q` drops the **entire stack** on the ground instantly.
- **How to use**: Hover over any item stack → press `Ctrl+Q`.
- **Why it matters**: Fastest way to discard unwanted items during looting without dragging them to the edge of the screen. Also useful for emergency inventory clearing.

> [!WARNING]
> Items dropped on the ground in the Nether or The End will be destroyed if they touch lava or the void. Be careful where you're standing when you use Ctrl+Q.

### Q: Drop Single Item

- **What it does**: Drops **one item** from the currently selected hotbar slot.
- **How to use**: Press `Q` while holding any item.
- **Why it matters**: Useful for discarding a single item (e.g., one wooden sword you no longer need) without opening your inventory.

### Summary Table

| Shortcut | Action | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| `Shift + Click` | Move entire stack instantly | Every chest, every time |
| `1–9` (hover) | Send item to hotbar slot | Placing key items into hotbar |
| `Double-Click` | Consolidate all of one type | After looting multiple chests |
| `Right-Click Drag` | Split/distribute stacks | Preparing beds, splitting food |
| `Ctrl + Q` | Drop entire stack | Discarding unwanted items fast |
| `Q` | Drop one item | Tossing single unneeded item |

## 3. Chest Looting Order and Priority

Knowing what to take before you open a chest is just as important as how you take it. Here are the looting priorities for the most common MCSR Ranked structures:

### Blacksmith (Village)

The Blacksmith chest is often the first chest of a run, usually found within seconds of spawning. Time here is especially precious because it sets the tone for your entire run.

**Priority Order:**
1. **Iron ingots / iron armor / iron tools** — The core reason to visit a blacksmith. Iron armor and tools are essential for Nether entry.
2. **Obsidian** — Can substitute or supplement your crafted Nether Portal obsidian requirement.
3. **Food** (bread, apples) — Take if you're short on food from the overworld.
4. **Diamonds** — Extremely lucky find. Take immediately.
5. **Saddle** — Not needed in MCSR Ranked speedrunning; skip.
6. **Everything else** — Buckets, horse armor, armor stands: **skip entirely**.

> [!TIP]
> **Open the blacksmith chest while mentally listing your needs before you click.** If you already have iron armor from other sources, don't waste time Shift+Clicking every iron ingot — take only what fills your gaps.

**Target time at blacksmith:** 5–8 seconds from opening to closing the chest.

---

### Desert Temple

Desert Temples contain **4 chests** buried underground beneath a pressure plate (which triggers a TNT trap). There are two approaches:

#### Approach A — Dig to the Chests Manually
- Dig down on the side (not the center) to avoid the pressure plate.
- Shift+Click through each chest in priority order:
  1. **Diamonds** — always take
  2. **Gold ingots / gold** — useful for Nether bartering
  3. **Iron ingots** — take if needed
  4. **Emeralds** — low priority, skip unless you have room
  5. **String / saddles / rotten flesh / bones** — skip

#### Approach B — TNT Method (Faster)
- Dig a small hole on the blue block (the center structure) at ground level, looking down into the TNT chamber.
- Light the TNT or throw a flint-and-steel spark at it.
- The TNT detonates, breaks the pressure plate, and **opens all 4 chests simultaneously** (destroying some items in the explosion).
- Quickly drop down and Shift+Click everything from all 4 chests at once.
- **This is faster overall** because it eliminates the need to carefully navigate to each chest separately.

> [!NOTE]
> The TNT method destroys some items — usually around 50–70% of lower-tier loot survives. Diamonds and gold (being rare drops) are sometimes lost. Weigh this risk based on how badly you need the items vs. time saved.

---

### Shipwreck

Shipwrecks contain **up to 3 chests** in different sections of the ship. They are often partially buried or underwater.

**Chest Types and Priority:**

| Chest Location | Contents | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Treasure Chest** (stern/back) | Diamonds, emeralds, gold, iron | **Highest — always visit** |
| **Supply Chest** (bow/front) | Food, TNT, paper, leather | Visit if you need food |
| **Map Chest** (middle) | Buried treasure map | Only if doing treasure route |

**Looting order:**
1. Go straight to the **treasure chest** at the stern (back of the ship). This is the most valuable.
2. Check the **supply chest** only if you're low on food or TNT.
3. Only open the **map chest** if your strategy requires a buried treasure route (common in SSG seeds).

> [!TIP]
> Shipwrecks can be waterlogged. Bring a **door** to breathe underwater (place it, stand in the air pocket it creates). Alternatively, drink a **Water Breathing potion** if you have one, or just hold your breath and work quickly.

**Target time at shipwreck**: 10–20 seconds depending on depth and what you need.

---

### Bastion Remnant (Nether)

Bastions are one of the most complex and dangerous loot structures. Fast looting here requires clear priority and nerves of steel.

**Priority Items:**
1. **Gold blocks** (on the surface near hoglin stables or treasure room) — valuable for bartering
2. **Chest loot** in the treasure room or other rooms — can contain gold, iron, chains, gilded blackstone
3. **Ancient Debris / Netherite** — only if visible and accessible quickly
4. **Lodestones, chains, etc.** — skip

**Critical Rules for Bastion Looting:**
- **Do not stop and evaluate every item.** Know your priorities before you enter.
- **Barter while you loot.** Throw gold at piglins and loot chests simultaneously — don't wait for barter results before moving.
- **Avoid killing piglins if possible.** Aggro'd piglins near loot chests create dangerous looting scenarios.
- **Use Shift+Click rapidly** — do not hand-drag anything in a bastion chest.
- **Get in, get your items, get out.** Don't explore every room. Know the bastion type (Bridge, Housing, Stables, Treasure) and where the loot is before entering.

> [!WARNING]
> Bastion Treasure Rooms are guarded by **Piglin Brutes** — they are always hostile, deal massive damage, and cannot be made passive with gold. Clear them or avoid them.

## 4. Inventory Management

### Recommended Hotbar Layout

Having a consistent hotbar layout is essential. Your hands need to know where everything is without thinking. Here is a common MCSR Ranked runner hotbar arrangement:

| Slot | Item |
|---|---|
| 1 | Sword |
| 2 | Pickaxe |
| 3 | Bow |
| 4 | Food (cooked meat / golden apple) |
| 5 | Blocks (for pillaring) |
| 6 | Flint and Steel / Fire Charge |
| 7 | Ender Pearls |
| 8 | Beds (for Nether / End) |
| 9 | Water Bucket / Torch / Misc |

> [!NOTE]
> This layout is a **starting point**, not a rule. Adapt it to your playstyle. The key is that you use the **same layout every single run** so it becomes reflex.

When you pick up a new item from a chest, immediately use the number key shortcut to send it to its designated slot. Don't let items pile up in random inventory positions.

### When and What to Drop

Your inventory fills up quickly in a speedrun. Know when to drop things:

**Drop these early:**
- **Wooden and stone tools** once you have iron equivalents — they're dead weight.
- **Dirt, gravel, sand** picked up while digging (keep a small stack for pillaring but discard bulk).
- **Excess food** if you have more than 10 pieces of cooked food — you won't eat more than that.
- **Cobblestone excess** — keep ~16 for crafting but dump the rest.
- **Seeds, saplings, flowers** — zero speedrun value; never pick these up in the first place.

**Keep these:**
- Ender pearls (all of them — you'll need them for stronghold navigation)
- Blaze rods (all — you need these for eyes of ender)
- Gold (all — bartering fuel)
- Iron (keep until you have full armor + tools, then dump ingots)
- Beds (bring 10–12 into The End)

### Stacking Items Efficiently

- After looting multiple chests of the same item, **double-click** to consolidate stacks.
- Avoid having the same item in multiple inventory slots — it wastes space and makes it hard to know how much you have.
- Sort your inventory in broad categories: **combat items in hotbar, crafting materials in upper inventory, food in one spot**.

### The "Don't Pick It Up" Rule

The fastest way to deal with a useless item is to **never pick it up in the first place.** When you see a chest full of mixed items:

- Identify the 1–3 things you need with your eyes first.
- Shift+Click only those items.
- Close the chest.
- Walk away.

Do not Shift+Click the entire chest and sort afterward. **Pre-filtering is always faster than post-sorting.**

## 5. Movement While Looting

One of the biggest time sinks beginners don't notice is **standing still when they don't have to.**

### Continue Moving Toward Your Next Objective

- After closing a chest, **immediately start sprinting** toward your next destination. Don't pause to reorganize your inventory while standing still — do it while moving.
- You can open your inventory (`E`) while running and reorganize items without stopping your character's momentum. Your character keeps moving in the last direction you were going.

> [!TIP]
> This is especially useful between the Overworld and Nether portal. Run toward your portal while Shift+Clicking items around in your inventory to make room.

### Craft on the Move

Many crafting operations can be done while moving:

- **2x2 crafting grid** (accessible via `E`) works while you're running — craft basic items like torches, ladders, or beds without stopping.
- **Crafting tables** require you to stand near one and open it, but you can still hold movement keys.
- Plan your crafting so you do it while traveling between structures rather than stopping in place.

**Common items to craft while moving:**
- Beds (3 wool + 3 planks) — do this while running to the Nether portal
- Ender eyes — craft these while running to the stronghold
- Torches — craft as needed during cave travel
- Bowls / basic tools — whenever you have a moment

### Opening Inventory While Sprinting

You do not lose sprint momentum when you open your inventory (`E`). You can:
- Sort items
- Craft in the 2x2 grid
- Eat food
- Organize hotbar slots

All while your character continues to sprint forward. This is one of the easiest ways to save passive time throughout a run.

### Pre-Positioning for Chest Interaction

As you approach a chest, angle your camera toward the chest while still running so that the moment you stop, you're already facing it. This cuts 0.5–1 second off every chest interaction — small individually, but significant across 10+ chests.

## 6. Common Time Wastes to Avoid

These are the most frequent looting mistakes that cost runners significant time. Study this list and actively work to eliminate these habits:

### Looting Every Chest Even When You Have What You Need

**The mistake**: You already have full iron armor, a sword, food, and everything you need — but you still open every chest in the dungeon "just in case."

**Why it hurts**: Each chest takes 3–10 seconds to open, scan, and close. Three unnecessary chests = up to 30 seconds wasted.

**The fix**: Before opening any chest, ask yourself: *"Do I have a specific gap in my supplies that this chest could fill?"* If the answer is no, walk past it.

---

### Organizing Inventory When It's Not Necessary

**The mistake**: Stopping to drag items into a neat grid, sorting by type, making everything "look clean" in the inventory screen.

**Why it hurts**: A run is 6–15 minutes. Your inventory will be messy. That is okay. You only need to find items in your **hotbar** instantly — the rest of your inventory just needs to not be full.

**The fix**: Only organize the hotbar. Let the main inventory be chaotic. The only time you should "sort" is if you literally cannot tell what you have, which is rare.

---

### Picking Up Useless Blocks and Items

**The mistake**: Automatically picking up everything that pops out of broken blocks — dirt, gravel, flint, seeds, saplings, rotten flesh from zombie fights.

**Why it hurts**: A full inventory forces you to stop and drop things later at a worse time.

**The fix**: Keep a mental "do not pick up" list:
- Seeds, saplings, flowers: **never**
- Excess dirt/gravel beyond what you need for pillaring: **drop immediately**
- Rotten flesh: **only if you have no other food**
- String: **only if you need a bow**
- Pumpkins, melons, cactus: **never**

---

### Reading Item Names/Tooltips During Chest Looting

**The mistake**: Hovering over items and waiting to read the tooltip to decide whether to take them.

**Why it hurts**: Tooltips don't appear instantly — there's a small delay. Waiting for them on 10 items per chest adds up.

**The fix**: Know your item priorities so well that **you recognize items visually** by their icon before any text appears. This comes from practice and VOD review.

---

### Inventory Full Panic

**The mistake**: Opening a chest, finding great loot, realizing your inventory is full, then frantically and randomly dropping things to make room — often making poor decisions about what to drop.

**Why it hurts**: Panic drops are slow and you often discard the wrong items. Then you have to reconsider, pick things back up, drop something else — it cascades into a 20+ second disaster.

**The fix**: **Pre-emptively manage your inventory.** After every major loot stop, do a quick scan: identify the 3–5 items you're most likely to want to drop if space becomes an issue. Then the next time you need space, you know exactly what to drop without hesitation.

---

### Not Knowing What a Structure Contains Before Entering

**The mistake**: Running into a Bastion, Desert Temple, or Shipwreck with no plan, then spending time figuring out which way to go and which chests to open.

**Why it hurts**: Every second of "which way is the treasure chest again?" is a direct time loss.

**The fix**: **Study structure layouts.** Know exactly where the blacksmith chest is in every village type. Know the three locations of Bastion treasure rooms by type. Know that Shipwreck treasure is always at the stern. This knowledge pays off on every single run.

---

### Waiting for Barter Results Before Continuing

**The mistake**: Throwing gold to piglins and then standing there waiting to see what they give you before moving on.

**Why it hurts**: Barter RNG takes 6–8 seconds per trade. If you're waiting for each one, you're wasting time.

**The fix**: Throw gold and immediately start doing something else — looting the next chest, running to the fortress, clearing a nearby room. The barter results will be on the floor when you come back.

## 7. Practice Recommendations

Fast looting is a skill that improves through **deliberate practice**, not just playing more runs. Here's how to structure your practice:

### Drill Individual Shortcuts in Creative Mode

Open a creative mode world, fill a chest with mixed items, and practice:

1. Shift+Clicking every item into inventory within 3 seconds.
2. Using number keys to send 5 specific items to specific hotbar slots.
3. Double-clicking to consolidate scattered stacks.
4. Ctrl+Q to clear unwanted items instantly.

Repeat until all of these feel completely automatic — no conscious thought required.

### Time Yourself at Each Structure

In practice runs, use a split timer (LiveSplit or SpeedrunIGT) and add a split for each major loot stop:

- Split at: Village chest, Nether Portal light, Bastion entry, Fortress entry, Eye throw, End entry, Dragon death.
- After each run, review your split times and identify which structure took the longest.
- Focus your practice on the slowest structures.

### Watch High-ELO Runs in Real Time

Find high-ELO MCSR Ranked matches on [mcsrranked.com](https://mcsrranked.com) or Twitch and pay attention specifically to:
- How fast they move through chests.
- What they skip.
- How their hotbar is organized.
- When they craft (while running vs. stationary).

Watching 30 minutes of top-player runs at 0.5x playback speed to study inventory interaction is more valuable than 2 hours of unfocused play.

### Set Personal Looting Time Goals

Give yourself targets to beat:

| Structure | Beginner Target | Intermediate Target | Advanced Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Blacksmith chest | < 15 seconds | < 8 seconds | < 5 seconds |
| Desert Temple (4 chests) | < 45 seconds | < 25 seconds | < 15 seconds |
| Shipwreck (2 chests) | < 30 seconds | < 15 seconds | < 10 seconds |
| Bastion loot pass | < 60 seconds | < 35 seconds | < 20 seconds |

Use a stopwatch during practice and measure your actual times against these goals. Once you consistently hit an intermediate target, push for the advanced one.

### The Muscle Memory Principle

The goal of all this practice is to make looting **automatic and unconscious**. When your hands know what to do without your brain having to direct them, you free up your mental bandwidth to think about routing, decisions, and your opponent's pace. That cognitive freedom is what separates good runners from great ones.

> [!TIP]
> Start every practice session by doing 5 minutes of pure inventory shortcut drilling in creative mode before doing a live seed. This "warms up" your muscle memory and you'll be noticeably faster in your actual runs immediately after.
