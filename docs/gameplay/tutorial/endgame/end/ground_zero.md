## What Is Ground Zero?

**Ground Zero** is a variant of the Zero Cycle strategy in which the runner kills the Ender Dragon **without ever climbing an obsidian pillar**. Instead, the setup is performed entirely at **ground level**, typically on or immediately adjacent to the central exit fountain structure.

The kill mechanism is the same as standard zero cycle — **bed explosions aimed at the dragon's head** during its brief node hover. However, instead of being on the pillar looking horizontally at the head, you are **on the ground, aiming upward** into the head as the dragon hovers above.

This technique is called "Ground Zero" because:
- "Zero" = killing the dragon before it perches (zero perches).
- "Ground" = the entire setup and execution happens at ground level.

---

## Key Differences from Regular Zero Cycle

| Factor | Standard Zero Cycle | Ground Zero |
|---|---|---|
| Setup location | On or near pillar top | Ground level / fountain |
| Pillar climbing required | Yes | No |
| Aim direction | Horizontal (toward head) | Vertical/diagonal (upward into head) |
| Setup speed | Slower (must climb) | Faster (no climbing) |
| Execution difficulty | High | Very High |
| Mouse precision required | Moderate | Pixel-precise |
| Risk of fall damage | Yes (on pillar) | No |
| Time saved vs. One Cycle | ~35–55 seconds | ~40–65 seconds |
| Recommended for | Intermediate-Advanced | Advanced only |

The defining challenge of Ground Zero is the **vertical angle requirement**. Hitting a dragon head that is 40–70 blocks above you with a bed explosion requires knowing the exact mouse look angle to aim the explosion upward into the hitbox. This cannot be eyeballed reliably — it must be practiced to muscle memory or assisted with a zoom macro.

## Why It's Used

Ground Zero is used by high-level runners for one primary reason: **time saved from eliminating pillar climbing**.

### Time Cost of Pillar Climbing

In standard zero cycle:
- Reaching a tall pillar top via blocks takes approximately **5–10 seconds**.
- Reaching via ender pearl takes approximately **1–3 seconds** (but costs a pearl).
- Building the M85 or similar setup at the top takes additional time.

In Ground Zero:
- You are already at the fountain area from crystal clearing.
- Setup time at ground level is near-instant — typically just placing 1–2 blocks and positioning.
- The time saved per run is **5–15 seconds** compared to pillar zero cycle, depending on the pillar height and runner's efficiency.

Over many runs, this adds up significantly, particularly for runners competing in the top percentile of MCSR Ranked where even 5-second improvements to splits matter.

### When Ground Zero Is Preferred

- Runner is already near the fountain when the last crystal is destroyed.
- Target pillar for node hover is directly above the fountain area.
- Runner has practiced the angle and can hit it consistently (70%+).
- All other End execution is already optimal (fast crystal clear, good dragon routing knowledge).

|---|---|---|

## The Setup

Ground Zero's setup is physically simpler than a pillar zero cycle — but what it lacks in build complexity it makes up for in positioning precision.

### Location: The Fountain

The setup is performed at the **central fountain structure** — the raised bedrock platform in the middle of the End arena. Specifically:
- The top of the fountain's central bedrock pillar.
- Or the outer ring of the fountain, depending on the specific node position.

The fountain is a natural, pre-existing raised platform that puts you slightly above ground level, which helps with the upward explosion angle.

### Block Placement

1. Place your **obsidian block** on top of the fountain's central bedrock.
2. This elevates you by 1 block — a small but meaningful improvement to your explosion angle.
3. Stand on or immediately adjacent to the obsidian block.
4. Have beds in hotbar slots 1–3.

### Why the Fountain?

The fountain is positioned directly below the dragon's flight path. The node the dragon hovers at is typically one of the taller pillars adjacent to or near the fountain. From the fountain top, the geometry of the explosion arc aligns with the head hitbox when the correct angle is used.

> [!NOTE]
> The exact fountain position that maximizes your angle will vary slightly by which node (pillar) the dragon hovers at. Practice maps let you experiment with exact foot position for each possible node.

## Step-by-Step Execution

### Phase 1: Enter The End

1. Enter through the End Portal.
2. Immediately orient toward the dragon and the fountain.
3. Begin sprinting toward the nearest crystals.

### Phase 2: Crystal Clearing

4. Follow your practiced crystal clearing route.
5. Clear all exposed crystals first (sword hits).
6. Destroy caged crystals with arrows as you pass near them.
7. **Hold the final 1–2 crystals** — do not destroy them until your ground setup is ready.

### Phase 3: Ground Setup

8. Move to the fountain structure.
9. Place your obsidian block on top of the central bedrock.
10. Stand on or beside the obsidian block.
11. Arrange your beds in your hotbar.
12. **Aim your mouse to the pre-practiced vertical angle** (see [Mouse Positioning](#mouse-positioning)).

### Phase 4: Trigger the Dragon

13. Destroy the final crystal(s) to set the dragon onto its node approach path.
14. Confirm the dragon is flying toward the target node above you.

### Phase 5: Execute the Kill

15. As the dragon begins to hover at the node above:
    - **Place a bed** at your position on the obsidian block.
    - **Right-click to detonate immediately.**
    - The explosion fires upward at your aimed angle toward the head.
16. Watch for the damage indicator (hitbox flash, dragon flinch, sound).
17. Place the second bed immediately and detonate.
18. If 2–3 head hits land, the dragon dies.

### Phase 6: Confirm or Recover

19. Dragon death animation plays → Exit portal opens → Run ends.
20. If the dragon survives: fall back to one cycle (see [Failure Recovery](#failure-recovery)).

> [!WARNING]
> The bed explosion at your feet while you're on the fountain is still dangerous to **you**. You will take damage if you are too close to the explosion center. The small elevation of the obsidian block helps — your character is slightly higher than the bed when it explodes.

## Mouse Positioning

This is the defining technical skill of Ground Zero. Unlike one cycle (where you look horizontally at the head) or pillar zero cycle (where you look roughly horizontally from the pillar), Ground Zero requires looking **steeply upward** to angle the explosion into the dragon head above.

### The Problem

In Minecraft, bed explosions emanate from the explosion center at the bed's location and travel outward spherically. For the explosion to reach the head hitbox 40–70 blocks above you, you need to aim the bed placement such that the explosion center is close enough to the head.

This doesn't mean you literally "aim" the explosion direction — beds always explode at their own location. What you are controlling is **where on the fountain surface you place the bed**, which depends on your look direction and position.

### The Required Angle

The exact look angle (pitch value) required varies by:
- Which pillar the dragon is hovering at.
- The exact height of the dragon's head during the hover.
- Your exact foot position on the fountain.

However, a general benchmark used by many runners:
- Look angle of approximately **-60° to -70° from horizontal** (i.e., looking steeply upward).
- Horizontal facing direction aligned with the node pillar's position relative to the fountain.

### How to Find Your Angle

1. Load the **Zero Practice Map** (see [Practice Resources](#practice-resources)).
2. Set up in the Ground Zero configuration.
3. Attempt the kill repeatedly.
4. For each attempt, note the **pitch value** shown in F3 debug info (the `f:` field, combined with your look direction).
5. When you land a successful head hit, record the exact angle.
6. Practice until you can reproduce that angle without looking at F3.

> [!TIP]
> Some runners use a **crosshair mod** or **mini-crosshair** overlay that displays pitch in the corner of the screen. This assists with angle verification in live runs.

## Eye Zoom Macro

A **zoom macro** (commonly called an "eye zoom" or just "zoom key") is a keybind that temporarily narrows your field of view (FOV), magnifying your view — similar to looking through a telescope.

### Why It's Used in Ground Zero

With a narrow FOV:
- The dragon head and its hitbox appear larger on screen.
- Subtle differences in your look angle are amplified.
- It is significantly easier to visually confirm whether your aim is correct.
- The act of clicking a zoom key is a **muscle memory anchor** — it forces you to pause momentarily and confirm your angle before detonating.

### Is It Allowed in MCSR Ranked?

> [!NOTE]
> Zoom mods such as **OptiFine** or **Zoomify** are permitted in MCSR Ranked runs. They provide no unfair advantage as they don't affect game mechanics — only your visual perspective.

### Common Zoom Mods

| Mod | Notes |
|---|---|
| OptiFine | Built-in zoom key (default: `C`). Widely used. |
| Zoomify | Standalone zoom mod, more configurable. |
| Sodium + Zoomify | Common fabric mod combination for performance + zoom. |

### How to Use Zoom in Ground Zero

1. Bind your zoom key to an easy-to-reach key (e.g., `C` or `Z`).
2. During setup, hold the zoom key and look upward toward where the dragon head will be.
3. Visually confirm the angle looks right based on practice.
4. Release zoom, place bed, detonate.
5. Some runners keep zoom held during detonation — both approaches work.

> [!TIP]
> Practice with zoom enabled from the very beginning of your Ground Zero training. Switching between zoom and no-zoom approaches mid-training creates muscle memory confusion.

## Failure Recovery

Ground Zero failures are more common than pillar zero cycle failures due to the difficulty of the angle. Having a clear recovery plan is non-negotiable.

### Scenario 1: Explosion Misses the Head

Most common failure. The explosion goes off but doesn't intersect the head hitbox:
- You'll hear the explosion but no dragon damage sound.
- The dragon's health bar won't decrease significantly.
- **Immediately attempt a second bed** if the dragon is still hovering.
- If the hover ends: fall back to one cycle mode.

### Scenario 2: Dragon Takes Partial Damage but Survives

Your explosion hit but the dragon isn't dead:
- Check if the hover window is still open for one more attempt.
- If yes, detonate immediately.
- If the dragon departs: fall back to one cycle — the dragon is already damaged, so the one cycle will require fewer hits.

### Falling Back to One Cycle

1. Move to the central bedrock fountain pillar.
2. Confirm your obsidian block is already placed (you placed it during setup).
3. Wait for the dragon to complete its flight circuit and perch.
4. Execute one cycle as practiced.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Do NOT waste multiple beds on failed Ground Zero attempts when it's clear you can't hit the angle. Cut losses, fall back to one cycle, and get the kill cleanly.

### Scenario 3: You Used Too Many Beds

If repeated Ground Zero attempts depleted most of your beds:
- Count remaining beds immediately.
- With 3+ beds remaining: one cycle is still very viable.
- With 1–2 beds remaining: a two-bed one cycle is possible but risky. Consider bow shots to the perching head as supplemental damage.

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Attempting Ground Zero without pillar zero cycle mastery
The angle requirement on top of all other zero cycle knowledge makes this a poor starting point. Learn steps in order.

### ❌ Looking at the wrong horizontal direction
Ground Zero requires aiming toward the specific node pillar, not just upward randomly. Your horizontal facing must align with the pillar's position relative to the fountain.

### ❌ Wrong vertical angle
Too shallow = explosion hits the ground near the fountain. Too steep = explosion goes past the head. The angle must be practiced to muscle memory.

### ❌ Not using F3+B
Without the head hitbox visible, you are guessing whether your angle is correct. F3+B is mandatory for learning this technique and highly recommended even in live runs.

### ❌ Detonating before the dragon arrives at the node
The dragon must be at the hover position — not en route to it. Exploding too early means the head isn't in range of the explosion, regardless of your angle.

### ❌ Not having a fallback plan
Players who attempt Ground Zero without a clear one cycle fallback plan tend to panic when it fails, wasting more beds and time.

### ❌ Skipping the zoom macro
Many runners learn Ground Zero without zoom and plateau at 40–50% success rates. Adding a zoom key often immediately improves success rates by making the angle verification far more reliable.

## Tips & Tricks

> [!TIP]
> **Learn your angle number.** In practice, open F3 and note the exact pitch value when you land a successful Ground Zero kill. Write it down. Use this number as your target in future practice sessions.

> [!TIP]
> **Use the fountain edge.** Your exact foot position on the fountain affects the angle slightly. Experiment with standing on different edges of the obsidian block to find what gives you the most consistent hit.

> [!TIP]
> **Pre-aim before the dragon arrives.** Don't wait for the dragon to be at the node to start aiming. Get your angle set during the few seconds the dragon is still en route, so you can place and detonate the moment it arrives.

> [!TIP]
> **Accept that Ground Zero isn't for every run.** Even elite runners don't succeed every time. If you miss on the first attempt and the hover window is closing, immediately switch mentality to one cycle. Don't waste a second bed chasing a failed setup.

> [!TIP]
> **Combine with a fast crystal clear.** Ground Zero's time advantage is maximized when paired with an optimized crystal clearing route. Shaving 5 seconds off your crystal clear and 10 seconds off your End kill adds up significantly over many runs.
