## Introduction

The End is the third and final dimension in Minecraft, accessible through a Stronghold's End Portal. It is a desolate island of End Stone floating in a void, populated by Endermen and dominated by the Ender Dragon — Minecraft's final boss.

In MCSR Ranked, reaching The End means you are in the final stretch of your run. The objective is straightforward but demands execution under pressure:

1. **Destroy all End Crystals** atop the obsidian pillars surrounding the central fountain.
2. **Kill the Ender Dragon.**

The moment the dragon dies, your run timer stops and your final time is recorded. Every second you spend in The End counts directly against your ranking, so efficiency here is critical.

---

## Win Condition in MCSR Ranked

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The run timer stops **the instant the Ender Dragon's death animation completes** and the exit portal opens. This is the official finish condition for MCSR Ranked.

Unlike some other speedrun categories, MCSR Ranked does not require you to interact with the exit portal, collect the dragon egg, or read the End Poem. The dragon simply needs to die.

This means:
- Any extra time you spend fumbling with crystals, missing bed explosions, or getting hit by Endermen adds directly to your final time.
- A run with a fast nether but a slow End can still be competitive — and vice versa.
- Optimizing your End strategy is one of the highest-value improvements a mid-level runner can make.

## What to Bring Into The End

Preparation before entering the End Portal is one of the most impactful decisions of the run. Below is a prioritized checklist:

### Essential Items

| Item | Quantity | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Beds | 4–8 | Primary weapon for killing the dragon |
| Obsidian block | 1–2 | Platform for placing beds at the fountain |
| Food | 8–16 hunger bars worth | Regeneration during the fight |
| Sword (Iron or better) | 1 | Killing Endermen if necessary, self-defense |

### Highly Recommended

| Item | Quantity | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Bow & Arrows | 1 bow + 8–16 arrows | Shooting caged crystals (iron bars) from a distance |
| Ender Pearls | 2–4 | Mobility, escaping Endermen, reaching pillars |
| Water Bucket | 1 | Emergency void fall recovery, Enderman distraction |
| Carved Pumpkin | 1 | Wear on head to prevent Endermen from aggroing |

### Optional / Advanced

| Item | Quantity | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Blocks (any) | 8–16 | Building towers for crystal or zero cycle setups |
| Flint and Steel | 1 | Lighting beds without right-click in some setups |
| Shield | 1 | Blocking dragon breath and Enderman hits |

> [!TIP]
> If you arrive at the End Portal with **fewer than 4 beds**, seriously consider whether to enter at all. You can fight the dragon without beds, but it is dramatically slower and less consistent.

> [!NOTE]
> A **carved pumpkin** is a hidden gem of End preparation. Wearing it as a helmet completely prevents Endermen from aggro-ing you when you look at them, freeing up mental bandwidth during the fight. The vision obstruction is manageable with practice.

## The End Environment

Understanding the physical layout of The End is essential for executing any kill strategy efficiently.

### The Central Island

The main arena is a roughly circular island of End Stone, approximately 100×100 blocks. The center is defined by the **Exit Fountain** (also called the portal structure), a raised bedrock platform that sits directly over the End Portal.

### Obsidian Pillars

Surrounding the fountain are **10 obsidian pillars** of varying heights (ranging from approximately Y=76 to Y=103). Each pillar has an **End Crystal** sitting on top.

- **Tall pillars** have iron bar cages around their crystals — these must be shot with arrows or approached with blocks and a sword.
- **Short pillars** have exposed crystals — these can be hit directly with a sword, fist, or any projectile.

The pillar layout is consistent across all seeds. The two tallest pillars have caged crystals. Their exact positions relative to the fountain are fixed.

### The Fountain / Exit Portal Structure

The central fountain is a multi-layered bedrock structure:
- **Base level** — the outer ring of bedrock at ground level.
- **Middle ring** — a slightly raised platform.
- **Top** — a small central bedrock pillar where a Torch sits before the dragon is killed.

This structure becomes the Exit Portal (the black obsidian portal surrounded by torches) when the dragon dies. During the fight, it is the central staging area for most kill strategies.

### Endermen

The End island spawns many Endermen. They are passive unless provoked (i.e., you look directly at their eyes). During a hectic fight:
- Avoid direct eye contact.
- If one aggros, strafing into a 2-block-high space (like under a pillar) works as they cannot pathfind to you.
- They teleport when hit with water.
- A carved pumpkin prevents all aggro entirely.

## The Dragon's Behavior

The Ender Dragon cycles through a predictable set of behaviors. Understanding these phases is the foundation of every kill strategy.

### Flying Phase

When the dragon is not perching, it flies in a large circular pattern around the central fountain. During this phase:
- It is largely **immune to projectiles** when flying in circles (arrows deflect off).
- It periodically dives toward you at the fountain — this is a charge attack.
- It flies to **nodes** (the tops of obsidian pillars) where it briefly pauses.
- It can breathe a purple dragon breath cloud that deals damage and applies poison.

### Perching Phase

After all crystals have been destroyed, the dragon will eventually descend and **perch** on top of the fountain's central bedrock. During this phase:
- The dragon is stationary and **fully vulnerable**.
- Its head sweeps back and forth along the North and South sides of the fountain.
- This is the moment exploited by the **One Cycle** strategy.
- The perch lasts approximately 35–45 seconds before the dragon takes off again.

### Node Phase (Flying Hover)

During its flying phase, the dragon briefly **hovers at specific pillar nodes** before continuing its circuit. During this pause:
- The dragon is **vulnerable to damage**.
- Its head is in a predictable position.
- This is the moment exploited by **Zero Cycle** and **Ground Zero** strategies.
- The hover is short (roughly 2–4 seconds), making timing critical.

## End Crystals

> [!IMPORTANT]
> You **must destroy all End Crystals** before the dragon can be killed efficiently. While the dragon flies over an active crystal, it is healed rapidly (approximately 1 HP per tick). Any damage you deal will be undone almost instantly.

### Crystal Types

| Type | Appearance | How to Destroy |
|---|---|---|
| **Open Crystal** | Crystal sitting exposed on top of pillar | Hit with sword, fist, arrow, or any projectile |
| **Caged Crystal** | Crystal surrounded by iron bars | Must shoot through bars with arrow, OR pillar up and hit with sword |

### Crystal Destruction Order

There is no strict required order, but experienced runners follow a general priority:

1. **Short pillars first** — fastest to reach and destroy.
2. **Medium pillars** — one clean sword hit each.
3. **Caged crystals last** — require arrows or block-climbing.

> [!TIP]
> You can use a **single arrow** through the iron bars to destroy a caged crystal. Aim slightly downward toward the base of the crystal for the most reliable hit angle. Some runners carry 2 arrows per cage as insurance.

> [!WARNING]
> Destroying a crystal causes an **explosion** at the crystal's location. If you are standing on the pillar when you hit the crystal, the explosion will deal massive damage or kill you. Always step back, use projectiles, or use blocks to create distance.

## Kill Strategy Overview

There are four main strategies used to kill the Ender Dragon in MCSR Ranked. They vary in speed, difficulty, and required setup.

### Zero Cycle ⚡

> **Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time Save vs. One Cycle:** ~30–60 seconds

Zero Cycle kills the dragon **before it ever perches** by exploiting the dragon's first node hover. The runner builds a specific bed setup on or near a pillar, times the explosion to coincide with the dragon's head at the node, and deals enough damage to kill it.

- Requires knowledge of dragon pathing and node timing.
- Crystal-clearing strategy is different from one cycle.
- Mistakes are hard to recover from without falling back to one cycle.
- [→ Full Zero Cycle Guide](end_zero_cycle.md)

---

### One Cycle 🛏️

> **Difficulty:** Beginner–Intermediate | **Time Save vs. Two+ Cycles:** ~20–40 seconds

One Cycle kills the dragon **the first time it perches** using bed explosions aimed at its head. It is the most widely used strategy in MCSR Ranked because it is reliable, repeatable, and easy to practice.

- Best strategy for players new to The End.
- Requires destroying all crystals, then waiting for a perch.
- 4–5 head-hitting bed explosions is enough to kill the dragon.
- [→ Full One Cycle Guide](end_one_cycle.md)

---

### Ground Zero 🎯

> **Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time Save vs. One Cycle:** ~35–65 seconds

Ground Zero is a **variant of Zero Cycle** performed entirely at ground level, near the fountain. Instead of climbing a pillar, the runner positions at the fountain and fires bed explosions upward to hit the dragon's head as it hovers above.

- No time lost climbing a pillar.
- Requires very precise vertical mouse angle.
- Many runners use a zoom macro to assist with aiming.
- [→ Full Ground Zero Guide](end_ground_zero.md)

---

### Oneshot 🏹

> **Difficulty:** Extreme / Situational | **Frequency in Ranked:** Very Rare

The Oneshot uses the physics of arrow velocity combined with the dragon's own tail knockback to fire an arrow at extreme speed that deals enough damage to kill the dragon in a single hit. In MCSR Ranked (RSG), this is almost exclusively a situational bonus rather than a planned strategy.

- Most common in SSG (Set Seed Glitchless) runs with known setups.
- In RSG, only attempt if conditions are perfect.
- [→ Full Oneshot Guide](end_oneshot.md)

## Which Strategy Should You Learn First?

> [!TIP]
> **Start with One Cycle.** It is the right answer for the vast majority of MCSR Ranked players.

Here is a general progression roadmap:

```
New Player
    │
    ▼
Learn One Cycle (Consistent, forgiving, well-documented)
    │
    ▼
Practice until you hit one cycle reliably in real runs (~80%+ success rate)
    │
    ▼
Learn Zero Cycle basics (M85 setup or similar)
    │
    ▼
Add Ground Zero when zero cycle is comfortable
    │
    ▼
Oneshot is situational — learn it eventually, never rely on it
```

Don't skip steps. Many runners try to learn Zero Cycle too early and end up with a **slower average time** due to failed attempts forcing a late one cycle or even a two cycle. A consistent one cycle beats a shaky zero cycle every time.
