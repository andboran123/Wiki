## What Is the One Cycle?

The **One Cycle** is a kill technique where the Ender Dragon is killed the **first time it perches** on top of the fountain, using a series of **bed explosions** aimed precisely at its head.

When the dragon perches, it sits stationary over the central bedrock fountain and sweeps its head back and forth. Beds, when placed and right-clicked in The End, explode in the same way they do in the Nether — and an explosion that hits the dragon's **head hitbox** deals massive damage (approximately 22–25 HP per hit). With 4–5 successful head hits, the dragon's full 200 HP pool is depleted and it dies.

The term "one cycle" refers to killing the dragon on its **first perch** (one perch cycle). If you fail, the dragon flies again and you must wait for a second perch — this is called a "two cycle," and costs you significant time.

---

## Why It's The Standard Technique

One Cycle is the dominant End strategy for MCSR Ranked for several reasons:

- **Reliability** — The dragon always perches eventually after crystals are destroyed. You are not racing a tight timing window.
- **Forgiveness** — If you miss one bed, you have time to correct before the perch ends.
- **Consistency** — Practiced runners can hit a one cycle 90%+ of the time in live runs.
- **Low prerequisite skill** — You don't need pixel-perfect aim or frame-precise timing.
- **Easy to practice** — Practice maps and vanilla survival both allow effective training.
- **Scales well** — Even top-ranked players use one cycle frequently when zero cycle setups don't align.

> [!NOTE]
> Zero cycle is faster in optimal execution, but a failed zero cycle often results in a late one cycle anyway. A consistent one cycle beats an inconsistent zero cycle for your average split time.

## Prerequisites

Before you attempt a one cycle, ensure the following:

### Items Required

| Item | Minimum | Recommended | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Beds | 4 | 6–8 | 4 is the theoretical minimum; bring more for safety |
| Obsidian block | 1 | 2 | Platform base on the fountain pillar |
| Food | 8+ hunger | 16 | You need to heal during the fight |
| Sword | Iron or better | Diamond | For breaking misplaced beds and killing Endermen |

### Optional but Highly Recommended

| Item | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Bow + arrows | Destroying caged crystals from range |
| Ender pearls | Mobility; escape Endermen; reach pillar tops |
| Water bucket | Emergency Enderman counter; void prevention |
| Carved pumpkin | Prevents Endermen from aggroing you |

### Game Settings

- **F3+B** — Enable hitbox display before entering. The dragon head hitbox is invisible to the naked eye; F3+B makes it visible as a yellow wireframe box.
- **Render Distance** — Set high enough that you can see the dragon clearly.
- **FOV** — Normal or Quake Pro; both work, but practice with whatever you use in runs.

> [!WARNING]
> Do NOT enter the End without destroying all crystals having a clear plan. Attempting to one-cycle while crystals are still active is essentially impossible — the dragon heals faster than you can damage it.

## Understanding the Perch

The **perch** is the dragon's stationary phase where it lands on the fountain's central bedrock structure. This is your kill window.

### What Triggers the Perch

The dragon does not perch at a fixed time. It perches after:
1. **All End Crystals have been destroyed.**
2. The dragon has completed a certain number of flying passes (approximately 1–4 passes after the last crystal is destroyed, though this varies).

The perch is guaranteed — the dragon will always perch eventually after crystals are gone. This is what makes one cycle so reliable: you simply wait.

### What the Perch Looks Like

When the dragon begins a perch:
- It will spiral inward toward the fountain.
- Its wings begin to **fold** as it descends.
- It lands on the central raised bedrock with its body straddling the fountain.
- Its head sweeps **left and right** (roughly North and South of the fountain).
- The dragon emits a purple fog/dragon breath cloud around the fountain during the perch.

### Head Movement During Perch

The dragon's head sweeps rhythmically:
- It moves from one side (e.g., North) to the other (South) in approximately **3–5 seconds**.
- Then reverses and goes back.
- The head travels in a low arc — it passes **across the top of the fountain bedrock**, which is where your obsidian block and beds are placed.

> [!TIP]
> The head hitbox is **larger than it looks**. Don't panic if the head seems far — if F3+B shows the hitbox overlapping your bed, that's a valid hit.

## Forcing the Perch

While the perch triggers on its own, you can influence the dragon's behavior and get to your setup position faster:

### Positioning

- **Stand near the fountain** (within 10 blocks of the center) after destroying the last crystal.
- The dragon tends to be attracted to the player's position. Staying near the fountain encourages it to cycle back sooner.
- Avoid running around the island; stay central.

### Staying Safe While Waiting

While waiting for the perch:
- Watch for the dragon's **charge attack** — it will dive toward you occasionally. Jump to the side or sprint away to dodge.
- Watch for **dragon breath clouds** on the ground. Avoid walking through them (purple/magenta cloud).
- Keep your hunger bar full so passive regeneration keeps your health up.

### Estimating Time to Perch

After the last crystal is destroyed, typical timing is:
- **Best case:** The dragon perches on its very next pass (~15–30 seconds).
- **Average:** 1–3 flying passes after crystals cleared (~30–90 seconds).
- **Worst case:** 3–5 passes (~90–150 seconds). Rare, but happens.

## Step-by-Step Execution

Follow these steps in order during the one cycle:

### Phase 1: Crystal Clearing

Before approaching the fountain for the kill:

1. Destroy all **short/exposed crystals** first — sprint to each one and hit it once with your sword.
2. Use arrows to destroy **caged crystals** from ground level. Aim slightly downward through the iron bars at the base of the crystal.
3. Keep track of which crystals are gone — you can count the explosions (10 total crystals).
4. Keep your health up during crystal clearing — explosions hurt at close range.

### Phase 2: Setup at the Fountain

Once all crystals are destroyed or you are ready to wait:

1. **Navigate to the fountain's central pillar** — the small central raised bedrock in the middle of the fountain structure.
2. **Place your obsidian block** on top of the central bedrock torch position. This creates a raised platform slightly above the fountain level.
3. Have your **beds hot-barred** (in easy-to-reach inventory slots, e.g., slots 1–6).
4. Enable **F3+B** if you haven't already.
5. Wait for the perch.

### Phase 3: The Perch Begins

As the dragon begins to descend:

1. **Watch the dragon's head position**. Identify whether it is approaching from the **North or South** side of the fountain.
2. **Position yourself on the same side** as the head will be sweeping toward first.
3. Stay on your obsidian platform or immediately adjacent to it.

### Phase 4: Bed Explosion Sequence

This is the core of the one cycle:

**Step a.** Place a bed on your obsidian block (or on the raised bedrock nearby).

**Step b.** The bed should be placed **where the head hitbox will be or is currently passing**.

**Step c.** Right-click the bed **immediately after placing it** to detonate it. Do NOT sleep — immediately right-click to cause the explosion.

**Step d.** If the explosion connects with the head: you'll see the damage indicator (the dragon takes visible damage) and hear a hit sound. The dragon also flinches.

**Step e.** Place the next bed immediately. Don't wait.

**Step f.** Repeat until the dragon is dead. 4–5 successful head hits are typically lethal.

**Step g.** If the head sweeps to the other side, reposition quickly and continue.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Don't stop placing beds.** The biggest mistake beginners make is hesitating after a hit. Keep cycling through beds as fast as you can place and right-click them. Momentum matters.

> [!WARNING]
> The bed explosion **will damage you**. You need at least 3–4 blocks of distance from the explosion center to survive reliably. Your obsidian block height plus standing position usually provides this. If you're taking too much damage, you are too close.

## Timing — When to Detonate

Getting the explosion timing right is the difference between a head hit (22+ damage) and a body hit (5 damage) or a miss.

### The Ideal Moment

- The bed should be placed and detonated **as the head hitbox passes directly over or adjacent to your placement position**.
- You are aiming for the **transition point** — when the head is closest to the fountain center on its sweep.
- A slight **early detonation is better than late**. If you explode early, the fireball travels into the incoming head. If you explode late, the head has already passed.

### Visual Cues

With F3+B on:
- The head hitbox is a yellow wireframe box.
- When that box is **over or adjacent to your bed placement spot**, right-click.
- The hitbox is roughly 2–3 blocks wide and tall — don't wait for a perfectly centered overlap.

### Audio Cues

- Listen for the dragon's movement sounds.
- You can hear the swooping sound as the head approaches.
- Experienced runners use audio to predict the head position even before they see the hitbox clearly.

> [!TIP]
> Practice the timing in Dibedy's practice map (see [Practice Resources](#practice-resources)) where you can repeat the perch phase endlessly without doing a full run.

## Damage Numbers

Understanding damage values helps you know exactly how many hits you need:

| Hit Location | Bed Explosion Damage | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Dragon Head** | ~22–25 HP | Primary target. The only efficient damage source. |
| **Dragon Body/Wings** | ~4–6 HP | Negligible. Avoid wasting beds on body shots. |
| **Near Miss** | 0 HP | Explosion must overlap the head hitbox. |

### Dragon HP and Kill Math

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| Dragon Total HP | **200 HP** |
| HP per head hit | ~22–25 HP |
| Minimum hits to kill | **~4** (at 25 per hit) |
| Practical average hits | **4–6** (some body hits and partial head hits) |

> [!NOTE]
> The dragon's health is split across a "body" health pool and a "head" health pool in the game code, but for practical purposes, all damage to the head counts toward killing it. Damage to the body is not shared with the head HP in the same efficient way.

## Using F3+B Hitboxes

**F3+B** is a Minecraft debug shortcut that toggles visible hitbox displays on all entities.

### How to Enable

- Press `F3` and `B` simultaneously while in-game (not in a menu).
- All entities will now display colored wireframe boxes representing their hitboxes.
- Press again to toggle off.

### What You See With F3+B

- The Ender Dragon appears with a **large red/yellow hitbox** for its body.
- The **head hitbox** appears as a distinct, smaller box attached to the dragon's neck — this is your target.
- The head hitbox is typically **visible even through blocks**, which is extremely useful when the head passes near your fountain setup.

### Practical Use During One Cycle

- Keep F3+B enabled from the moment you enter The End.
- During the perch, watch the head hitbox (yellow box) and place/detonate beds when it overlaps your position.
- The hitbox makes it obvious when you've scored a hit vs. a miss — you'll see the box intersect the explosion radius.

> [!TIP]
> Some runners find the hitbox display visually overwhelming. If that's you, practice with it on until you are comfortable, then experiment with turning it off. Most top players still use F3+B.

## Recovery — If You Miss the One Cycle

Not every attempt will succeed. Here's what to do when things go wrong:

### The Dragon Flies Again (Two Cycle Situation)

If you run out of beds or miss too many hits before the dragon takes off:

1. **Stay calm.** A two cycle costs ~30–60 seconds, not the run.
2. **Keep your remaining beds.** You need them for the second perch.
3. **Stay near the fountain** to encourage a fast second perch.
4. **Don't fight Endermen** — conserve resources and health.
5. When the dragon perches again, restart the bed sequence.

### If You Run Out of Beds Completely

This is the most dangerous failure state:

- You cannot efficiently damage the dragon with a sword or bow during its flying phase.
- Your only option is to wait for another perch and hope the dragon's body is in reach of any remaining explosives.
- This is why bringing **6–8 beds** instead of the theoretical minimum 4 is strongly recommended.

### The Dragon Charges You

During both the wait phase and the perch sequence, the dragon may dive at you:

- Sprint **perpendicular** to the dragon's path to dodge.
- If you're on the fountain, jump to a lower level briefly.
- Do not sprint away from the fountain — you'll just end up far from your setup.

## Enderman Awareness

Endermen are a persistent hazard throughout the End fight. Mismanagement leads to getting pulled into extended melee combat and losing precious time.

### Passive Prevention

- **Wear a carved pumpkin** if you brought one. This completely prevents Endermen from aggroing when you look at them. The vision obstruction is worth the trade-off in most situations.
- **Avoid looking at their eye level** — look at the ground, sky, or dragon while moving around the island.

### If an Enderman Aggros

- **Don't panic sprint** — this moves you away from your setup.
- **Use a 2-block high gap** — Endermen cannot fit under a 2-block ceiling. The edge of the pillar bases or a makeshift 2-block ceiling works.
- **Water bucket** — Endermen teleport away when hit with water, instantly deaggroing.
- **Kill it** — If you have a sword and can afford the time, 2 hits with an iron sword kills an Enderman. Only do this if you can't disengage.

### During the Perch Sequence

- If an Enderman aggros during the bed sequence: **prioritize the dragon**. A few hits from an Enderman won't kill you quickly. Land your beds first, then deal with the Enderman.
- Try to end the fight fast enough that Endermen are never a real problem.

## Common Mistakes

Here are the most frequent errors made by beginners attempting one cycle:

### ❌ Not destroying all crystals first
The dragon heals too fast to damage effectively while any crystal is active. Always confirm all 10 are gone before starting the kill sequence.

### ❌ Placing the obsidian block wrong
The obsidian goes on top of the **central bedrock pillar** of the fountain — the small raised center piece. Placing it on the outer bedrock rings gives you an awkward angle.

### ❌ Exploding too late
Waiting until the dragon's head is directly over the bed means the hitbox is already moving away. Explode **slightly early** — as the head is approaching.

### ❌ Standing too close to the bed
The explosion will kill you if you're right next to it. Keep 3–4 blocks of clearance. Your standing-on-obsidian elevation helps, but don't lean over the bed.

### ❌ Panicking and repositioning mid-sequence
Once you start placing beds, commit to the side you're on unless the head clearly is not coming. Switching sides mid-sequence wastes beds and time.

### ❌ Hitting the body
Bed explosions that hit the body deal ~5 damage — you'd need 40 body hits to kill the dragon. Each bed you waste on the body instead of the head is a huge inefficiency. Watch the hitbox and target the head.

### ❌ Not having enough beds
Coming to the End with only 4 beds is risky. Bring 6–8 for a comfortable margin.

### ❌ Ignoring F3+B
Many beginners try to eyeball the head position. This is much harder than using the hitbox display. Always enable F3+B.
