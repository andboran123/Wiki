## What Is Zero Cycle?

**Zero Cycle** kills the Ender Dragon **before it ever perches** — specifically during its **first node hover** phase, when the dragon briefly pauses at an obsidian pillar after a predictable flight path following crystal destruction.

The term "zero" refers to killing the dragon on **zero perches** (it never sits on the fountain). Instead of waiting for the dragon to land, you intercept it at a specific pillar where it hovers briefly and blast its head with bed explosions from a pre-built setup.

This is the primary technique used by high-level MCSR Ranked players who aim for sub-9 or sub-10 minute End splits. Executed correctly, it is **30–60 seconds faster** than a one cycle.

---

## Why It's Advanced

Zero Cycle is mechanically demanding for several interconnected reasons:

- **Narrow timing window** — The dragon's node hover lasts only approximately 2–4 seconds. You must have your bed placed and detonated within this window.
- **Crystal-clearing path must be optimized** — You cannot clear crystals in any order; you need to clear specific ones to set up the dragon's path while reaching the pillar fast enough.
- **Build accuracy under pressure** — You must construct your setup on or near the pillar quickly, often while the dragon is flying overhead.
- **Setup consistency** — Different seeds can slightly alter your approach due to RNG, spawn position, and available resources.
- **High cost of failure** — A missed zero cycle often means falling back to a late one cycle, costing more total time than if you had just done a one cycle from the start.

> [!WARNING]
> If you cannot one cycle at a 75%+ success rate in practice, zero cycle will likely **hurt** your average ranked time. Build the foundation first.

## Key Concept — Dragon Nodes

Understanding dragon nodes is essential to zero cycle. Nodes are the **obsidian pillars that the dragon visits during its flying phase**.

### What Is a Node?

When the dragon is flying, it doesn't fly randomly. It follows a scripted circuit, periodically diverting to **hover at the top of specific obsidian pillars** for a few seconds before continuing. This hovering behavior is called visiting a **node**.

### Why Nodes Matter

During the node hover:
- The dragon is **stationary** (or nearly so) relative to the pillar.
- Its head descends to approximately the level of the crystal (or slightly above).
- **The dragon is vulnerable** — unlike during its regular flight circle where arrows bounce off.
- This is the kill window for zero cycle.

### The First Node

After all crystals are destroyed, the dragon enters a **predictable node visitation sequence**. The first node it visits is consistent across seeds and is almost always one of the taller obsidian pillars.

> [!NOTE]
> The exact identity of the "zero cycle pillar" (first node) can vary slightly based on where the dragon is in its circuit when the last crystal is destroyed. In practice, runners learn to identify the pillar the dragon is heading to and position accordingly.

### Node Flight Path Prediction

After all End Crystals are destroyed:
1. The dragon will typically **spiral or arc** toward one of the tall pillars.
2. It approaches at a slight downward angle.
3. It **hovers** at the pillar's crystal level for ~2–4 seconds.
4. It then resumes its circular flight path.

You need your bed setup ready **before** the dragon arrives at the node.

## The Setup

The setup is the physical construction you build at or near the zero cycle pillar to enable the kill.

### Where to Build

There are two primary setup locations:

| Location | Description | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| **On top of the pillar** | Build a platform at pillar top level; place bed directly adjacent to where head will be | Moderate — requires climbing |
| **Ground level** | Position near pillar base and aim explosions upward | Hard — requires precise angle (see Ground Zero) |

For standard pillar-based zero cycle, you build **on or near the top of the target pillar**.

### Core Setup Components

1. **Climb to pillar top** (or near it) using blocks or ender pearls.
2. **Place a block** next to or slightly below the crystal's position to serve as your bed platform.
3. **Have beds ready** in hotbar slots 1–3.
4. **Know the exact position** where the dragon's head will be during the hover.

### Why Obsidian or Blast-Resistant Blocks?

The obsidian pillar itself provides natural blast resistance. Your setup blocks don't need to be obsidian — any solid block works for the platform. The key is placement position relative to the head hover point.

## Tower Setups — M85 and Variants

Several specific "setups" have been developed and named by the speedrunning community. These are exact block configurations tested for consistency and damage output.

### M85 Setup (Most Common Beginner Zero Cycle)

The **M85 setup** is the most widely taught zero cycle configuration for beginners entering the technique.

**Named after:** Community runner/researcher who refined it.

**How it works:**
- Built on or slightly offset from the top of the zero cycle pillar.
- Uses a specific placement of blocks to position the bed at the exact height where the dragon's head hovers.
- Aims to hit the head hitbox **as the dragon arrives** at the node, rather than during the middle of the hover.

**Why M85 first:**
- It has a relatively forgiving timing window (hitting on arrival vs. peak hover).
- The bed placement location is consistent across different pillar heights.
- It requires fewer blocks to construct than some alternatives.

### Building the M85 Bed Tower

> [!NOTE]
> Exact block counts vary by pillar. The following is a generalized guide — refer to video tutorials or the Zero Practice Map for pillar-specific instructions.

```
Step 1: Climb the target pillar (blocks or ender pearl)
Step 2: Stand on the pillar top (or slightly below crystal level)
Step 3: Place 1 block outward from the pillar edge (toward where head approaches)
Step 4: This block is your bed platform
Step 5: Have beds in hotbar, ready to place-and-click
```

### Other Named Setups

| Setup Name | Description | Relative Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| M85 | Standard pillar-top placement | ⭐⭐ Beginner |
| Ground Zero | Ground-level aim-up variant | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Advanced |
| Staircase Zero | Build ascending staircase to head height | ⭐⭐⭐ Intermediate |
| Offset Zero | Placed offset from pillar using angled blocks | ⭐⭐⭐ Intermediate |

> [!TIP]
> Start with M85 exclusively. Only experiment with other setups once you can successfully complete M85 in the Zero Practice Map at a consistent rate.

## Crystal Clearing Strategy

In zero cycle, crystal clearing is fundamentally different from one cycle. You are not just destroying all crystals — you are **controlling the dragon's path** while simultaneously reaching your setup position.

### The Core Principle

The dragon visits nodes **near crystals that are still active** during its flight. If you clear crystals strategically, you can influence which pillar the dragon flies to next.

### Zero Cycle Crystal Order

The general approach:

1. **Clear most crystals first** (all except the target zero cycle pillar's crystal and possibly one or two adjacent ones).
2. **Clear the zero cycle pillar crystal** only when you are ready — this triggers the dragon to begin its final node visitation pattern toward your target pillar.
3. **Rush to your setup** on or near the target pillar while the dragon is still in flight.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Do NOT clear all crystals and then walk to the pillar. You'll arrive too late. The timing must be: **build setup → clear last crystal(s) → dragon arrives → execute kill.**

### Which Crystal Is the Zero Cycle Crystal?

The zero cycle pillar is typically one of the **tall pillars** (caged crystals). The specific pillar targeted depends on the setup being used and where the dragon is in its circuit.

Learning this comes from:
- Watching tutorial videos that show the specific pillar.
- Using the Zero Practice Map where the target pillar is indicated.
- Studying the dragon's movement after each crystal is cleared.

### Crystals That Can Be Skipped

If you are confident in your zero cycle attempt, you do **not** need to clear the two crystals on the pillars adjacent to the zero cycle pillar — the dragon won't heal from them fast enough to matter in the brief kill window.

However, for beginners: **clear all crystals first** and accept the slightly slower execution until your timing is more practiced.

## Step-by-Step Execution

### Phase 1: Enter The End

1. Activate the End Portal and enter.
2. Quickly orient yourself on the obsidian platform. Note the fountain location.
3. Identify the zero cycle pillar (pre-learn this from practice).

### Phase 2: Crystal Clearing

4. Sprint to the nearest exposed crystals and destroy them with your sword.
5. Use arrows for caged crystals you pass near.
6. Follow your practiced crystal-clearing route.
7. Stop clearing before you destroy the last 1–2 crystals — save them as a timing mechanism.

### Phase 3: Build Your Setup

8. Navigate to the target pillar.
9. Climb to setup height using blocks or ender pearl.
10. Construct your bed platform (M85 or chosen setup configuration).
11. Confirm your hotbar has beds in accessible slots.

### Phase 4: Trigger the Dragon

12. Destroy the final crystal(s) to release the dragon toward your target pillar.
13. Watch the dragon's flight path confirm it is heading to your pillar.

### Phase 5: Execute the Kill

14. As the dragon approaches the node:
    - Place a bed on your platform.
    - Right-click to detonate immediately.
    - The explosion should connect with the head as it arrives.
15. Place a second bed immediately.
16. Right-click to detonate again if needed.
17. 2–3 successful head hits are typically enough at zero cycle (the dragon has full HP but head hits here are still ~22–25 damage each).

> [!TIP]
> Unlike one cycle, in zero cycle you often want to **pre-place the bed before the dragon arrives** and right-click the moment the head is in range, rather than place-and-click simultaneously. The shorter timing window rewards preparation.

### Phase 6: Confirm Kill or Recover

18. If the dragon dies: the death animation begins, then the exit portal opens. Run finishes.
19. If the dragon survives: see [Failure Recovery](#failure-recovery).

## Timing Windows

The timing of zero cycle is significantly tighter than one cycle. Here is a breakdown:

### Node Arrival Window

| Phase | Duration | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Dragon approaching pillar | ~2–5 seconds | Finalize setup, pre-place bed |
| Dragon hovering at node | **~2–4 seconds** | **KILL WINDOW — detonate beds** |
| Dragon departing node | ~1–2 seconds | Last possible detonation (risky) |

### Timing Benchmarks

- **Ideal detonation:** When the head hitbox is **at or just arriving** at the node hover position.
- **Early detonation (0.5s before arrival):** Can still work — the explosion can catch the leading edge of the hitbox.
- **Late detonation (after hover starts departing):** Usually misses or scores a body hit.

### How to Practice Timing

- Use the Zero Practice Map (see [Practice Resources](#practice-resources)).
- Repeatedly attempt the node hover until you develop an intuitive sense of the window.
- Watch your own recordings at 0.5x speed to analyze where your detonations land relative to the hitbox.

## Ground Zero Connection

**Ground Zero** is a variant of Zero Cycle where the setup is performed at **ground level** near the fountain, rather than on top of a pillar.

Key differences:
- No pillar climbing required → faster setup.
- Uses the fountain structure as the base.
- Requires aiming bed explosions **upward** at the dragon's head as it hovers above.
- Demands extremely precise vertical mouse angle.
- Considered harder than pillar-based zero cycle despite being conceptually similar.

> [!NOTE]
> Ground Zero is documented fully in the [Ground Zero strategy page](end_ground_zero.md). Master standard zero cycle first before attempting Ground Zero.

## Failure Recovery

Zero cycle failure is common even for experienced runners. Having a clear recovery plan is essential.

### Scenario 1: The Dragon Survives the Explosion

If your bed detonation hits but doesn't kill the dragon:
1. Attempt a second bed if you have time in the hover window.
2. If the dragon departs the node, **immediately fall back to one cycle mode**:
   - Return to the fountain area.
   - Set up your obsidian block on the central bedrock.
   - Wait for the perch.
   - Execute one cycle as normal.

### Scenario 2: You Missed Completely

If the explosion missed the head hitbox entirely:
- Don't panic. Fall back to one cycle.
- You still have most of your beds.
- The time cost of a failed zero cycle into a one cycle is roughly equal to just doing a one cycle — you haven't catastrophically lost the run.

### Scenario 3: You Fell Off the Pillar

- Use an ender pearl to get back up, or
- Use remaining blocks to pillar back up for another attempt, or
- Fall back to one cycle.

### Scenario 4: You Run Out of Beds

This is the worst-case. If you used all beds in failed zero cycle attempts:
- Sword damage to the perching dragon is slow (each hit deals ~2–3 damage to the head).
- Bow shots during the perch deal ~7–10 damage per hit to the head.
- The run is in serious trouble. Focus on not dying and find a way to deal damage with what's left.

> [!TIP]
> This is why bringing **6–8 beds** is strongly recommended even for zero cycle attempts. A failed zero cycle with 3+ beds remaining can comfortably fall back to one cycle.

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Attempting zero cycle before mastering one cycle
A shaky zero cycle that fails 50% of the time and converts to a late one cycle will be slower on average than a clean one cycle. Build the foundation.

### ❌ Clearing all crystals before approaching the pillar
This gives you no timing control. The dragon will start its node visits immediately, and you'll arrive at the pillar too late to set up.

### ❌ Building the setup at the wrong height
The bed must be at the right level relative to the dragon's head hover position. Too high or too low means misses even with correct timing.

### ❌ Detonating too early (before the dragon arrives at the node)
The explosion radius won't reach the head if it hasn't arrived yet. Learn to wait until the leading edge of the approach.

### ❌ Detonating too late (dragon already leaving)
If the head has started moving away from the hover position, your explosion is hitting the departing underside of the body, not the head. This is a body hit (5 damage) not a head hit (25 damage).

### ❌ Standing too close to the explosion
Just like one cycle, be at least 3–4 blocks away from the bed when it detonates. Pillar-top setups are especially dangerous if your platform is small.

### ❌ Not having a recovery plan
Attempting zero cycle without knowing how to fall back to one cycle means that when you fail (and you will, early on), you waste additional seconds figuring out what to do.

## Tips & Tricks

> [!TIP]
> **Record your attempts.** Watching back failed zero cycles at slow speed teaches you more in 5 minutes than 30 minutes of additional practice without review.

> [!TIP]
> **Use ender pearls to climb pillars.** Pearling to the top of a pillar is significantly faster than block-pillaring. Even one ender pearl saved from the nether for End use can be the difference in reaching your setup in time.

> [!TIP]
> **Pre-place the bed.** On the M85 setup, experienced runners place the bed on the platform before the dragon arrives, then just right-click the moment the head is in range. This eliminates one step from the narrow timing window.

> [!TIP]
> **Learn the dragon's sound cues.** The dragon makes distinct audio cues as it approaches a node. Experienced runners begin placing beds based on audio before they even see the head.

> [!TIP]
> **Watch your horizontal angle.** On pillar-top setups, your facing direction matters. Make sure you are positioned and facing toward where the head will be — not toward the fountain or away from the approach path.

> [!TIP]
> **Start with a 'relaxed' zero cycle.** In early practice, accept that you might use 2–3 beds to secure the kill rather than 1–2. As your timing improves, the bed count will drop naturally.
