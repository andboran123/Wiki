## Introduction

Desert Temple seeds are one of the most recognizable and commonly encountered overworld types in MCSR Ranked. In this seed category, your guaranteed structure spawn is a **Desert Temple** (also called a Desert Pyramid), a large sandstone structure found exclusively in desert biomes. Rather than crafting gear through mining or trading with villagers, your primary goal is to **loot the temple's underground chest chamber** and then use those resources to build a Nether portal as efficiently as possible.

### Elo Distribution

Desert Temple seeds are distributed across the MCSR Ranked Elo ladder as follows:

| Elo Range | Desert Temple Seed Probability |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| 0 - 599   | ~30%                          |
| 600 - 1199 | ~25%                         |
| 1200+     | ~20%                          |

This means Desert Temple seeds are **most common at lower Elo brackets** and become somewhat less frequent as you climb the ladder. At higher Elo, other seed types (such as shipwreck or village) become more prevalent. Regardless of bracket, you should be comfortable executing this strategy efficiently, because it will show up frequently at every skill level.

### What Makes Desert Temple Seeds Different

Unlike village seeds (which give you access to beds, food, and trades) or shipwreck seeds (which provide buried treasure maps and reliable iron), Desert Temple seeds offer:

- A **one-time loot jackpot** from four underground chests
- **No reliable food** nearby — the desert itself is barren
- **No wood** in the immediate area — deserts have no trees
- **High variance** loot — you might get diamonds and enchanted items, or you might get very little
- A **genuine death trap** in the form of TNT that can instantly kill you if mishandled

The Desert Temple strategy demands careful sequencing: you must collect wood **before** entering the desert, survive the TNT chamber, prioritize gold for Piglin trading, and find food before you starve. When executed well, this route can be extremely fast. When executed poorly, it results in death, wasted time, or a portal that cannot be lit.

## MCSR Ranked Seed Guarantees

MCSR Ranked uses a **filtered seed pool** to ensure fair and speedrun-viable conditions. When you receive a Desert Temple seed, the following guarantees apply:

- A **Desert Temple spawns within a reasonable distance** of your spawn point (typically within ~200-400 blocks, though variance exists).
- The desert temple is **fully intact** — the structure generates completely and is not cut off by chunk borders or terrain issues.
- The **underground chest chamber is accessible** — all four chests are present and intact.
- The **TNT trap is live** — the pressure plate and TNT beneath the chamber are fully generated. (This is standard Minecraft generation; MCSR Ranked does not disable the trap.)
- There will be **at least some loot** in the chests — empty chests do not occur due to the game's loot table system.

> **Note:** MCSR Ranked does **not** guarantee specific loot quality, the presence of diamonds, or a nearby lava pool. You must adapt your routing after seeing what the temple chests contain.

## Desert Temple Anatomy

Understanding the structure of a Desert Temple is essential before attempting to loot it under time pressure.

### Exterior

- The temple is a large **sandstone pyramid-like structure**, approximately 21x21 blocks at its base, standing about 14 blocks tall.
- It has a **distinctive orange and blue terracotta pattern** on its exterior, making it easy to spot from a distance across the flat desert terrain.
- The entrance is on the **north side** — a large sandstone doorway at ground level leading into the main hall.
- Inside the main hall you will find a **central blue terracotta block** in the floor — this is the critical landmark for looting.

### Interior Main Hall

- The main hall is largely hollow, with **decorative sandstone pillars** and a flat floor.
- The **center of the floor** has a distinctive checkerboard pattern of **orange and blue terracotta**, with a single **blue wool block** directly in the middle. This block is the trap trigger — it conceals a pressure plate below.
- Stairways on the sides of the interior lead upward to the upper floors (which contain no loot relevant to speedrunning).

### Underground Chest Chamber

Directly beneath the blue wool block, approximately **8-9 blocks underground**, lies the real prize:

- A **square chamber** accessed by a vertical shaft through the center of the structure.
- The chamber contains **four chests**, one in each corner of the room.
- The **loot table** for each chest can include: gold ingots, iron ingots, diamonds, emeralds, rotten flesh, bones, spider eyes, horse armor (iron/gold/diamond), enchanted books, saddles, and sand.
- In the center of the chamber floor sits a **pressure plate** connected to **TNT**.

### The TNT Trap

Beneath the pressure plate in the chamber floor:

- **9 blocks of TNT** are embedded in a cross pattern under the stone floor.
- Stepping on the **pressure plate** in the chamber instantly triggers all 9 TNT blocks simultaneously.
- The resulting explosion is **lethal at close range** — it will kill an unarmored player with no chance to escape, and will **destroy all four chests and their contents**.
- The trap also destroys the stone floor and parts of the chamber walls.

---

## Critical Warning: The TNT Trap

> **CAUTION: Do NOT step on the pressure plate in the underground chamber.** This is the single most important rule of Desert Temple looting. The TNT trap has killed countless speedrunners — both beginners and experienced players who lost focus for a split second. The pressure plate is a small stone object in the very center of the chamber floor. Do not walk over the center of the room at floor level without first neutralizing the trap.

There are two safe methods for dealing with the trap:

1. **Water Bucket Method** — Pour water into the chamber before descending. Water disables TNT detonation and prevents the pressure plate from triggering (the plate becomes inaccessible under water). This is the **safest** method.
2. **Controlled Detonation Method** — Intentionally detonate the TNT from a safe position (outside the chamber, triggering it from above), then collect loot from the blast radius. This is used by advanced runners to save time but risks destroying loot.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

### Phase 1 — Getting Wood Before the Desert

> **IMPORTANT: You must collect wood before entering the desert.** Deserts have no trees. Without wood, you cannot craft a crafting table, pickaxe, or any of the tools needed to build a Nether portal. Running directly to the desert temple without wood is one of the most common beginner mistakes on this seed type.

**At spawn, look around immediately:**

1. Identify your biome at spawn. If you spawn **in or near a forest, plains, or jungle**, collect wood from those trees first.
2. You need a minimum of **8-12 logs** to have enough wood for tools, torches, and a crafting table. Collect more if you have time.
3. Craft a **wooden pickaxe** as soon as you have 3 planks + 2 sticks, so you can mine stone while traveling.
4. While gathering wood, punch or mine **any gravel** you pass for flint — you will need flint for a flint and steel later.
5. Craft a **stone pickaxe** as soon as you find stone (essential for mining in the Nether).

**If you spawn directly in the desert with no trees visible:**

- Look for the **edge of the desert biome** — trees from adjacent forest or plains biomes are often just a short run away.
- Check for any nearby biome transitions by looking at the horizon for green terrain.
- In rare cases, sprint to the desert temple first to loot gold quickly, then find a biome border for wood. However, this is risky and is not recommended for beginners.

---

### Phase 2 — Reaching the Desert Temple

1. **Navigate to the desert temple.** On MCSR Ranked seeds, the temple is usually visible on the horizon or discoverable within 1-2 minutes of travel from spawn.
2. **Approach from any side.** The temple is equally accessible from all directions — the main entrance is decorative and not the fastest access route for speedrunning.
3. **Scout the temple exterior briefly.** Note the terrain around the temple — is there a lava pool nearby? A river? These will matter for portal building later.
4. **Do not enter through the main door and walk to the center** — inexperienced players often walk straight in and trigger the pressure plate.

---

### Phase 3 — Looting the Desert Temple (TNT Method)

#### Method A: Water Bucket Method (Recommended for Beginners)

1. **Stand at the center of the temple floor** (near the blue wool block) from the side, not on top of it.
2. **Break the blue wool block** using any tool (it breaks instantly by hand).
3. Look down — you will see the pressure plate below, and a shaft descending to the chamber.
4. **Pour your water bucket** into the shaft. Water will flow down and fill the chamber, disabling the pressure plate.
5. **Drop down the shaft** into the now-water-filled chamber. You will not trigger the TNT.
6. **Loot all four chests** by swimming to each corner. Water does not prevent chest access.
7. After looting, pillar up out of the chamber using blocks or swim up the water column.

#### Method B: Controlled Detonation (Faster, Higher Risk)

1. **Stand at the center of the temple floor** from the side.
2. **Break the blue wool block.**
3. Look down the shaft to locate the pressure plate or the TNT below.
4. **Do NOT drop down.** Instead, trigger the detonation:
   - **Drop a gravel or sand block** onto the pressure plate (the falling block triggers the plate).
   - **Use Flint and Steel** on the TNT from the side if you have broken through the side wall.
5. **Back away immediately** — the TNT detonation has a fuse of roughly 4 seconds.
6. The explosion will blow apart the chamber. **Loot the scattered items** from the blast — chests are destroyed, but items are ejected as drops.
7. Items may scatter over a wide area; sweep the blast zone quickly.

#### Method C: Direct Navigation — No Water, No Detonation (Most Common)

1. Break the blue wool block.
2. Drop carefully to one side of the shaft, landing near a wall.
3. **Move only along the walls and corners** to access each chest.
4. **Never step into the center** of the chamber floor where the pressure plate sits.
5. After looting all four chests, pillar up with blocks to escape.

> **Tip:** The most reliable beginner method is Method C — break the blue wool, drop carefully to one side, and loot all four corner chests while hugging the walls. The pressure plate is only in the very center. Good spatial awareness is all that is required.

### Phase 4 — Loot Priorities

Desert Temple chests have a randomized loot table. Here is what to prioritize and why:

| Item | Priority | Reason |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Gold Ingots** | Highest | Used for Piglin barter in the Nether to get Ender Pearls |
| **Diamonds** | Highest | Rare and powerful — can be used for pickaxe or armor |
| **Iron Ingots** | High | Crafting tools, bucket, flint and steel, armor |
| **Emeralds** | Medium | Not directly useful in speedruns — low priority |
| **Horse Armor (Gold/Diamond)** | Low | Not useful for running — skip if inventory is tight |
| **Enchanted Books** | Medium | Situationally useful — Fire Protection or Feather Falling are valuable |
| **Saddle** | Low | Rarely speeds up the run at this stage |
| **Rotten Flesh** | Low | Edible but causes hunger — take only as emergency food |
| **Bones / Spider Eyes** | Skip | Not relevant to the speedrun |
| **Sand** | Skip | Do not fill your inventory with this |

> **IMPORTANT: Gold is your most critical resource from this temple.** You need gold ingots to trade with Piglins in the Nether to obtain Ender Pearls. Aim to have at least **8-16 gold ingots** going into the Nether. If the temple provided fewer, plan to mine additional gold before portaling.

**Inventory management at the temple:**
- Drop useless items (bones, spider eyes, excess sand) immediately to keep inventory clean.
- Do not pick up horse armor unless you have a spare slot.
- Rotten flesh can serve as emergency food if you have nothing else, but prioritize proper food sources.

---

### Phase 5 — Finding Food

> **WARNING: Desert Temples contain NO food.** The loot table does not include bread, apples, or any non-rotten edibles. You **will** need to find food before entering the Nether, or you risk starving mid-run.

Options for finding food after looting the temple:

- **Cows, pigs, or sheep** in adjacent biomes — kill animals for raw meat, cook if possible.
- **Chickens** — drop raw chicken; easy to kill.
- **Wheat crops** near any village — hay bales give 9 wheat each; village chests often have bread.
- **Fish** from rivers or ocean biomes adjacent to the desert.
- In an emergency, **rotten flesh** from the temple can be eaten — each piece has an 80% chance of applying the Hunger effect, but keeps your health from dropping.

Plan your route after the temple to pass through a food-viable area before entering the Nether.

## After the Desert Temple

Once you have looted the temple and secured food, your goal is to build a Nether portal. Here is the typical post-temple checklist:

### 1. Assess Your Loot

- Count your gold ingots — do you have enough for Piglin barter (ideally 8-16+)?
- Do you have iron for a bucket and flint and steel?
- Do you have enough wood for a crafting table and pickaxe?
- Are you carrying food?

### 2. Find or Mine Iron

If the temple did not provide enough iron:
- Look for **iron ore** in nearby ravines, exposed cliffs, or cave entrances.
- You need a minimum of **3 iron ingots**: one for a bucket and two more for flint and steel or extra tools.
- Ideally, aim for **7+ iron ingots** for a full iron pickaxe and a bucket.

### 3. Locate a Lava Pool

To build a Nether portal, you need 10 obsidian (or the lava-water mold method using a bucket). Options:

- **Surface lava pools** — check low-elevation terrain near the desert.
- **Cave lava** — descend into any cave system. Lava pools are common deep underground.
- **Ruined Portal** — if one was generated nearby, it may already have some obsidian and a partial portal frame, saving significant time.

### 4. Navigation After the Temple

Deserts are visually open and flat, which makes navigation easier. Use this to your advantage:

- Scan the horizon for **villages, ravines, exposed stone, or biome edges** as you travel.
- The flatness of deserts means you can spot **lava pools at a distance** more easily than in forested biomes.
- **Rivers bordering deserts** are excellent landmarks — they mark biome transitions and often contain fish for food.

## Desert-Specific Challenges

### No Wood in the Desert

This is the defining challenge of desert temple seeds. Deserts have absolutely no natural wood generation. You **must** solve this before or after the temple:

- **Before the temple:** Collect wood at spawn if any adjacent biome has trees.
- **After the temple:** Travel to the nearest biome border (forests, plains, jungles all have trees).
- **Crafting priority:** Once you have logs, your first craft should be a **crafting table**, then **wooden pickaxe**, then upgrade to **stone pickaxe** immediately.
- **Minimum logs needed:** 4 logs (crafting table + pickaxe sticks). Aim for 8-12 total.

### No Food in the Desert

Deserts are food-barren. Passive mobs rarely spawn in deserts, there are no crops, and desert temples do not provide proper edible loot. Always route through a food source before entering the Nether.

### Navigation and Biome Identification

- Note your spawn coordinates before traveling — you may need to return toward spawn to find wood or food in adjacent biomes.
- Desert temples are almost always **visible from a distance** due to their height and distinctive color. If you cannot find the temple quickly, try ascending to higher ground for a better view.
- Use the **F3 debug screen** to check coordinates if you become disoriented in the flat desert terrain.

### Psychological Pressure

- The barren environment can cause rushed, careless play.
- Stay calm when approaching the temple. The TNT trap punishes rushed players more than any other mechanic in the game.
- If you cannot find the temple and are wasting time, use a logical search pattern: spiral outward from spawn rather than running in one direction indefinitely.

## Building the Nether Portal

Once you have loot from the temple and have secured food, wood, and iron, it is time to build your Nether portal.

### Requirements

- **10 obsidian** (for a minimal 4x5 portal frame, corners not needed) — or lava + water bucket for the mold method.
- **Flint and Steel** to light the portal — requires 1 iron ingot + 1 flint (from gravel).
- Alternatively, **fire charges** can light the portal if found in chests.

### Bucket Mold Method (Most Common in Speedruns)

If you do not have pre-mined obsidian, use the **lava-water mold technique**:

1. Craft an **iron bucket** (3 iron ingots in a V shape on the crafting table).
2. Find a **lava source pool** (underground or surface).
3. Use your pickaxe to **dig a portal-shaped mold** — 4 blocks wide and 5 blocks tall, leaving the corners open (you need 10 obsidian blocks total).
4. Pour **lava** from your bucket into each mold cell, then pour **water** over the lava to convert it to obsidian.
5. Collect the water back with your bucket and repeat for each block.
6. Light the completed portal with Flint and Steel.

> **Tip:** The **2-bucket method** is faster: carry both a lava bucket and a water bucket so you can place and convert without walking back to the source pool repeatedly. This requires 6 iron ingots total, so only do this if you have surplus iron from the temple.

### Portal Lighting

- Craft **Flint and Steel**: 1 iron ingot + 1 flint (break gravel with a shovel or by hand — flint drops at ~10% per gravel block).
- Right-click the bottom interior block of the portal frame to ignite it.
- The portal will display the purple swirling animation when active.

## Inventory Checklist

Use this checklist before entering the Nether portal:

### Essentials (Must Have)

- [ ] **Gold ingots** — at least 8, ideally 16+ (for Piglin trading)
- [ ] **Stone or Iron Pickaxe** — for mining in the Nether
- [ ] **Crafting Table** — in your hotbar for quick access
- [ ] **Food** — at least 5-10 food items (cooked meat, bread)
- [ ] **Flint and Steel** — to re-light the portal if it goes out

### Strong Additions

- [ ] **Water Bucket** — useful for Nether navigation and emergency lava control
- [ ] **Sword** — for combat (Piglins, Blazes, Zombie Pigmen)
- [ ] **Bow + Arrows** — for Ghast or Blaze ranged combat
- [ ] **Iron or Diamond Armor** — any protection helps in the Nether
- [ ] **Torches** — for Nether marking and fortress navigation
- [ ] **Blocks (cobblestone/dirt)** — for bridging, pillaring, and building in the Nether

### Nice to Have

- [ ] **Diamonds** — save these; do not spend on tools unless you have 3+ spare
- [ ] **Enchanted Book** — Fire Protection is extremely valuable in the Nether
- [ ] **Extra Iron Ingots** — for tools, crafting, or trading

### Drop Before Entering Nether

- Bones, spider eyes, excess sand from the temple
- Horse armor (unless you have a genuinely spare slot)
- Duplicate tools you will not use

## Tips & Tricks

### Temple Navigation

- **Memorize the color:** The blue terracotta checkerboard and central blue wool block are your landmarks. Train your eyes to spot them instantly upon entering the temple.
- **Sprint-jump to the temple roof** to get a quick bird's-eye view of surrounding terrain before descending into the chamber — this helps you spot lava pools and biome borders nearby.
- **Break the blue wool from the side** — never stand directly on it before breaking it. Crouch (Shift) when near the hole to avoid falling in accidentally.

### TNT Trap Avoidance

- **Practice the chamber descent** in a creative mode world before ranked matches. The spatial awareness of "where is the center" becomes intuitive with practice.
- **Crouch when near the hole** to prevent accidentally falling in at full sprint speed.
- If using the no-water direct-nav method, **always hug the corners** — imagine the chamber floor split into a 3x3 grid; the pressure plate is in the very center cell only.
- **If you accidentally fall onto the plate:** there is almost no time to save yourself — jumping and sprinting immediately has saved some runners if they react within one tick, but do not count on this. Just avoid the plate in the first place.

### Loot Speed

- **Open all four chests quickly** — each chest opens in under a second. Move corner to corner in one flowing path.
- **Shift-click** to move all items into your inventory at once from each chest.
- **Sort inventory immediately** after looting — drop junk before climbing out of the chamber so you do not waste time sorting later.

### Gold Management

- If you have **fewer than 8 gold ingots** from the temple, plan to mine some gold before entering the Nether. Gold ore generates between Y=-64 and Y=32; most common around Y=-16 in 1.18+ terrain.
- **Every gold ingot matters** — each Piglin trade costs 1 gold ingot and yields a random item. You need Ender Pearls, which have roughly a 4.7% chance per trade, so plan for **16-32 trades** minimum to have a reliable chance.
- **Do NOT craft gold ingots into armor or tools** — save every ingot for Piglin trading unless you have an extreme surplus of 32 or more.

### Desert Routing

- **Scan the horizon before committing to a direction.** Deserts are flat — use this to identify the temple, biome borders, and terrain features from a distance before wasting sprint momentum.
- If the temple is **partially buried** (a rare generation quirk), look for the orange and blue terracotta of the roof sticking above the sand. The chamber is still fully underground and intact.
- **Rivers bordering deserts** are excellent landmarks — they mark biome transitions, often contain fish for food, and sometimes have clay or gravel for flint.

### Time-Saving Habits

- Do not spend time **fully crafting iron tools** if stone tools will suffice — a stone pickaxe is fast enough for most portal-building needs.
- **Skip the upper floors** of the temple entirely — they contain no speedrun-relevant loot whatsoever.
- If you have a **saddle from the temple**, consider catching a horse in adjacent plains for faster overworld travel — but only if it would not cost more than 15-20 seconds to catch.
- Pre-plan your **portal building spot** before you have all the materials. Identify a nearby lava source while traveling to the temple so you know exactly where to go afterward.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Going to desert without wood | Cannot craft tools or portal | Always collect wood first at spawn |
| Stepping on the pressure plate | Instant death, all loot destroyed | Use water, detonation, or hug the walls |
| Ignoring food | Starvation mid-Nether | Find food at a biome border before portaling |
| Spending gold on tools | Not enough for Piglin trades | Save all gold for the Nether |
| Wasting time on upper floors | No useful loot there | Go straight to the underground chamber |
| Filling inventory with sand | Limits space for useful items | Drop sand immediately on pickup |
| Forgetting flint and steel | Cannot light the portal | Craft it from temple iron and gravel flint |
| Looting without sorting | Slow post-temple movement | Manage inventory before climbing out |

## Summary

The Desert Temple strategy is a high-reward, high-risk overworld route. Its key advantages are the potential for excellent loot (especially gold and diamonds) and a straightforward, predictable structure layout. Its challenges — no wood, no food, and the deadly TNT trap — demand careful planning and a specific order of operations.

**The Golden Rule of Desert Temple:** Get wood first, loot the temple safely, prioritize gold, find food, then build your portal.

Master these fundamentals, and the Desert Temple seed becomes one of the most satisfying and efficient overworld routes in your MCSR Ranked arsenal.
