# Overworld

The Overworld is the first and most variable phase of an MCSR Ranked run. It begins the instant your world loads and ends the moment you step through a Nether Portal. While it is often the shortest phase in terms of absolute time at high Elo, it sets the tone for everything that follows — a strong overworld means entering the Nether with the right resources at the right time, while a poor overworld can put you in deficit before the race truly begins.

This page provides an overview of all five overworld seed types, the resource targets you should always aim for, and links to each strategy's dedicated subpage.

---

## Goal of the Overworld Phase

Your single objective in the Overworld is to **enter the Nether as quickly as possible while meeting the minimum resource threshold** to survive and succeed in the Nether and beyond.

That means gathering:
- A reliable **tool set** (iron pickaxe at minimum, diamond pickaxe preferred)
- Sufficient **food** to sustain you through the Nether
- **Obsidian** and a **Flint and Steel** (or a repaired Ruined Portal) to create a portal
- **Iron armor or better** to survive Piglin aggression and Blaze combat
- Optionally: a **water bucket**, **a bed**, or extra food as safety buffers

What you do *not* need in the overworld:
- Diamond armor (heavy time investment, rarely worth it)
- Bows (Blaze Rods are obtained from melee or bow; beginners should focus on melee)
- More than ~15–20 food items

The overworld is about **hitting your minimums efficiently** — not maximizing every possible item.

## Seed Type Distribution by Elo

In MCSR Ranked, the seed type you receive is not purely random — it is weighted by your **Elo rating**. Lower-rated players receive more forgiving seed types such as Village and Shipwreck, while higher-rated players encounter a wider mix including the faster but more demanding Buried Treasure and Ruined Portal seeds.

The approximate distribution is as follows:

| Elo Range | Village | Shipwreck | Desert Temple | Ruined Portal | Buried Treasure |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **< 800** | ~40% | ~30% | ~15% | ~10% | ~5% |
| **800 – 1000** | ~35% | ~28% | ~17% | ~12% | ~8% |
| **1000 – 1200** | ~30% | ~25% | ~18% | ~15% | ~12% |
| **1200 – 1500** | ~25% | ~22% | ~18% | ~18% | ~17% |
| **> 1500** | ~20% | ~20% | ~20% | ~20% | ~20% |

> **Note:** These percentages are approximate and reflect community-observed trends. The exact formula is determined by the MCSR Ranked mod and may be updated across seasons.

As you climb the ladder, you will need to be comfortable with **all five seed types**. Do not neglect the rarer types — they will appear, and being unprepared for them costs you both the match and the Elo.

## Overworld Seed Types

Each seed type is named for the dominant structure near your spawn that drives your strategy. Click the links below for full step-by-step guides.

### [Village](./overworld/village.md)
A **Village** seed spawns a village (with or without a Blacksmith) within reasonable distance of your start. Villages provide food (bread, carrots, potatoes), beds, and occasionally iron tools or armor from the Blacksmith chest. They are the most forgiving seed type and the best starting point for new players.

- **Key loot:** Blacksmith chest (iron armor, iron tools, obsidian, occasionally diamond items), farm crops
- **Portal method:** Mine obsidian from Blacksmith chest or craft from lava + water
- **Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆ — Beginner

---

### [Shipwreck](./overworld/shipwreck.md)
A **Shipwreck** seed spawns a sunken ship near spawn, usually in an ocean or on a beach. Shipwrecks contain up to three chests: a **supply chest** (food, tools), a **treasure chest** (iron/gold/emeralds), and a **map chest** (a Buried Treasure map). The map chest is the priority — it leads you to a Buried Treasure that contains a **Heart of the Sea** and typically iron/gold/food.

- **Key loot:** Buried Treasure map, food, iron/gold ingots
- **Portal method:** Iron from treasure + lava bucket, or obsidian from alternate source
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆ — Beginner/Intermediate

---

### [Desert Temple](./overworld/desert_temple.md)
A **Desert Temple** (also called a Desert Pyramid) seed spawns the pyramid structure in a desert biome near spawn. It contains four buried loot chests accessible through a pressure-plate TNT trap in the center floor. The loot can include iron, gold, diamonds, horse armor, and enchanted books.

- **Key loot:** Four buried chests (varied; iron, gold, diamonds possible), desert village nearby sometimes
- **Portal method:** Diamonds or iron from temple + crafted obsidian via lava
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ — Intermediate (TNT trap awareness required)

---

### [Ruined Portal](./overworld/ruined_portal.md)
A **Ruined Portal** seed spawns a partially-constructed Nether Portal near spawn, always accompanied by a loot chest. The chest frequently contains **obsidian, iron/gold items**, and occasionally **flint and steel** or a **fire charge**. The existing portal structure reduces the obsidian you need to mine.

- **Key loot:** Ruined Portal chest (obsidian, gold, flint, fire charge), pre-placed obsidian blocks
- **Portal method:** Repair the existing portal with additional obsidian and ignite
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ — Intermediate (fast when optimized, strict resource routing)

---

### [Buried Treasure](./overworld/buried_treasure.md)
A **Buried Treasure** seed (sometimes called "BT") provides a Buried Treasure map from a nearby Shipwreck or directly as a spawn structure variant, and the treasure chest itself contains **iron, gold, diamonds, and food**. This is considered an advanced seed type because it requires precise digging placement and fast map reading.

- **Key loot:** Buried Treasure chest (diamonds, iron, gold, cooked fish), very little else
- **Portal method:** Diamonds from chest for tools + lava source for obsidian
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ — Advanced (high ceiling, fast top-end, demanding execution)

## General Overworld Principles

Regardless of your seed type, the following principles apply to **every** overworld:

### Always Be Moving
Time spent standing still — even to check your inventory — is lost time. Practice opening and managing your inventory while walking or sprinting. Sprint-jumping gives approximately 30% faster movement than walking and should be your default movement mode whenever you have food to spare.

### Prioritize the Portal
Your number one objective is always the Nether Portal. Do not over-invest in armor or food if it delays your portal entry. A player in iron armor who enters the Nether at 3:00 will nearly always beat a player in diamond armor who enters at 4:30.

### Know When to Skip Loot
Not every chest needs to be fully looted. If you already have enough food and iron, skip the extra items and keep moving. The art of the overworld is recognizing when you have "enough" and committing to the next objective.

### Use Your Map
Press `F3` to check your coordinates frequently. Knowing where you are relative to your portal or loot source saves you from wandering and backtracking — one of the biggest time losses for new players.

### Flint and Steel
Always carry **flint** (mined from gravel) and either a **steel (iron ingot)** to craft a Flint and Steel at the portal, or pick one up from loot. You cannot enter the Nether without igniting your portal.

## Universal Nether Entry Checklist

Before stepping through the portal, confirm you have the following:

| Item | Minimum | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Pickaxe** | Iron | Diamond preferred; needed for Blaze spawner and gold blocks in Bastion |
| **Sword** | Iron | For Blaze/Piglin combat |
| **Armor** | Iron (2–3 pieces) | Full iron is ideal; helmet + chestplate at minimum |
| **Food** | 10+ items | Cooked meat, bread, carrots — any filling food |
| **Flint and Steel** | 1 | Or a fire charge; to relight your portal if needed |
| **Shield** | Optional | Blocks Piglin arrows; highly recommended for beginners |
| **Water Bucket** | Optional | Extinguishes fire, useful for Nether navigation |
| **Gold Armor** | 1 piece | To avoid Piglin aggression during bartering |

> **Important:** You must wear **at least one piece of gold armor** when near Piglins, or they will immediately become hostile. A gold helmet is the most common choice as it does not replace your iron chest or leggings protection.
