## Introduction

**Village** is one of the most common and beginner-friendly overworld seed types in MCSR Ranked. On a Village seed, you spawn within a short distance of a village — a generated structure containing houses, farms, wells, and most importantly a **blacksmith** (also called a weaponsmith or toolsmith) chest that provides crucial early-game resources.

Because the seed filter ensures a guaranteed blacksmith with useful loot, Village seeds allow players to **skip nearly all early resource gathering** and go straight to building a Nether portal. This makes it the ideal starting point for new MCSR Ranked competitors who are still learning the game.

### Elo Distribution

Village seeds appear at different rates depending on your Elo rating:

| Elo Range | Village Seed Probability |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| 0 – 599   | **55%** of all seeds     |
| 600 – 1199 | **30%** of all seeds    |
| 1200+     | **20%** of all seeds     |

> [!NOTE]
> As you climb the ranked ladder, Village seeds become less frequent and you will encounter more complex seed types such as Shipwreck, Desert Temple, and Buried Treasure. Mastering Village early is essential because you will still see it occasionally at high Elo, and the fundamentals it teaches (golem kill, portal building) apply to every other seed type.

The high frequency at low Elo is intentional — it gives newer players a reliable, forgiving route that doesn't require advanced knowledge of ocean monuments or buried treasure compasses to execute.

## MCSR Ranked Seed Guarantees

MCSR Ranked uses a **seed filter** to ensure every generated world meets a minimum standard of fairness and viability. When you receive a Village seed, the following properties are **always guaranteed**:

### Spawn Distance
- You will always spawn **within 7 chunks** of the village center (approximately 112 blocks).
- This means you should be able to **see or hear the village** almost immediately after spawning.
- You will **never** have to run more than a few seconds to reach it.

### Coordinate Orientation
- The village is always located at **positive X and positive Z coordinates** relative to spawn.
- This means you should generally look toward the **positive X/Z quadrant** (South-East direction) when orienting yourself at spawn.
- Your in-game **F3 debug screen** or a compass pointing toward the spawn point can help you orient quickly.

### Guaranteed Blacksmith
- Every Village seed has **at least one blacksmith** in the village.
- The blacksmith chest will always contain **useful loot** — it will never be completely empty or filled with only junk items.
- This is the single most important guarantee, as it allows you to skip most early-game crafting.

### Lava / Obsidian Guarantee
Every Village seed is guaranteed to have **one of the following**:
- **3 lava pools** within a reasonable distance, used for casting a portal via the bucket method, OR
- **8 or more obsidian** in the blacksmith chest, allowing you to place the portal directly.

This means every Village seed has a viable path to the Nether — you will never be stuck without a way to build a portal.

### Iron Golem Guarantee
- The village's **Iron Golem always drops exactly 4 iron ingots** when killed.
- This is a guaranteed drop, not a random one — you will always get exactly 4 iron from the golem.

### Minimum Iron + Food
- Between the blacksmith chest, the golem drop, and village resources, you are guaranteed **at least 7 iron ingots total**, as well as **sufficient food** to survive the route.
- This ensures you can always craft a **bucket** and a **flint and steel**, which are the two essential crafting goals of the overworld phase.

### Taiga Village Special Rules
- Taiga villages follow slightly different rules (see [Village Types](#village-types) below).
- Taiga seeds are guaranteed to have **10 or more obsidian**, which is enough to build a full portal without any bucket crafting.
- Because of this, Taiga villages have a **simplified portal-building method** (see [Method C: Taiga Enter](#method-c-taiga-enter)).

## Village Types

Not all villages are the same. The biome determines the village's visual style, available structures, and sometimes its loot table. Understanding each type helps you recognize them quickly and adapt your route.

### Plains Village

- **Appearance:** Oak wood buildings with cobblestone and oak planks. White/gray color scheme.
- **Common in:** Plains and sunflower plains biomes.
- **Farms:** Has wheat farms — wheat can be crafted into **bread** for food.
- **Hay Bales:** Often has hay bales scattered around, each crafting into **9 bread**.
- **Notes:** The most commonly seen Village type. Very recognizable. Blacksmith has the standard stone building with a grindstone outside.
- **Portal Method:** Usually Lava Cast (Method A) unless 8+ obsidian is in the chest.

### Desert Village

- **Appearance:** Sandstone buildings. Yellow/tan color scheme.
- **Common in:** Desert biomes.
- **Farms:** Has melon, wheat, or no traditional farms. Food can sometimes be scarcer.
- **Hay Bales:** Has hay bales — reliable food source.
- **Notes:** Visually distinct and easy to spot against sand. No grass, so navigation can feel different. The blacksmith building is the same functional layout but made of sandstone.
- **Portal Method:** Usually Lava Cast or pre-placed obsidian from chest.

### Savanna Village

- **Appearance:** Acacia wood buildings. Orange/red-brown color scheme.
- **Common in:** Savanna biomes.
- **Farms:** Wheat farms typically present. Hay bales common.
- **Notes:** The most visually distinctive village type due to the bright orange acacia wood. Blacksmith layout is identical in function. Savanna villages can sometimes be spread out, so pay attention to where the blacksmith is relative to where you enter the village.
- **Portal Method:** Usually Lava Cast or pre-placed obsidian from chest.

### Taiga Village

- **Appearance:** Spruce wood buildings. Dark brown color scheme with cobblestone.
- **Common in:** Taiga and snowy taiga biomes.
- **Farms:** May have wheat, carrot, or potato farms. Hay bales common.
- **Notes:** Taiga villages have **special seed filter rules** — they always have **10+ obsidian** guaranteed. This makes them the most powerful village type because you can skip bucket crafting entirely and place the portal directly.
- **Portal Method:** Always **Taiga Enter** (Method C) — no bucket needed.

> [!TIP]
> You can identify the village biome before you even reach it. Listen for ambient sounds and look at the terrain color. Acacia trees = Savanna, spruce trees = Taiga, sand = Desert, flat green plains = Plains. Identifying the biome early lets you mentally prepare the correct route.

## General Route Overview

Here is a high-level summary of the Village route before we break it down step by step:

1. **Spawn** → Orient yourself, locate the village (sounds/rooftops/compass).
2. **Gather wood** → Punch 3–5 logs, craft a crafting table and stone axe as quickly as possible.
3. **Get stone tools** → Mine cobblestone, craft a stone pickaxe and stone axe.
4. **Loot the blacksmith** → Open the chest, take iron ingots, obsidian, food, and other useful items.
5. **Kill the Iron Golem** → Use the 3-block pillar method; collect 4 guaranteed iron ingots.
6. **Gather food** → Break hay bales for bread, collect chest food if available.
7. **Get flint** → Break gravel blocks until you have at least 1 flint (ideally 2+).
8. **Craft** → Stone pickaxe (if needed), bucket (3 iron), flint and steel (1 iron + 1 flint).
9. **Build the Nether Portal** → Choose Method A, B, or C based on your loot.
10. **Enter the Nether** → Ready for the Nether phase.

The entire overworld phase on a Village seed should take approximately **2–4 minutes** for intermediate players, and **4–7 minutes** for beginners still learning the route.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

### Spawn

The first few seconds of a run are critical. Move efficiently and don't waste time standing still.

**Immediately upon spawning:**

- **Look around in a full circle** to scan for village rooftops, tall structures (churches/towers), or farm plots on the horizon.
- **Listen carefully** — villager "hmm" sounds carry quite far and are often the first indicator that a village is nearby.
- **Check your coordinates** (F3 or visible HUD) — the village is always at positive X/Z, so note which direction is positive X and positive Z (East and South respectively in standard Minecraft orientation). Begin moving in that general direction.
- If you have a **compass** in your inventory (provided by some resource packs or visible HUD mods), it points toward spawn — the village will be roughly in the opposite or angled direction.

**What to look for:**
- **Tall pointed roofs** (Plains/Savanna churches are very visible).
- **Hay bales** visible from a distance — tall yellow blocks in groups.
- **Flat farm plots** — rectangular darker patches of dirt/farmland at ground level.
- **Torches on buildings** — visible at night but the run usually starts in daytime.
- **Gravel paths** — villages have gravel-covered paths between buildings, visible when you get close.

> [!TIP]
> Sprint-jump constantly while traveling to the village. Holding **W + Space** while sprinting gives you slightly faster horizontal movement than running alone. Never walk when you can sprint.

**If you can't find the village immediately:**
- Try climbing a nearby hill or tree for a better vantage point.
- Run in the positive X/Z diagonal for 20–30 blocks, then stop and rotate your view — a village within 7 chunks should be visible from slightly elevated ground.
- Remember the 7-chunk guarantee — if you've run more than ~120 blocks and still see nothing, try a slightly different angle.

---

### Getting Wood and Tools

Wood is the first priority before anything else. You need it for a crafting table, and stone tools are far more efficient than punching everything by hand.

**Steps:**

1. **Punch 3 logs** from the nearest tree (oak, acacia, spruce, etc.) — hold left-click on the log block until it drops.
   - You can also punch **2 logs from a village building** if one is right next to you, saving travel time.
2. Open your **2×2 inventory crafting grid** and craft the logs into **12 wooden planks**.
3. Craft a **crafting table** (4 planks in a 2×2).
4. Place the crafting table on the ground (right-click).
5. At the crafting table, craft **2 wooden sticks** (2 planks vertically).
6. Craft a **wooden pickaxe** (3 planks + 2 sticks in pickaxe shape).
7. Use the wooden pickaxe to mine **3 cobblestone** from the ground or any stone surface.
8. Craft a **stone axe** at the crafting table (2 cobblestone + 2 sticks in axe shape).

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Craft a **stone axe first**, not a stone sword. The stone axe deals **9 damage** per hit (as opposed to the sword's 5), making it far more effective for killing the Iron Golem. You can also use it to break wood faster. The sword offers no meaningful advantage in this route.

**Why not iron tools first?**
- The blacksmith may give you iron ingots, but you only have **7+ iron guaranteed**. You need iron for the **bucket (3 iron)** and **flint and steel (1 iron)**. Spending iron on tools wastes it.
- Stone tools are free (cobblestone is everywhere) and more than adequate for the overworld phase.
- The one exception is if you get a surplus of iron from the blacksmith chest (10+ ingots) — then crafting an iron sword or pickaxe is fine.

### Finding and Looting the Blacksmith

The blacksmith is the single most important structure in the village. Its chest contains the resources that define your entire route.

**How to identify the blacksmith:**

- It is a **stone building** (cobblestone walls, stone brick accents) regardless of village biome — though it may have some biome-specific wood mixed in.
- There is always a **grindstone** sitting on a stone slab **outside the front door**, at ground level. This is the most reliable identifier — look for the grindstone.
- It may also have a **lava pool** or **anvil** nearby, though these are not always present.
- The building has a **chest inside** — there will be a wooden chest visible through the open doorway or just inside.

**Looting the chest:**

1. Open the blacksmith door (right-click) or just run through if the door is open.
2. Right-click the chest to open it.
3. **Immediately assess the loot.** Key items to look for:
   - **Iron Ingots** — Take all of them. This is your primary goal.
   - **Obsidian** — If you see 8 or more, you can skip the lava cast entirely (see Method B).
   - **Food** — Bread, apples, carrots — take any you see.
   - **Saddle** — Usually skip. It has no use in a standard run.
   - **Iron/Gold/Diamond Armor** — Take iron or diamond pieces if they appear; iron boots + iron chestplate, etc. Skip gold.
   - **Swords/Pickaxes** — An iron sword or pickaxe can be worth taking if it doesn't cost you time. An iron pickaxe saves you from needing to craft one later.
   - **Emeralds** — Skip. No trading is done in this route.

4. **Shift-click** each item to instantly move it to your inventory (hold Shift and left-click each item). This is significantly faster than drag-and-drop.
5. Aim to spend **2–4 seconds** in the chest. Pre-plan which items to grab based on a quick visual scan of the chest grid before clicking.

> [!TIP]
> Experienced players scan the entire chest in under a second and immediately start shift-clicking priority items. Practice identifying the items by their icons at a glance: iron ingots are gray hexagonal shapes, obsidian is dark purple/black squares, bread is a brown curved shape. The faster you recognize them, the faster you loot.

> [!WARNING]
> Don't get greedy in the blacksmith chest. Spending 8–10 seconds grabbing every last item (including useless saddles or single emeralds) wastes more time than those items are worth. Be selective and fast.

**Counting your iron:**
- After looting the chest, quickly count your iron ingots in your hotbar/inventory.
- You need a minimum of **4 iron** for the bucket (3) and flint and steel (1).
- You'll get **4 more iron from the golem**, bringing your total to 8+. But you want to know what you're working with before you continue.

### Killing the Iron Golem

The Iron Golem patrols the village and will attack you if you harm it or nearby villagers. **Never accidentally hit a villager** — it will cause the golem to aggro you, and a golem can kill you in 2–3 hits, ending your run.

**The 3-Block Pillar Method (Recommended for Beginners):**

The pillar method keeps you completely safe from the golem's massive melee attacks while you deal damage from above.

1. **Find the Iron Golem.** It wanders around the village, usually near the center or on paths. It is a large, gray-white humanoid mob about 2.9 blocks tall.
2. **Get close enough** to the golem so it notices you (it will turn to face you and begin walking toward you).
3. **Quickly place 3 dirt or cobblestone blocks beneath yourself**, jumping between each placement:
   - Jump → place block beneath you while in the air (look down at your feet and right-click).
   - Jump again → place a second block beneath you.
   - Jump again → place a third block.
   - You are now standing on a 3-block-high pillar. The golem **cannot reach you** at this height.
4. **Equip your stone axe** (or whatever weapon you have) in your hotbar.
5. **Left-click the golem repeatedly.** Attack it from the top of your pillar — you can hit its head/body from 3 blocks up.
6. **Critical hits:** For maximum damage, attack the golem while **falling** (while descending after a jump). A critical hit deals 150% damage (shown by small star particles appearing on the mob). To do this:
   - Jump on your pillar → at the peak of your jump, hold left-click → your attack will land as you descend, dealing a critical hit.
   - Alternate between jumping and landing attacks in rhythm.
7. The golem has **100 HP (50 hearts)**. A stone axe deals 9 damage normally and ~13.5 damage on a critical hit. It takes approximately **8–12 hits** with a stone axe to kill the golem.
8. **Collect the drops:** The golem always drops **4 iron ingots** and 0–2 poppies (flowers). Pick up the iron ingots — ignore the poppies.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Always build the pillar **before** engaging the golem. If you try to fight the golem on the ground, it will knock you into the air and deal massive damage. Even one hit from a golem can chunk your health significantly. The pillar costs you 2–3 seconds and completely eliminates all risk.

**Alternative: Fight on the ground (Advanced):**
- Experienced players sometimes strafe and attack the golem on flat ground, using the attack cooldown to maximize DPS and dodging its swings.
- This is faster (~3–5 seconds saved) but risky — if you mis-time a dodge, you take significant damage and may die.
- Not recommended for beginners. Stick to the pillar method until you are comfortable with the route.

**When to kill the golem:**
- Kill the golem **after** looting the blacksmith. This ensures you already know if you have enough iron before deciding how to spend the golem's drops.
- Ideally, kill it **while traveling between structures** — if the golem is between the blacksmith and the well, engage it naturally without backtracking.

### Gathering Food

You will burn hunger throughout the run from sprinting and taking damage. Running out of food in the Nether is a run-ender, so take food seriously.

**Food sources in a Village seed:**

- **Hay Bales:** The most reliable food source. Each hay bale drops **9 wheat** when broken. 3 wheat craft into 1 bread. A full hay bale gives you 3 bread. Break hay bales with any tool (axes are fastest). Aim to collect **6–10 bread minimum** (2–3 hay bales).
- **Blacksmith Chest:** May contain bread, apples, or carrots. Take all food items.
- **Village Farms:** Carrot and potato farms can be harvested quickly. Right-click to harvest (replants automatically) or break the farmland for bulk harvest. Carrots and potatoes restore a small amount of hunger and stack to 64.
- **Villager Chest Food:** Some village houses have chests with food inside. Usually not worth hunting for specifically, but if you pass by a house with an open chest, glance at it.

> [!TIP]
> Hay bales are far more efficient than harvesting individual crops. Always prioritize breaking hay bales. Two hay bales (6 bread) give you enough food to comfortably complete the entire Nether phase without worrying about starvation.

**How much food do you need?**
- Minimum: **6 bread** (or equivalent) to survive the Nether without issues.
- Comfortable: **10–12 bread** allows you to sprint freely without managing hunger carefully.
- Too much: Taking 20+ food items wastes inventory space and time. Don't go overboard.

### Getting Flint

Flint is required to craft **Flint and Steel**, which is used to light the Nether portal. You need at least **1 flint**.

**How to get flint:**

- Flint is obtained by breaking **gravel blocks**. Each gravel block has approximately a **50% chance** to drop flint instead of itself when broken.
- Gravel is easy to find in villages — the **paths between buildings** are made of gravel. You can break these path blocks for flint.
- Break gravel with any tool or your fist (it breaks quickly regardless of tool).

**Recommended approach:**

1. Find a gravel path (the tan/dark gray blocks forming the roads in the village).
2. Break **2–4 gravel blocks**.
3. With 50% drop chance per block, breaking 4 blocks gives you approximately a **93.75% chance** of getting at least 1 flint.
4. If you get unlucky (no flint after 4 blocks), break 2–3 more. The probability of getting no flint after 6 breaks is only ~1.5%.
5. Ideally, collect **2 flint** — one for Flint and Steel now, and one as backup in case the portal needs re-lighting.

> [!NOTE]
> Gravel paths are abundant in every village type. You will almost never need to search for gravel outside the village. If the paths seem short (some desert villages have limited gravel), check under the well or near the blacksmith building.

### Crafting Order

Once you have your materials gathered, craft your items in this order. Efficiency matters — open the crafting table only once if possible.

**Prerequisites before crafting:**
- 7+ iron ingots (4 from golem + 3+ from blacksmith)
- 1+ flint
- Cobblestone (you should have some from mining earlier)

**Crafting sequence:**

1. **Stone Pickaxe** (if you don't already have one):
   - 3 cobblestone + 2 sticks
   - Needed only if you plan to mine additional obsidian or if you don't have an iron pickaxe from the chest.

2. **Bucket** (top priority):
   - 3 iron ingots in a V-shape
   - Used to carry water for the lava cast portal method.
   - Even if you have 8+ obsidian, crafting a bucket is good insurance.

3. **Flint and Steel**:
   - 1 iron ingot + 1 flint in a diagonal
   - Used to light the Nether portal.
   - This is always required — there is no other way to light a portal in a standard run.

4. **Optional crafts** (only if you have surplus resources):
   - **Stone Sword** — If you still have cobblestone and expect combat.
   - **Iron Sword** — If you have extra iron (10+ ingots). Helpful in the Nether.
   - **Iron Pickaxe** — If you didn't get one from the blacksmith chest and need to mine obsidian.

> [!TIP]
> Plan your crafting before opening the table. Know exactly what you're making, click quickly, and close the table. Experienced players craft the bucket and flint and steel in under 5 seconds by pre-positioning the cursor.

## Building the Nether Portal

A Nether portal requires a **frame of 10 obsidian** (minimum, without corners) or **14 obsidian** (with corners). The inner 2×3 area of air must then be lit with Flint and Steel to activate the portal.

There are three methods used on Village seeds, depending on your loot.

---

### Method A: Lava Cast

**When to use:** When you have a **bucket** and there are **lava pools** nearby (guaranteed if you don't have 8+ obsidian in the chest).

The lava cast involves building a mold out of dirt or other non-flammable blocks, pouring lava into it, then converting the lava to obsidian using water.

**Detailed Steps:**

1. **Craft a bucket** (3 iron ingots in a V-shape) at the crafting table.

2. **Find a lava pool.** Lava pools are small surface pools found in the overworld. In a Village seed, 3 lava pools are guaranteed nearby. Look for the orange glow of lava on the surface, or listen for the characteristic bubbling/hissing sound. Common locations:
   - Open fields near the village.
   - Inside cave openings at the surface.
   - Sometimes partially hidden by tall grass or trees.

3. **Fill your bucket with lava.** Right-click on a lava source block (not a flowing lava block — it must be a still, full block) to fill the bucket. A lava source block is a solid, full cube of lava with no movement. If lava is flowing/dripping, it is not a source.

4. **Choose a flat location** near the lava pool to build your portal frame. Pick a spot with no overhead obstacles (trees, overhangs) within at least 5 blocks of height.

5. **Build the dirt mold.** The portal frame needs a 4-wide × 5-tall inner opening (or 2×3 minimum). Build a dirt shell that will hold lava in the correct shape:
   - Place a **2-wide, 1-block-deep channel** of dirt blocks: this forms the floor of the two vertical columns.
   - Alternatively, build a full "U-shape" mold from dirt:
     - Bottom row: 4 dirt blocks in a line (the bottom of the portal).
     - Left column: 3 dirt blocks stacked on the leftmost bottom block.
     - Right column: 3 dirt blocks stacked on the rightmost bottom block.
     - Top row: 4 dirt blocks connecting the two columns.
   - The mold should create 10 "slots" where lava will sit and be converted to obsidian.

6. **Place water at the top** of your mold. Right-click with a **water bucket** on the top edge of one of the columns so water flows down into the mold frame area. Water flowing over lava source blocks converts them to obsidian instantly.
   - Alternatively, place water at a position where it flows horizontally across the top of your mold.

7. **Pour lava into each slot.** With your lava bucket, right-click into each dirt slot in your mold, filling them with lava source blocks. As water contacts each lava source, it converts to obsidian.
   - Work from bottom to top or systematically so you don't accidentally stand in lava.
   - Each lava bucket fill creates one obsidian block. You will need to refill the bucket multiple times (10 times for a standard portal frame).

8. **Collect the water.** After all obsidian is placed, right-click the water source block to collect it back into your bucket.

9. **Break the dirt mold.** Use your axe or hands to quickly break the surrounding dirt frame, revealing the obsidian portal structure beneath.

10. **Light the portal.** Stand inside the obsidian frame, select your **Flint and Steel**, and right-click on any obsidian block forming the inner edge of the portal. The portal interior should immediately turn purple and swirl with the portal animation. Step into it to travel to the Nether.

> [!WARNING]
> Never stand directly on top of lava while pouring. Always position yourself above and to the side. One wrong step into a lava pool during a run is fatal and ends your attempt immediately.

> [!TIP]
> The most common lava cast shape runners use is the "2-column" method: pour lava into 5 slots on the left column, 5 on the right, with water running across the top of both. This requires exactly 10 lava pours (10 bucket trips). Some runners use a pre-built dirt frame for speed; practice this until it's muscle memory.

---

### Method B: Blacksmith Enter / Pre-placed Obsidian

**When to use:** When the blacksmith chest contains **8 or more obsidian** (the seed filter guarantees either this OR 3 lava pools).

This is the fastest portal-building method and requires no lava, no water, and no bucket crafting for the portal (though crafting a bucket is still recommended for Nether loot collection).

**Steps:**

1. **Confirm you have 8+ obsidian** in your inventory after looting the blacksmith.
2. **Find a flat surface** anywhere convenient — near the village well, on a field, anywhere with no obstacles above.
3. **Place the obsidian blocks** directly in the portal frame shape:
   - You need a minimum of **10 obsidian** for a standard portal (no corners).
   - Standard frameless portal shape:
     ```
     O O O O    <- top row (4 obsidian)
     O . . O    <- middle row (2 obsidian, 2 air)
     O . . O    <- middle row (2 obsidian, 2 air)
     O O O O    <- bottom row (4 obsidian)
     ```
   - Or a smaller 2×3 interior portal:
     ```
     O O O O    <- top row (4 obsidian)
     O . . O    <- 3 middle rows (2 obs each)
     O . . O
     O . . O
     O O O O    <- bottom row (4 obsidian)
     ```
   - The minimum viable portal has a **2-block wide × 3-block tall** interior, requiring 10 obsidian frame blocks (no corner pieces).
4. **Light the portal** with Flint and Steel (right-click the inner face of an obsidian block).
5. Enter the portal.

> [!NOTE]
> "Blacksmith Enter" is a term some runners use for Method B because it refers to using the obsidian already in the chest to directly "enter" the Nether. It requires no casting — just placing and lighting.

**What if you have exactly 8 obsidian?**
- An 8-obsidian portal is not possible for a standard frame (minimum is 10).
- However, you can use **natural terrain** to substitute some blocks. If you place obsidian against a cliff face, dirt wall, or other solid blocks, those solid blocks form the "corners," and you only need obsidian for the sides. This is called a **terrain portal** and can reduce the obsidian requirement to as few as 6 blocks in the right terrain.
- Alternatively, mine 2 more obsidian using an **iron pickaxe** from a lava pool at the surface (lava + water = obsidian). This requires an iron pickaxe — stone cannot mine obsidian.

---

### Method C: Taiga Enter

**When to use:** When you are on a **Taiga Village seed**. Taiga villages always have **10+ obsidian** guaranteed, making this the simplest and fastest portal method.

**Steps:**

1. **Loot the blacksmith** and collect the obsidian (there will be 10 or more).
2. The route is identical to **Method B** (Pre-placed Obsidian) — simply place your 10+ obsidian in a portal shape and light it.
3. No bucket is required for the portal itself.

> [!TIP]
> On a Taiga seed, you may not even need to craft a bucket. Save the 3 iron ingots you'd spend on a bucket for other uses (iron sword, iron pickaxe) or just hold onto them for potential Nether bastion trades. Always craft Flint and Steel regardless — you still need it to light the portal.

**Why Taiga is special:**
- The 10+ obsidian guarantee means Taiga villages have the **most consistent and fastest portal-building process** of any village type.
- The only downside is that Taiga biomes can sometimes have uneven terrain that makes placing obsidian slightly tricky — find a flat spot near the blacksmith.

## Inventory Checklist

Before entering the Nether portal, do a quick mental (or literal) check of your inventory. **Do not enter the Nether** if you are missing critical items.

### Must Have

| Item | Quantity | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Food (Bread/Carrots/etc.) | 6–12+ | Survive the Nether without starving |
| Flint and Steel | 1 | Re-light portal if it breaks; essential |
| Bucket (water or empty) | 1 | Collect blaze rods / loot bastions / emergency use |
| Iron Ingots | 1–3 spare | Trading in bastion / backup resources |
| Stone Pickaxe or Iron Pickaxe | 1 | Mine in Nether (gold ore, nether brick) |

## Common Mistakes

These are the errors that beginners most frequently make on Village seeds. Awareness of them is the first step to avoiding them.

### 1. Ignoring the Golem (or Forgetting to Kill It)
- New players sometimes skip the Iron Golem to "save time." This is a mistake — 4 guaranteed iron ingots are essential for crafting.
- If you skip the golem, you may not have enough iron to craft both a bucket and a flint and steel.

### 2. Fighting the Golem on the Ground
- Engaging the golem without a pillar is extremely dangerous. It can one- or two-shot you at low health.
- Always build the 3-block pillar. It costs 2–3 seconds and is 100% safe.

### 3. Accidental Villager Aggro
- Hitting a villager by mistake causes the golem to immediately aggro you at full speed with no warning.
- Be careful with your left-clicks around villagers. If the golem charges you unexpectedly, immediately pillar up.

### 4. Not Counting Iron Before Crafting
- Players often craft a bucket thinking they have enough iron, only to realize they don't have any left for Flint and Steel.
- Always count: you need 3 iron for bucket + 1 iron for flint and steel = **4 iron minimum**. Add the 4 from the golem = need at least 0 from the chest if you kill the golem (but the chest always gives you some too).

### 5. Standing in Lava During a Lava Cast
- Moving too quickly during the lava cast and stepping into a lava pool is a run-ending mistake.
- Move slowly and deliberately near lava. The 2–3 seconds you save by rushing are not worth dying.

### 6. Forgetting Flint and Steel in the Nether
- Ghast fireballs can break your Nether portal. If you didn't bring a Flint and Steel, you cannot re-light it and will be stuck.
- Always craft Flint and Steel before entering the portal. No exceptions.

### 7. Running Out of Food
- Sprinting constantly drains hunger. Going into the Nether with only 1–2 bread pieces will leave you starving mid-way through.
- Collect at least 2 hay bales worth of bread (6 pieces). More is safer.

### 8. Spending Too Long in the Blacksmith
- Carefully picking up every item one by one, reading item tooltips, or debating whether to take a saddle wastes valuable time.
- Scan fast, shift-click priority items, close the chest. Aim for under 4 seconds total in the chest.

### 9. Failing to Find the Blacksmith
- Some players run around the village for 30+ seconds looking for the blacksmith because they don't know the identifier.
- Remember: **look for the grindstone outside the door**. It is always present at the blacksmith and no other building.

### 10. Building the Portal in a Bad Location
- Placing the portal under trees (fire hazard), in water, on uneven terrain, or in a cramped spot wastes time and can cause problems.
- Pick a flat, open area. The field next to the blacksmith is usually ideal.

## Tips & Tricks

These optimizations separate fast Village runs from very fast ones. Apply them after you're comfortable with the basic route.

### Look Around While Killing the Golem
- While you repeatedly click to attack the golem from your pillar, **rotate your camera** to scout the surroundings.
- Identify where hay bales are, where the well is (for reference), where gravel paths are for flint.
- When the golem dies, you already know exactly where to go next — no hesitation.

### Sprint-Jump Between Every Structure
- Never walk. From spawn to the village, from the blacksmith to the golem, from the golem to the hay bales, from the hay bales to the crafting spot — sprint-jump the entire time.
- Sprint-jumping is ~30% faster than walking and gives a slight bonus to horizontal movement compared to just sprinting.

### Pre-scan the Blacksmith From the Door
- Before right-clicking the chest, **stand in the doorway** and check if there's an iron pickaxe or armor visible on the ground (dropped by golems or villagers).
- This takes zero extra time because you're already entering the building.

### Combine Looting and Moving
- After grabbing your blacksmith loot, immediately start walking toward the Iron Golem while your inventory is still open — sort/organize items as you move to the golem.
- Minecraft allows you to walk while your inventory is open (though you cannot sprint).

### Use the Grindstone to Identify the Blacksmith Instantly
- Don't visually scan every building. As you enter the village, run along the paths and watch for the grindstone at ground level — it's faster than trying to recognize the building shape.
- The grindstone looks like a two-cylinder stone object sitting on a stone slab.

### Hay Bale Location Shortcut
- Hay bales are almost always near the center of the village or in a cluster near farms.
- After killing the golem, head straight toward the largest open area in the village — hay bales will usually be visible there.

### Lava Cast: Pre-place Your Dirt Mold Quickly
- Build a practice dirt mold shape at your desk away from the screen until it's muscle memory.
- In a run, the fastest runners place the entire mold in under 5 seconds because they've done it hundreds of times.

### Use the Well as a Navigation Landmark
- The village well is a stone circle structure at the center of the village. It's visible from almost anywhere in the village.
- Use it to orient yourself — the well is usually at the center, and the blacksmith is typically within 20–40 blocks of the well.

### Check for Obsidian First
- When you open the blacksmith chest, **look for obsidian before anything else**.
- If you see 8+ pieces of dark purple-black blocks, you can immediately switch your mental plan to Method B and skip scouting for lava pools entirely.
- This saves the time you'd spend looking for a lava pool that you don't need.

### Pillar from Pre-placed Blocks
- Instead of building a separate pillar for the golem, look for any elevated surface in the village: the church/temple steps, a fence post platform, or a hay bale stack.
- Hay bales are 1 block tall — standing on 2 hay bales in a stack already puts you at height 3, which is enough to be safe from the golem.

### Craft Table on the Path
- Place your crafting table on the gravel path in the village. This lets you break the gravel **right next to where you're crafting** and pick up flint while simultaneously standing at the table.
- Saves 5–10 seconds of walking between the gravel area and the crafting area.

### Know When to Skip the Bucket
- If you have 10+ obsidian from a Taiga village, don't craft a bucket unless you have leftover iron.
- Bucket is 3 iron — that's iron you could use for an iron sword (2 iron + sticks), iron pickaxe (3 iron), or just keep for the Nether.
- On Taiga seeds specifically, always ask: "Do I actually need this bucket?" before crafting it.

### The 6-Obsidian Terrain Portal
- If you only have 8 obsidian and need 10 for a standard frame, look for a **cliff face, dirt wall, or two adjacent solid blocks** in the environment.
- Place your obsidian against those solid blocks — the terrain blocks can serve as corner substitutes, reducing the obsidian needed to as few as 6 in ideal terrain.
- This requires planning ahead but can save you 2 iron pickaxe uses or lava trips.

> [!TIP]
> The single best thing you can do to improve your Village times is **practice the lava cast** repeatedly in a creative world. Build the dirt mold 50 times until you can do it in 5–6 seconds without thinking. The lava cast is the slowest part of the Village route, and every second you shave off there directly improves your overall run time.
