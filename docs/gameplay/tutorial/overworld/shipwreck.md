## Introduction

**Shipwreck seeds** are among the most popular overworld starting strategies in MCSR Ranked. When you spawn near a shipwreck, you gain access to a powerful loot chain: the wreck itself provides early food, iron, gold, and — most importantly — a **Buried Treasure Map**. That map leads you to a **Buried Treasure chest**, which reliably contains a **Heart of the Sea**, food, iron, gold, and sometimes TNT or emeralds.

This loot chain can set you up for a nether entry with good gear, a stacked inventory, and minimal crafting overhead compared to other seed types.

### Elo Distribution

Shipwreck seeds appear across all MCSR Ranked Elo brackets, but their frequency is weighted to reward newer players while still appearing at high Elo:

| Elo Bracket | Shipwreck Frequency |
|---|---|
| 0 – 599 (Beginner) | ~15% of seeds |
| 600 – 1199 (Intermediate) | ~25% of seeds |
| 1200+ (Advanced) | ~20% of seeds |

At the **600–1199** bracket, shipwreck is the single most common structured seed type. This makes mastering the route especially valuable for players trying to climb out of intermediate Elo. Even at 1200+, a one-in-five chance means you will see shipwreck seeds regularly and must execute efficiently to stay competitive.

### Why Shipwreck Seeds Are Used

- **Reliable iron source** — The supply and treasure chests almost always contain iron ingots and/or iron nuggets.
- **Free food** — Supply chests drop carrots, potatoes, and rotten flesh in large quantities.
- **Buried Treasure Map** — Found in the map chest, this is the lynchpin of the entire strategy and leads to one of the best early-game loot pools in the game.
- **No mob killing required** — Unlike village or ruined portal seeds, you don't need to hunt for specific mobs or structures to get enough iron for a bucket and flint.
- **Predictable routing** — Once you understand the wreck layout, every run follows the same mechanical loop with very low variance.

## MCSR Ranked Seed Guarantees

MCSR Ranked uses a **custom seed pool** rather than random seeds. Every seed in the pool has been filtered to meet specific guarantees for each seed type. For shipwreck seeds, the following are guaranteed:

- **A shipwreck will spawn within a short distance of the player's spawn point.** You will never have to run more than a few hundred blocks to reach it.
- **The shipwreck will contain a map chest with a Buried Treasure Map.** Shipwrecks that are missing this chest (which can happen in vanilla) are excluded from the seed pool.
- **The Buried Treasure chest will always be present** at the location marked on the map.
- **The Buried Treasure chest will always contain a Heart of the Sea** (this is guaranteed by vanilla mechanics, but is worth stating explicitly).
- **Sufficient wood is accessible** — either from the shipwreck structure itself or from nearby trees — so that you are never stranded without crafting materials.
- **A lava source is reachable** within a reasonable distance for portal construction (either a surface pool, a lava lake, or obsidian from the treasure chest provides an alternative).

> [!IMPORTANT]
> MCSR Ranked seeds are **not** random. They are pre-vetted to be fair and completable. Do not panic if your first impression of a seed looks sparse — the guaranteed structures will always be nearby.

## Shipwreck Anatomy

A shipwreck is a partially destroyed wooden ship that generates in ocean and beach biomes. It can appear **upright, tilted on its side, or fully upside-down**, which affects which direction you navigate inside. There are **three possible chest rooms**, but a shipwreck may contain between one and three of them depending on how intact the wreck is.

In MCSR Ranked, the seed guarantee ensures the map chest is always present. The other two chests vary but are usually present in most seeds.

### The Three Chest Types

#### 1. Supply Chest (Bow or Stern — Upper Deck Area)

The **supply chest** is found in the front (bow) or rear (stern) section of the ship, typically near the top of the wreck.

**Common loot:**
- Carrots (often 4–15)
- Potatoes (often 4–12)
- Rotten flesh (2–8)
- Coal (0–8)
- Gunpowder (0–5)
- Pumpkins / suspicious stew (rare)
- Enchanted leather armor (rare)
- Paper (1–5)
- Wheat (1–7)

**Priority:** Medium — grab food and coal, but don't linger if you already have enough.

#### 2. Treasure Chest (Lower Section / Hull)

The **treasure chest** is found deep inside the ship's lower hull. It is the most valuable non-map chest.

**Common loot:**
- Iron ingots (1–5)
- Gold ingots (0–5)
- Emeralds (0–5)
- Iron nuggets (1–10)
- Gold nuggets (1–10)
- Lapis lazuli (0–10)
- Bottle o' Enchanting (0–1)
- Diamonds (rare, 0–1)

**Priority:** High — iron and gold here are critical for crafting and trade prep.

#### 3. Map Chest (Mid-Ship / Cabin Area)

The **map chest** is the most important chest in the entire shipwreck. It is almost always found in a small enclosed room in the middle of the ship.

**Common loot:**
- **Buried Treasure Map** (always present in MCSR Ranked seeds)
- Compass (1)
- Paper (1–10)
- Feather (0–5)
- Book (0–5)
- Clock (0–1)
- Enchanted books (rare)

**Priority:** Highest — getting the map is the entire point of visiting the wreck.

> [!TIP]
> On a tilted or upside-down shipwreck, the room layout is rotated. Pause briefly to get your bearings before swimming through. Use the shape of the wood beams and doorways to navigate — they look the same regardless of orientation.

## Strategy Overview

The shipwreck route follows a clean four-phase structure:

```
Spawn → Shipwreck → Buried Treasure → Portal → Nether
```

1. **Sprint to the shipwreck** as fast as possible from spawn.
2. **Loot the map chest first**, then the treasure chest, then the supply chest (in that order).
3. **Craft essential items** (pickaxe, bucket, flint & steel if possible) using wood from the wreck and iron from the treasure chest.
4. **Follow the treasure map** to the Buried Treasure chest and collect its loot.
5. **Build a Nether Portal** using the lava source you find and the iron bucket you crafted.
6. **Enter the Nether** and proceed to your bastion/fortress route.

The total overworld time for a well-executed shipwreck run is typically **3:30–5:30**, depending on travel distances and loot quality.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

### Step 1 — Finding the Shipwreck

When you spawn, take a quick look around. Shipwrecks spawn in **ocean and beach biomes**, so you are either:

- **Already on a beach** — the shipwreck may be visible immediately or just offshore.
- **Near an ocean** — sprint to the water's edge and look along the coast or into the shallow water.
- **On an island** — the wreck is likely nearby in the surrounding ocean.

**How to locate it quickly:**

- Look for the distinctive **wooden mast or hull** protruding from the water surface. Even mostly-submerged wrecks will have something visible.
- Use **F3** to check your coordinates. Shipwrecks in MCSR Ranked are always close to spawn — rarely more than 200–300 blocks away.
- If you're in a deep ocean, swim along the surface and look down — wrecks in deeper water are still visible from above with good visibility.
- **Do not waste time** searching in the wrong direction. If you run 100 blocks and see nothing, turn around and try the opposite direction toward the ocean.

> [!NOTE]
> Shipwrecks always generate in ocean or beach biomes. If your spawn is in a forest or plains, the ocean and its wreck are at the boundary of that biome. Head directly toward the nearest ocean edge.

---

### Step 2 — Entering and Navigating

Approach the shipwreck and begin your dive. Key navigation tips:

- **Upright wrecks:** Enter through the obvious hole in the hull. The map chest room is usually mid-ship at sea level or just below. The treasure chest is in the lower hull. The supply chest is at the bow or stern.
- **Tilted wrecks:** The ship is rotated, so "down" inside the ship is now sideways. The chest rooms are the same, but you'll be swimming horizontally through what used to be vertical corridors.
- **Upside-down wrecks:** The rarest orientation. Everything is flipped — the "bottom" of the hull is now on top. Navigate carefully and remember the map chest room is still in the central section.

**Efficient navigation:**
- Hold sprint and use **sprint-swimming** (hold Ctrl + W in water, or just hold W after getting the sprint-swimming momentum). This moves you significantly faster than regular swimming.
- Dolphins (if nearby) will give you a **Dolphin's Grace** speed boost. This is a nice bonus but unreliable.
- **Don't bother fighting** any drowned mobs inside or around the wreck. They will deal chip damage and slow you down. Dodge them and keep moving.

---

### Step 3 — Priority Looting Order

The single most important rule of shipwreck routing is:

> **Map Chest → Treasure Chest → Supply Chest**

Here's why this order matters:

1. **Map Chest first:** The Buried Treasure Map is the key to everything. Getting it early means you can already orient yourself toward the treasure location while looting the other chests.
2. **Treasure Chest second:** Iron and gold are your primary crafting materials. After getting the map, grab all the metal from here.
3. **Supply Chest last:** Food is important but less time-critical. You are unlikely to starve in the few minutes it takes to complete this route. Grab food quickly and move on.

**What to grab at each chest:**

| Chest | Take | Leave |
|---|---|---|
| Map Chest | Treasure Map (always), compass | Paper, feathers, books (skip) |
| Treasure Chest | All iron ingots/nuggets, all gold, emeralds | Lapis lazuli (only if space allows) |
| Supply Chest | Carrots, potatoes (10–15 total food), coal | Rotten flesh (only if starving), pumpkins |

> [!WARNING]
> Do **not** take everything from every chest. Inventory management matters. Cluttered inventory wastes crafting time later. Take only what you need.

---

### Step 4 — Getting and Reading the Treasure Map

The **Buried Treasure Map** is a special map item that shows a zoomed-out view of the surrounding ocean/beach area with an **X mark** indicating the buried treasure location.

**How to read the map:**

1. **Hold the map in your hand** (right-click or use off-hand). It will render your current location as a white dot and the treasure as a red X.
2. The map is already partially zoomed in. Your white dot moves as you move.
3. **Navigate toward the X.** When your white dot overlaps the X, you are standing directly on the treasure.
4. The X marks a specific **chunk**, and the treasure chest always generates at the **chunk center** — coordinates ending in **+9, +9** within that chunk (e.g., X=25, Z=9 or X=233, Z=57).

**Key tips for map navigation:**
- Hold the map in your **off-hand** while sprinting so you can see it without stopping.
- Use **F3** coordinates to cross-reference — once you're close, the map dot and X will nearly overlap, and you can use exact coords to pinpoint the dig spot.
- The treasure is **always on a beach or shallow ocean floor**, never inland beyond the coast or in deep ocean.
- Once you think you're in the right spot, **look for a small sand or gravel mound** — sometimes the terrain is slightly raised above the chest, though this is not always visible.

---

### Step 5 — What to Take vs. What to Skip

Being selective with your inventory is a mark of an experienced shipwreck runner. Here is a clear breakdown:

**Always take:**
- Buried Treasure Map
- All iron ingots and iron nuggets (smelt nuggets later if needed, or accumulate 9 for an ingot)
- All gold ingots and gold nuggets
- Carrots and/or potatoes (your primary food source for the overworld)
- Coal (for smelting and torches)
- Emeralds (for future trading if you visit a village)

**Take if space allows:**
- Enchanted books (situational value)
- Lapis lazuli (not usually needed early)
- Diamonds (always take — extremely rare in shipwrecks but game-changing)

**Leave behind:**
- Paper
- Feathers
- Clocks and compasses (beyond the one in the map chest)
- Rotten flesh (unless you have no food at all)
- Pumpkins
- Extra books with no enchantments

---

### Step 6 — Getting Wood from the Wreck

Shipwrecks are made of **wooden planks, logs, and slabs** — this is your early-game wood source. You do not need to find a tree if you harvest the wreck efficiently.

**What to break and why:**
- Break **wooden planks** from the walls and floor of the shipwreck. These drop directly as planks — no crafting needed.
- Break **wooden logs** if you see them — they drop logs which craft into 4 planks each.
- Avoid breaking structural pieces near the chests, as this can make navigation awkward.

**How much wood you need:**

| Item | Wood Cost |
|---|---|
| Crafting Table | 4 planks |
| Wooden Pickaxe | 3 planks + 2 sticks |
| Sticks (×4) | 2 planks |
| Wooden Shovel (for digging) | 1 plank + 2 sticks |
| **Minimum total** | ~12–16 planks |

Grab around **16–24 planks** worth of wood from the shipwreck. This is easily done by breaking 4–6 plank blocks. Do not over-harvest — you're wasting time breaking wood you don't need.

> [!TIP]
> If you spawn near trees on a beach, it is sometimes faster to quickly chop one tree before entering the water, especially if the wreck is mostly submerged and harvesting wood underwater is awkward.

## Buried Treasure

The **Buried Treasure chest** is the payoff for following the map. It is always buried under sand, gravel, or sometimes stone on a beach or in shallow ocean.

### How to Find It

1. Follow the Buried Treasure Map to the X location (see [Step 4](#step-4--getting-and-reading-the-treasure-map)).
2. Once your map dot overlaps the X, you are standing on the treasure.
3. The chest generates at the **top of the terrain** just below the surface, usually 1–3 blocks underground.
4. **Dig straight down** at the X location. Use your shovel for sand and gravel (faster than your pickaxe).
5. If you don't find it within 3–4 blocks, you may be slightly off. Use F3 to check your exact X and Z coordinates — they should end in 9 within the chunk (e.g., X=9, 25, 41, 57, 73, 89... +16 each chunk).

> [!NOTE]
> The treasure chest **always** generates at coordinates where `(X mod 16) == 9` and `(Z mod 16) == 9`. If you know this, you can pinpoint the exact dig spot from F3 without even perfectly aligning the map dot.

### Buried Treasure Loot Table

The buried treasure chest has one of the best early-game loot pools in the game:

| Item | Chance | Quantity |
|---|---|---|
| Heart of the Sea | 100% | 1 |
| Iron Ingot | 100% | 1–4 |
| Gold Ingot | 100% | 1–4 |
| Cooked Cod / Salmon | ~75% | 2–4 |
| Emerald | ~40% | 4–8 |
| Prismarine Crystals | ~35% | 1–5 |
| Iron Sword | ~25% | 1 |
| TNT | ~12% | 1–2 |
| Leather Tunic (Enchanted) | ~10% | 1 |
| Diamond | ~6% | 1–2 |
| Chainmail Armor (Enchanted) | ~5% | 1 |

**What to take from buried treasure:**
- **Heart of the Sea** — always grab this
- **All iron and gold ingots** — add to your existing stack from the shipwreck
- **Food (cooked fish)** — take all of it
- **Emeralds** — take for potential trading
- **TNT** — can be useful for mining quickly in niche situations
- **Diamonds** — always take if present

> [!TIP]
> The cooked cod and salmon from buried treasure are excellent food sources. They restore a decent amount of hunger and saturation, and they require no cooking unlike raw fish.

## Building the Nether Portal

After collecting loot from the shipwreck and buried treasure, your next goal is to build a **Nether Portal** and enter the Nether. You need **10 obsidian** (for a standard 4×5 portal) or you can use the bucket-and-lava method.

### Standard Method — Lava Pool + Water Bucket

The most common portal-building method in MCSR Ranked shipwreck seeds:

1. **Craft an iron bucket** using 3 iron ingots (arrange in a V-shape on the crafting table).
2. **Find a lava source.** After leaving the beach, look for:
   - **Surface lava pools** in plains, savanna, or badlands biomes nearby.
   - **Exposed lava** on the side of mountains or ravines.
   - **Underground lava lakes** — dig down to y=10 or lower if no surface lava is found (less efficient).
3. **Scoop the lava** with your bucket by right-clicking the source block.
4. **Build the portal frame** using a standard obsidian-casting technique:
   - Place water on the ground to create a water source.
   - Pour lava into a mold shape to create obsidian.
   - Use your pickaxe to mine the obsidian and reshape if needed.
   - Alternatively, use the **bucket method** to cast obsidian in-place without mining it.

### Alternative Method — Obsidian from Buried Treasure or Ruined Portal

Sometimes, the route is made even easier by:

- **Ruined portal nearby:** If you spot a ruined portal structure near spawn or near the shipwreck, you may be able to collect pre-placed obsidian and lava from the chest there, combining both seed types.
- **Pre-placed obsidian:** Some MCSR Ranked seeds have a lava pool or ruined portal within a short distance specifically because the seed filter accounts for portal-building viability.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Do **not** skip crafting the iron bucket. The bucket is the most important tool for portal building in this seed type. Prioritize getting 3 iron ingots before anything else once you have the map in hand.

### Minimum Iron Requirements

| Item | Iron Cost |
|---|---|
| Iron Bucket | 3 ingots |
| Iron Pickaxe (optional upgrade) | 3 ingots |
| Flint & Steel | 1 ingot + 1 flint |
| **Minimum for portal entry** | 4 ingots (bucket + F&S) |

You need at least **4 iron ingots** to build and light a portal without any obsidian pre-placed. The shipwreck treasure chest and buried treasure chest combined almost always provide this.

> [!TIP]
> Flint can be obtained by breaking gravel blocks on the beach (each block has a ~10% chance to drop flint). You'll likely break gravel while digging for the treasure chest anyway — keep an eye out for it.

### Essential Items

- [ ] **Iron Bucket** (filled with lava, or ready to collect lava)
- [ ] **Flint & Steel** (to light the portal)
- [ ] **Crafting Table** (keep in inventory until Nether entry)
- [ ] **Iron Pickaxe** (at minimum — stone is acceptable as a fallback)

- [ ] **Gold ingots × 7–9** (for bartering at bastion — ideally 8+ gold for a gold helmet)

## Tips & Tricks

### Navigation

- **Use F3 constantly.** Monitor your X, Y, Z coordinates when swimming to the wreck, reading the map, and digging for treasure. The F3 screen is your best friend in this seed type.
- **Sprint-swim everywhere.** Water movement is slow without Depth Strider. Maintain sprint-swimming (hold Ctrl + W in water, or just hold W after building sprint momentum) to move at maximum speed.
- **Set your respawn point** by crafting and placing a bed if one is available — though this is rarely a priority in competitive runs.

### Efficiency

- **Open all three chests in one dive** if possible. Plan your path through the wreck before entering so you hit all three rooms without backtracking.
- **Don't fight drowned.** They respawn, they do damage, and they slow your route. Dodge around them.
- **Craft your tools immediately** after surfacing from the wreck — before you start swimming to the treasure. This saves time compared to crafting at the buried treasure site.
- **Keep your hotbar clean:** Slot 1 = sword, Slot 2 = pickaxe, Slot 3 = shovel, Slot 4 = bucket, Slot 5 = food. Consistent hotbar layout builds muscle memory.

### Map Reading

- **Hold the map in your off-hand.** You can see it while running without stopping. Check your position every few seconds rather than constantly staring at it.
- **The X on the map does not move.** Only your white dot moves. Navigate to make your dot overlap the X.
- **Use the coastline as a reference.** If the treasure is on a beach, find the beach segment that matches the map's coastline shape — this gives you a rough direction even before the dot lines up.
- **Pre-calculate chunk coordinates.** If you know your chunk (visible in F3 as `Chunk: X Y Z in X Y Z`), you can instantly know the target block is at `(chunkX * 16) + 9` and `(chunkZ * 16) + 9`.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Looting supply chest before map chest** — Always prioritize the map chest. Looting in the wrong order means you swim back through the wreck and waste 15–30 seconds.
- **Not crafting a bucket** — Some players forget to craft an iron bucket before heading to the lava pool, costing them a second trip.
- **Digging in the wrong spot** — If you're off by even 5–6 blocks, you'll miss the buried treasure. Use F3 to confirm coordinates end in 9 within the chunk.
- **Over-looting** — Taking every item from every chest fills your inventory with junk and costs time. Be selective.
- **Forgetting flint** — You can't light the portal without flint & steel (or a fire charge). Watch for gravel drops while digging.
- **Skipping wood harvest** — Some players exit the wreck without grabbing planks, then have to backtrack to a tree. Get your wood while you're already inside the wreck.

### Advanced Tricks

- **TNT dig:** If you get TNT from buried treasure and have flint & steel, you can use TNT to excavate quickly or clear terrain around the lava pool area.
- **Dolphin pathing:** If there are dolphins near the shipwreck, swim near them to get Dolphin's Grace. This boost is significant and can save several seconds of swimming time.
- **No-pickup looting:** If you know your inventory is getting full, crouch and open the chest in bulk-move mode (Shift+Click each item) to transfer only what you want without accidentally picking up clutter.
- **Portal placement optimization:** Build your portal on the beach rather than inland — beach biomes are usually at a low Y level, meaning your Nether spawn will be closer to bastion/fortress Y levels, minimizing vertical travel in the Nether.

## Quick Reference Summary

```
1. Spawn → Sprint to shipwreck (beach/ocean, usually within 200 blocks)
2. Dive → Map Chest (get Buried Treasure Map)
3. Dive → Treasure Chest (get iron + gold)
4. Dive → Supply Chest (get food + coal)
5. Surface → Break wood planks from the wreck
6. Craft: Crafting Table → Pickaxe → Bucket → Flint & Steel
7. Read map → Sprint/swim to X location
8. Dig at X (chunk center: coords mod 16 = 9)
9. Loot buried treasure (iron, gold, food, emeralds)
10. Find lava source → Cast obsidian → Build portal
11. Light portal → Enter Nether
```
