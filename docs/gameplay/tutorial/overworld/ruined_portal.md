## Introduction

**Ruined Portals** are naturally generated structures in Minecraft that consist of a partially-built Nether Portal frame, a loot chest, and a surrounding pile of netherrack, magma, and blackstone. They were added in Java Edition 1.16 and have become a core element of MCSR Ranked routing for intermediate and advanced players.

Unlike village seeds — which give you a straightforward path to iron tools, food, and bed materials — ruined portal seeds demand **adaptability**. The portal will never be fully complete when you find it; you must assess what blocks are present, what the chest contains, and then decide how to efficiently finish the frame and light it. This additional decision-making layer is why ruined portal seeds are deliberately withheld from lower Elo brackets.

### Why Ruined Portals are Considered Advanced

- The portal frame is **always partially destroyed** — you must evaluate and respond to what you find.
- You may need to perform a **lava cast** to create obsidian blocks on the spot.
- The chest loot is **randomized within a specific loot table**, so you cannot always predict what you will get.
- **Crying obsidian** — which looks similar to regular obsidian — is scattered throughout and will waste your time if you try to use it.
- Optimal play requires practice at recognizing portal layouts quickly and executing frame completions efficiently.

### Advantages Over Other Seed Types

Despite their complexity, ruined portal seeds offer significant advantages:

- You **skip the iron-mining phase** almost entirely — the chest gives you gold tools/armor and often iron.
- You are **immediately adjacent to a partial Nether Portal**, saving travel time.
- With a good chest, you can enter the Nether faster than almost any other seed type.
- Gold pickaxe from the chest can mine obsidian (slowly), or you can use a lava cast to avoid needing a pickaxe at all.

## Ruined Portal Types

Ruined portals come in two broad categories based on their **vertical placement**: above-ground and underground. Both can appear in most biomes.

### Above-Ground Ruined Portals

- The most common type in MCSR Ranked ruined portal seeds.
- Visible from a distance — look for a cluster of **blackstone, netherrack, and magma blocks** rising from the terrain.
- The partial portal frame stands upright and is immediately accessible.
- Usually the fastest type to complete because you do not need to dig.
- **Identifying features:** Dark irregular stone formation on the surface, often with magma blocks glowing orange underneath.

### Underground / Buried Ruined Portals

- Spawns partially or fully underground, requiring you to dig down to access it.
- Indicated on the surface by a **patch of netherrack and/or gravel** sitting at ground level.
- Slightly slower to reach — you need to dig in and find the chest.
- Can occasionally be faster if the portal frame is more complete than average.
- **Identifying features:** Flat netherrack patch at ground level, sometimes with a visible corner of blackstone poking through.

> [!TIP]
> When you first load into a ruined portal seed, immediately look around 360 degrees for the characteristic dark-stone formation. It is almost always visible from spawn without moving more than a few blocks.

### Size Variants

Ruined portals also vary in **physical size**:

- **Small portals** — The most common. Standard 4×5 Nether Portal frame size (interior 2×3).
- **Large portals** — Rare in MCSR Ranked seeds. A bigger frame requiring up to 14 obsidian blocks total for a complete portal. These are generally slower and should be handled with lava-casting.

For MCSR Ranked, you will almost always encounter standard-size portals.

## Portal Frame Analysis

A complete, standard Nether Portal frame requires exactly **10 non-corner obsidian blocks** for a valid portal. The frame is a 4-wide × 5-tall structure, meaning you need obsidian at:

- **2 blocks on the bottom** (floor row, excluding corners)
- **2 blocks on the top** (ceiling row, excluding corners)
- **3 blocks on each side** (left and right columns, 3 each = 6 total)

This gives **10 obsidian blocks minimum** for a valid portal (without corners). Corners are optional for the portal to activate — this is called a **cornerless portal** and is fully valid in MCSR Ranked.

> [!NOTE]
> You only need the **10 non-corner blocks** to light a valid portal. Filling all 14 positions (including corners) wastes time and obsidian. Always build cornerless portals in speedrunning.

### Pre-Placed Obsidian

When you arrive at the ruined portal, some obsidian blocks will already be in the frame. The rest will be missing. The number of pre-placed blocks is **random per seed**:

| Pre-placed Obsidian | Blocks Still Needed | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| 8–10 | 0–2 | Easy — minimal work needed |
| 5–7 | 3–5 | Moderate — lava cast or use chest obsidian |
| 3–4 | 6–7 | Hard — significant lava casting required |
| 1–2 | 8–9 | Very Hard — nearly a full lava cast needed |

### Materials That Can Complete the Frame

The only valid block for a Nether Portal frame is **regular obsidian**. Your sources are:

1. **Existing obsidian in the frame** — already placed; do not touch these.
2. **Obsidian from the chest** — occasionally the chest contains 1–4 obsidian blocks.
3. **Lava casting** — pour water onto lava source blocks to create obsidian in place.
4. **Mining existing obsidian** — requires a diamond pickaxe, or uses a gold pickaxe (~9 seconds per block). Generally avoided.

> [!WARNING]
> **Crying obsidian cannot be used in a portal frame.** It will not activate the portal no matter how it is placed. See the [Crying Obsidian](#crying-obsidian) section for full details.

## The Ruined Portal Chest

Every ruined portal structure includes **exactly one chest**, typically buried under or beside the portal frame. Opening this chest is one of the first things you should do when you arrive.

### Guaranteed Loot (Always Present)

- **Gold items** — nuggets, ingots, a gold pickaxe, gold sword, gold armor pieces, or gold blocks. You will always get at least one gold item.
- **Flint and steel OR fire charge** — one of these two is always present for lighting the portal.

### Common Loot Table

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Gold Pickaxe | Can mine obsidian (~9s/block) |
| Gold Sword | Minor combat help |
| Gold Helmet / Armor pieces | Wear for piglin neutrality in the Nether |
| Iron Nuggets | 9 nuggets = 1 iron ingot |
| Iron Ingots | Directly useful for crafting |
| Flint | Can craft flint and steel with an iron ingot |
| Fire Charge | Alternative portal lighter |
| Obsidian (1–4 blocks) | Huge bonus — reduces or eliminates lava casting |
| Crying Obsidian | Cannot be used in frame — decorative only |
| Gold Blocks | Can craft into 9 ingots each |
| Golden Apple | Minor healing |
| Enchanted Gold Items | Occasionally present |

> [!TIP]
> Always open the chest **before** you start working on the portal frame. Knowing whether you have obsidian in the chest changes your entire strategy — it might mean you need no lava casting at all.

### Priority Loot (In Order)

1. **Flint and steel / fire charge** — Confirms your portal-lighting method.
2. **Obsidian blocks** — Reduces lava casting required.
3. **Gold pickaxe** — Lets you mine obsidian in a pinch.
4. **Iron ingots / nuggets** — For later crafting.
5. **Gold armor** — Wear the best pieces for Nether survival and piglin safety.

## Crying Obsidian

**Crying obsidian** is a purple-hued block that appears scattered throughout ruined portal structures. It has a distinctive **glowing purple particle effect** dripping from it and is one of the most common sources of confusion for new runners.

### Key Properties

| Property | Regular Obsidian | Crying Obsidian |
|---|---|---|
| **Portal frame validity** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| **Color** | Dark black-purple | Purple with glowing drips |
| **Blast resistance** | 1200 | 1200 |
| **Mining time (iron pick)** | ~15 seconds | ~15 seconds |
| **Drops when mined** | Itself | Itself |
| **Used in portal** | ✅ | ❌ |

### Why It Matters in Speedrunning

Crying obsidian is one of the most common mistakes for players new to ruined portal seeds:

- It **looks like regular obsidian** from a distance, especially when you are moving quickly.
- If you count it as a valid portal block, you will **miscalculate** how many blocks you still need.
- If crying obsidian occupies a non-corner frame slot, the portal simply will **not light**.

### How to Identify Crying Obsidian

- Look for the **purple/magenta glowing particle drips** falling from the block.
- It has a slightly **more purple hue** than regular obsidian.
- It is most often placed as decorative or accent blocks around the portal structure, though it can occasionally end up in a frame slot.

### What to Do With Crying Obsidian

- **If it is not in a frame slot:** Ignore it completely. It does not affect your portal.
- **If it occupies a non-corner frame slot:** Mine it out and replace with regular obsidian via lava cast or chest inventory.
- **Never waste time** trying to incorporate crying obsidian into your portal frame.

> [!CAUTION]
> Do NOT mine crying obsidian unless it is directly blocking a frame slot you need. Mining takes ~15 seconds and gives you a crying obsidian block — useless for your portal. Every second counts in a ranked run.

## Step-by-Step Strategy

### Step 1: Arrive and Assess (5–15 seconds)

When you spawn or travel to the ruined portal:

1. **Locate the structure** — Find the dark stone mass above ground, or the netherrack patch for buried portals.
2. **Identify the portal frame** — Find the upright rectangular frame structure.
3. **Count the existing obsidian blocks** in valid (non-corner) frame positions.
4. **Identify crying obsidian** — Note any purple dripping blocks near or within the frame.
5. **Look for lava pools** — Check if lava source blocks are nearby.

> [!NOTE]
> Ruined portals always generate with some magma blocks and often with lava nearby. This lava is critical for lava casting if needed.

---

### Step 2: Loot the Chest (10–20 seconds)

Before doing anything else with the portal:

1. **Find the chest** — It is always nearby, often buried under netherrack at the base of the portal.
2. **Open it and take everything.**
3. **Identify your key items:**
   - Do you have obsidian? How many blocks?
   - Do you have flint and steel or fire charge?
   - Do you have a gold pickaxe?
4. **Equip relevant gold armor** — Gold armor makes piglins neutral toward you in the Nether.

---

### Step 3: Plan Your Completion Strategy

Based on your count of existing obsidian (in frame) plus chest obsidian:

**Scenario A — Enough obsidian (10+ total available):**
Fill gaps directly from inventory. Light portal. This is the fastest possible outcome.

**Scenario B — Slightly short (need 1–4 more blocks):**
Use chest obsidian first. Lava cast any remaining gaps. Light portal.

**Scenario C — Many blocks missing (need 5+ more blocks):**
Lava casting is your primary method. Locate lava, acquire water, execute casts, then light portal.

---

### Step 4: Lava Cast Completion Technique

The **lava cast** is the core skill of ruined portal speedrunning. Here is how to execute it for portal frame completion:

#### What You Need

- A **water bucket** (craft with 3 iron ingots at a crafting table, or find one in a structure)
- **Lava source blocks** at or near the portal

#### Basic Lava Cast Method

1. **Find the lava** — Usually present directly beneath or beside the ruined portal.
2. **Position yourself** above or beside the empty frame slot where you need obsidian.
3. **Place water** so it flows onto the lava source block at the frame position.
4. **Water converts lava source → obsidian** instantly.
5. **Pick up the water** — right-click with your empty bucket to retrieve it.
6. **Repeat** for each missing block.

#### Key Rules for Lava Casting

- Always target **lava source blocks**, not flowing lava. Flowing lava + water = stone, not obsidian.
- Work **top-to-bottom** when filling a column — gravity helps guide water flow.
- Use **temporary blocks** (netherrack, dirt) as scaffolding to reach upper frame slots.
- With one bucket you can alternate between collecting and placing lava to do a full cast.

> [!TIP]
> Practice the lava cast in creative mode. Set up a partial portal with lava in empty slots and practice filling them with a water bucket. Target under 30 seconds for a 5-block lava cast.

> [!WARNING]
> Control your water carefully. Uncontrolled water flow can flood the area, destroy your working surface, or push you into lava.

#### When No Lava Is Adjacent to the Frame

If the lava pool is not immediately at the portal:

1. Use a bucket to collect a lava source block and carry it to the frame position.
2. Place the lava source in the empty frame slot.
3. Pour water onto it to convert it to obsidian.
4. Retrieve your water bucket.
5. Repeat for remaining slots.

---

### Step 5: Handle Crying Obsidian in Frame Positions

If any crying obsidian blocks are occupying non-corner frame slots:

1. **Mine them out** — Requires at minimum an iron or gold pickaxe. Wooden and stone picks cannot mine obsidian-tier blocks.
2. **Replace** with regular obsidian via lava cast or from chest inventory.
3. If crying obsidian is only in **corner positions** — leave it alone. Corners are optional.

> [!NOTE]
> Corners of the portal frame (4 diagonal positions) are **not required** for the portal to activate. If crying obsidian is only in corners, you can ignore it entirely and light the portal without touching those blocks.

---

### Step 6: Light the Portal

Once all 10 non-corner frame slots are filled with regular obsidian:

1. **Equip your flint and steel** (from the chest, guaranteed) or hold a fire charge.
2. **Right-click any interior air block** within the portal frame.
3. The portal activates and turns purple immediately if the frame is valid.
4. If it **does not light**, the frame is incomplete — check for missing blocks or crying obsidian in frame positions.

#### Crafting Flint and Steel (If Needed)

Recipe: **1 iron ingot + 1 flint** at a crafting table.
- Flint: break gravel blocks (10% drop per block; use a shovel for speed).
- Iron ingot: from the chest, a nearby cave, or craft 9 iron nuggets together.

## Portal Completion Practice

Ruined portal completion is a **distinct sub-skill** that requires dedicated practice, separate from your general Minecraft mechanics knowledge. Many players who are competent at other aspects of speedrunning struggle with ruined portals until they have spent specific time practicing this.

### What to Practice

1. **Rapid frame assessment** — Train yourself to count existing obsidian blocks and identify crying obsidian within 3–5 seconds of seeing the portal.
2. **Lava casting accuracy** — Place water in exactly the right position to convert lava to obsidian in a specific frame slot without flooding the area.
3. **Multi-block lava casting** — Practice filling 3–5 frame slots efficiently in sequence.
4. **Chest routing** — Practice opening the chest, reading inventory, and deciding your strategy before closing the chest.

### Recommended Practice Routine

1. Open Minecraft in **creative mode** or a dedicated practice world.
2. Build a partially complete Nether Portal frame — remove 4–6 random non-corner blocks.
3. Place lava source blocks in each empty frame slot.
4. Give yourself a water bucket.
5. Practice converting each lava source to obsidian and lighting the portal.
6. **Time yourself.** Aim for under 30 seconds for a 5-block lava cast, under 60 seconds for a full 10-block cast.

### Watching Top Runners

Watch MCSR Ranked VODs from experienced runners specifically at their ruined portal sections. When they arrive, pause and observe:

- How quickly they assess the frame (count + crying obsidian check).
- Whether they go to the chest or frame first.
- Their water placement precision during lava casting.
- How they handle non-standard portal layouts.

## Common Portal Configurations

### Configuration 1: 8–10 Existing Blocks + Chest Has Obsidian

**Situation:** Frame is nearly complete. Chest gave you extra obsidian blocks.

**Strategy:** Place chest obsidian in the gaps. Light portal immediately.

**Time cost:** Very low — 10–20 seconds of frame work.

---

### Configuration 2: 6–7 Existing Blocks, No Chest Obsidian

**Situation:** Need 3–4 more obsidian. Chest did not provide any.

**Strategy:** Find nearby lava (almost always present). Perform 3–4 lava casts. Light portal.

**Time cost:** Moderate — 20–40 seconds of frame work.

---

### Configuration 3: 4–5 Existing Blocks + Crying Obsidian in Frame Slots

**Situation:** Many blocks missing AND some crying obsidian occupies frame positions.

**Strategy:** Mine crying obsidian from frame positions using gold pickaxe. Lava cast all remaining empty slots. Light portal.

**Time cost:** High — 40–60+ seconds. Consider whether to concede depending on run timer.

---

### Configuration 4: Many Blocks Missing, No Adjacent Lava

**Situation:** Frame is mostly destroyed. No lava pool is directly at the portal.

**Strategy:** Collect a lava source with a bucket and bring it to the frame. Convert with water bucket. Repeat for all missing blocks.

**Time cost:** Very high. Assess run viability based on your current Elo situation.

---

### Configuration 5: 9–10 Existing Blocks, Excellent Chest Loot

**Situation:** Nearly complete frame AND excellent chest loot (gold armor, obsidian, fire source).

**Strategy:** Fill 0–1 missing blocks (trivial). Light portal immediately. Enter Nether fully equipped.

**Time cost:** Minimal — this is the dream ruined portal seed scenario.

## Inventory Checklist

Check that you have the following before stepping through the portal:

### Essential

- [ ] **Flint and steel** or fire charge — for re-lighting the portal if it gets extinguished in the Nether
- [ ] **Food** — enough to survive the Nether (any food source will do)
- [ ] **Wood / crafting table** — for crafting during the rest of the run
- [ ] **At least 1 bucket** — for future lava/water needs

### Strongly Recommended

- [ ] **Gold armor (at least one piece)** — makes piglins neutral in the Nether
- [ ] **Sword** — any sword for basic combat
- [ ] **Pickaxe** — at least iron, for Nether mining
- [ ] **Torches** — visibility in the Nether
- [ ] **Beds (3+)** — for the End dragon fight
- [ ] **Blocks for bridging** — dirt, cobblestone, gravel

### From the Chest (Use What You Got)

- [ ] Gold pickaxe — useful for mining Nether gold ore
- [ ] Any gold armor pieces — wear them all
- [ ] Iron nuggets — keep for later crafting

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Do not enter the Nether without food. Ruined portal seeds can leave you with no food if you skipped looting nearby structures. Take 30 seconds to gather apples from trees or berries from berry bushes if needed.

## Tips & Tricks

### Speed Tips

- **Do not mine anything you do not need to.** Every second spent mining is a second lost. Only mine crying obsidian if it is in a frame slot.
- **Loot the chest before analyzing the portal** if the chest is more accessible — but count frame blocks while walking toward it.
- **Count obsidian blocks while approaching** — you can often estimate the count from 10+ blocks away as you run in.
- **Pre-position your water** — before placing lava, think about where you will stand and exactly where the water will flow.
- **Use surrounding netherrack** as free scaffolding when filling upper frame slots.

### Resource Tips

- **Save your flint and steel** — use it only for the portal and re-lighting if extinguished.
- **Keep all iron nuggets** — 9 nuggets = 1 ingot. These can be crucial for crafting a bucket or iron pickaxe later in the run.
- **Always equip gold armor** from the chest. Piglins will not attack you in the Nether as long as you have at least one gold piece equipped.
- **Check for a nearby village** — if your seed has both a ruined portal and a village, consider whether a quick detour for beds and food is worth the time.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Is Bad | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Treating crying obsidian as a usable frame block | Portal will not light | Learn to identify crying obsidian by its purple dripping particles |
| Lava casting too aggressively (flooding the portal) | Destroys your work surface, wastes time | Practice controlled, deliberate water placement |
| Forgetting to pick up water after casting | You lose your bucket | Always right-click with empty bucket to retrieve water |
| Not wearing gold armor before entering the Nether | Piglins attack you instantly | Equip gold chest/helmet from the chest loot |
| Mining obsidian with a gold pickaxe without lava cast option | Each block takes ~9 seconds | Only mine obsidian as a last resort; prefer lava casting |
| Entering the Nether without food | You will starve during Nether navigation | Always gather food before entering |
| Not having a crafting table | Cannot craft anything mid-run | Always carry one |
| Wasting obsidian on corner slots | Corners are not needed; you waste materials | Build cornerless portals always |

### Advanced Optimization

- **"Pre-fill" analysis:** Count blocks while running toward the portal so you arrive already knowing your strategy — no hesitation needed.
- **Lava source vs. flowing lava:** Always convert **source** blocks (cube-shaped, not flowing) — flowing lava + water = stone, not obsidian.
- **Single-bucket technique:** Alternate between collecting lava and pouring water with one bucket to complete a full cast without needing two separate buckets.
- **Gold armor priority:** Helmet > Boots > Leggings > Chestplate for best protection-per-slot coverage. But always wear **at least one** piece.
- **Bed sourcing:** Ruined portal seeds do not give you beds. Plan your bed acquisition before entering the Nether — nearby sheep for wool, or a village, is ideal.

### Mental Approach

Ruined portal seeds reward **calm assessment** over frantic action. The players who lose the most time are those who immediately start breaking blocks or pouring water without evaluating the situation first. Take 3–5 seconds to fully read the portal before touching anything — this almost always saves more time than it costs.

> [!TIP]
> If you are struggling with ruined portal seeds at 600–1199 Elo, do not be discouraged. This is the bracket where these seeds first appear, and everyone goes through a learning curve. Focus on three habits first: **(1)** correctly identifying crying obsidian, **(2)** mastering one clean lava cast, and **(3)** always looting the chest before touching the frame. These three habits alone will handle the vast majority of situations you encounter.
