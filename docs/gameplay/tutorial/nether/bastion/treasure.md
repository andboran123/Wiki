# Treasure Bastion — Complete Guide

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Treasure Bastion is the **hardest** of the four bastion types. It features the most Piglin Brutes, the most complex geometry, and the most dangerous entry points. However, it also offers the **highest loot ceiling** in the game, including Netherite Scrap and a dedicated treasure chest room. Master this type and no other bastion will intimidate you.

## Introduction

The **Treasure Bastion** is instantly recognizable by its iconic **stepped pyramid** shape. Unlike the other three bastion types — which have a more horizontal, campus-like spread — the Treasure Bastion rises dramatically upward in tiered levels, resembling an ancient temple or Aztec pyramid constructed from Blackstone.

What makes it unique in speedrunning:

- It contains a **dedicated Treasure Room** at the center-bottom of the pyramid, filled with a large chest containing extraordinary loot
- It has **more Piglin Brutes** than any other bastion type — up to 4–6 depending on the seed
- It has **no ramparts** (the tall balcony structures seen on other types), making it visually distinct
- The **loot ceiling is the highest in the game**: the treasure chest can contain Netherite Scrap, Ancient Debris, Gold Blocks, and Diamond Horse Armor — items that can dramatically accelerate a run
- It is surrounded by a **lava moat**, making falling extremely punishing

Despite its difficulty, experienced runners often prefer Treasure Bastion because a good run there can save a minute or more compared to slower bastions, thanks to the volume of gold and the quality of the treasure chest.

## Visual Identification

Identifying a Treasure Bastion quickly is a critical skill. Here's what to look for:

### From the Air (Elytra/High Altitude)

- **Pyramid/stepped structure** — the bastion clearly rises in tiers from a wide base to a narrow top
- **No ramparts** — there are no protruding balcony-like extensions at the sides
- **Magma block cap** — the topmost tier is crowned with magma blocks (you can see their orange glow even at distance in low-light conditions)
- **Lava moat** — a ring of lava surrounds the structure at the base level, visible as an orange glow beneath the blackstone
- The structure is **taller than wide** compared to Housing or Stables bastions

### From a Distance (Ground Level)

- The stepped tiers create a staircase silhouette on each side
- The pyramid rises sharply — you can often see multiple Z-levels of blackstone stacked
- The lava moat creates a distinctive orange border at ground level
- No horizontal bridge structure is visible (ruling out Bridge type)
- No three-pronged rampart cluster (ruling out Stables)

### Quick Mental Model

```
        [ MAGMA ]        <- Top tier, narrow
       [  ████  ]
      [ ██████   ]
     [  ████████ ]
    [ ██████████  ]      <- Base tier, widest
   [  LAVA MOAT   ]      <- Surrounding the base
```

> [!TIP]
> The **magma block top** is your most reliable visual cue from a distance. No other bastion type has magma blocks prominently on its exterior crown. Look for the orange tint at the structure's highest point.

## Structure Breakdown

Understanding the Treasure Bastion's layout before you enter prevents disorientation and wasted time.

### The Pyramid Tiers

The Treasure Bastion has **3–4 distinct tiers**, each progressively smaller:

- **Bottom Tier (Base)**: The widest level, surrounded by the lava moat. Contains the main entrance archways, most of the exterior gold blocks, and the highest concentration of Piglins.
- **Middle Tier(s)**: One or two intermediate levels accessed via internal staircases or exterior ledges. Piglin Brutes often patrol here.
- **Top Tier**: Small platform at the summit, crowned with magma blocks. Fewer mobs here but important for navigation.
- **Sub-Level (Treasure Room)**: Below the main pyramid, accessible via a staircase descending from the interior. This is the most important room.

### The Treasure Room Location

The treasure room is located **beneath the central pyramid**, accessible by finding the interior staircase and descending. It is a dedicated chamber with:
- High blackstone walls
- A large chest (or double chest) in the center or against the back wall
- Potential magma block flooring
- Usually 1–2 Piglin Brutes guarding the entrance or interior

### Mob Density

| Mob | Count (Approx.) | Location |
|---|---|---|
| Piglins | 8–15 | All tiers, exterior |
| Piglin Brutes | 4–6 | Middle tiers, treasure room entrance, interior |
| Zombified Piglins | 0–3 | Exterior, random |

> [!WARNING]
> The **Piglin Brute density** in Treasure Bastion is the highest of any type. Do not assume a corridor is safe — always have an escape plan before rounding corners.

## Pre-Entry Checklist

Before you fly/bridge/descend into a Treasure Bastion, verify ALL of the following:

- [ ] **Gold Helmet** is equipped (minimum gold armor piece)
- [ ] **Lava Bucket** is in hotbar slot 1–3 for fast access
- [ ] **Food** stocked — at least 8 cooked food items (Piglin Brutes hit hard)
- [ ] **Pickaxe** ready — Iron or better, Fortune III if possible
- [ ] **Sword** in hotbar — if you plan to fight brutes
- [ ] **Bow + Arrows** — if you plan to pillar-shoot brutes
- [ ] **Shield** — highly recommended for brute encounters
- [ ] **Blocks** — at least 16–32 for pillaring or bridging
- [ ] **Escape route** mentally planned before entry

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Never enter a Treasure Bastion without a Lava Bucket. The lava moat can be used offensively against Piglin Brutes, and the bucket is essential for clearing mobs from the treasure room if you get cornered.

## The Boomer Route — Step by Step

The **Boomer Route** is the standard optimized route for Treasure Bastion. It is named after the speedrunner who popularized it. It prioritizes loot efficiency and minimizes unnecessary Piglin Brute encounters.

> [!NOTE]
> This route assumes you are approaching the Treasure Bastion from above (the standard approach in any% runs where you enter the Nether via a ruined portal or village portal near the bastion). Adjust step 1 if your approach is from ground level.

---

### Step 1 — Approach from a Safe Angle

- Approach the Treasure Bastion from **above and from one of the four diagonal corners**, not directly from a cardinal side
- Diagonal approach reduces the number of Piglin Brutes with line-of-sight to you on entry
- **Do NOT land on the magma block top** — the magma will deal continuous damage and alert nearby mobs
- Target a landing spot on the **upper-middle tier ledge**, away from the magma crown

### Step 2 — Survey the Upper Tiers

- Once on the upper tier ledge, take 2–3 seconds to look around
- Identify the positions of any **visible Piglin Brutes** — note if they are patrolling or stationary
- Look downward to locate the **interior staircase entrance** (usually visible as a dark opening on the middle tier)
- Do NOT look directly at Endermen if any are present

### Step 3 — Navigate to the Staircase Entrance

- Move carefully along the outer ledge of the middle tier
- **Hug the inner wall** to reduce sight lines with Piglin Brutes on lower tiers
- The staircase down to the treasure room is typically found on the **interior face** of the middle tier — it appears as a descending corridor or opening in the blackstone floor/wall
- If a Piglin Brute is blocking the staircase entrance, see [Piglin Brute Management](#piglin-brute-management) below

### Step 4 — Descend Toward the Treasure Room

- Enter the staircase and descend carefully — do not sprint-fall as you may land in lava
- The staircase may have 1–2 Piglin Brutes patrolling it — be prepared to sprint past them if they are facing away, or pillar up to avoid them
- Listen for footstep sounds — this tells you if a Brute is moving toward you in the staircase

### Step 5 — Clear the Treasure Room Entrance

- At the bottom of the staircase, stop before entering the treasure room
- Peek around the corner (using Minecraft's natural camera lean, or crouch + peek)
- Identify Piglin Brutes guarding the room
- **Strategy A**: If brutes are facing away, sprint past them into the room
- **Strategy B**: Pillar up to 3 blocks in the staircase, let brutes come to you, and critical-hit them as they approach (jump + hit for 1.5× damage)
- **Strategy C**: Lure brutes up the staircase and place lava at the bottom to kill them while you stay safe above

### Step 6 — Enter and Loot the Treasure Room

- Once brutes are dealt with or bypassed, sprint directly to the **large chest** at the back/center of the treasure room
- Open it immediately and take **everything** — you can sort later
- Check for a **second chest** (sometimes there is a double chest arrangement)
- Look for **gold blocks** on the walls or floor of the treasure room — mine these immediately

### Step 7 — Collect the Treasure Room Gold Blocks

- The treasure room often has **2–4 gold blocks** in its walls or on a small pedestal structure
- Mine all of them with your pickaxe (Fortune III nets 9 ingots each)
- Don't miss blocks behind/beside the chest — rotate your camera fully

### Step 8 — Ascend and Mine Exterior Gold Blocks

- Return up the staircase and exit to the exterior of the pyramid
- Work your way around the **base tier exterior**, mining all visible gold blocks
- Gold blocks are often embedded in the **wall faces** of the pyramid tiers — look carefully, they blend in with Polished Blackstone Bricks
- Common gold block locations:
  - Corners of the base tier
  - Center of each wall face on the base tier
  - Occasional blocks on the middle tier exterior
- Total exterior gold blocks: typically **6–10**

### Step 9 — Locate a Safe Barter Area

- Find a flat area **outside or at the base of the pyramid**, away from Piglin Brutes
- Dig a **barter hole**: a 2×1 pit that is 2–3 blocks deep
- This traps Piglins and allows mass bartering

### Step 10 — Corral Piglins

- Walk near groups of Piglins to attract their attention
- Lead them to the barter hole — they will fall in as they walk toward you
- Aim for **5–8 Piglins** in the hole for maximum efficiency

### Step 11 — Barter

- Stand at the edge of the barter hole
- Right-click rapidly with gold ingots — distribute ingots to all Piglins in the hole simultaneously by moving your cursor between them
- Throw all gold ingots that you don't need for crafting (keep 5 for the gold helmet if you haven't made it yet)
- Wait for all Piglins to finish their inspection animations (~8 seconds)
- Collect all drops from the hole

### Step 12 — Count Pearls

- Check your Ender Pearl count
- **Target: 12 Ender Pearls minimum** (enough for the stronghold)
- If below 12: check if you have more gold ingots to barter, or if there are uncollected gold blocks
- If at or above 12: prepare to exit the bastion

### Step 13 — Final Sweep

- Quickly scan the exterior for any **missed gold blocks**
- Check the **treasure chest again** if you came up short on loot (you may have missed a chest)
- Collect any valuable non-pearl barter drops (Obsidian, Enchanted iron boots, etc.)

### Step 14 — Exit Safely

- Identify your exit direction (toward Nether Fortress)
- **Do NOT sprint blindly past Piglin Brutes** on exit — they will follow and damage you
- Exit via the opposite side of the bastion from where brutes are congregating
- Build a nether portal or use your existing portal coordinates to proceed

## The Treasure Room

The treasure room is the defining feature of this bastion type and why experienced runners often prefer it despite the difficulty.

### What to Expect in the Chest

The treasure room large chest pulls from an exclusive high-tier loot table:

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Netherite Scrap** | Extremely rare, but possible — equivalent to 2 ancient debris |
| **Gold Block** | Usually 2–5 blocks |
| **Gold Ingot** | Usually 3–9 ingots |
| **Diamond Horse Armor** | High value trade/collect |
| **Diamond** | 1–2 diamonds occasionally |
| **Iron** | Iron ingots/blocks |
| **Enchanted Golden Apple** | Rare, but occasionally present |
| **Saddle** | Common filler |
| **Ancient Debris** | Extremely rare but technically possible |

> [!TIP]
> Even a "bad" treasure room chest (mostly gold and iron) is still very efficient because you're receiving those resources without having to mine them. Always open it — it's the whole point of the route.

### Room Layout

- The treasure room is typically **7×7 to 9×9 blocks** in floor area
- **Ceiling height**: 3–4 blocks
- The central feature is the large chest, often on a raised blackstone platform
- **Magma blocks** may be present on the floor — watch your footing
- Gold blocks are sometimes **embedded in the walls** on either side of the chest

## Piglin Brute Management

Piglin Brutes are the primary threat in Treasure Bastion. Understanding their mechanics is essential.

### Stats

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| Health | 50 HP (25 hearts) |
| Damage (Normal) | ~7.5 hearts per hit |
| Damage (Hard Mode) | ~11.5 hearts per hit |
| Armor | None |
| Movement Speed | Fast |
| Aggro Trigger | Sight (regardless of gold armor) |
| Weapon | Golden Axe |

### Strategy 1 — Avoidance (Recommended for Beginners)

- **Observe patrol routes** before moving
- Brutes follow a set patrol path — wait for them to face away, then sprint past
- Never fight a brute unless you have full health and a shield
- Time savings: Avoidance is almost always faster than fighting

### Strategy 2 — Critical Hit Combo (Best Offensive Strategy)

- To critical hit in Minecraft Java Edition: **jump and attack at the peak of the jump**
- Critical hits deal **1.5× base weapon damage**
- With a Diamond Sword (7 base damage), a critical hit deals ~10.5 damage
- It takes approximately **5 critical hits** to kill a Piglin Brute with a Diamond Sword
- Sprint-attack for extra damage and knockback if you have the sprint momentum
- **Tip**: Hit from an elevated position (1 block above the brute) for easier critical timing

### Strategy 3 — Pillar + Shoot

- Place 3 blocks beneath yourself to pillar up above the brute's reach
- Shoot with a **bow at full charge** for maximum damage
- Charged bow shots deal ~9 base damage; **5–6 shots** to kill a brute
- This is safest but costs arrows and time
- Ideal when a brute is alone and blocking a mandatory corridor

### Strategy 4 — Lava Bucket

- If a brute is stationary in a small space, place lava above/next to it
- Lava kills a Piglin Brute in ~3–4 seconds
- **Caution**: Lava placed in a corridor will persist and may block your path — plan placement carefully
- Also destroys any item drops from the brute, so don't use near chests or gold

> [!WARNING]
> Never fight two Piglin Brutes simultaneously without a shield and full health. Two brutes can kill a player with full iron armor in 3–4 hits combined. Always separate them before engaging.

## Gold Block Mining

Gold blocks are the primary source of bartering fuel. Knowing exactly where they are saves precious seconds.

### Treasure Bastion Gold Block Locations

1. **Treasure Room walls**: 2–4 blocks embedded in the side walls of the room
2. **Treasure Room floor/pedestal**: Occasional blocks beneath or around the chest platform
3. **Base tier exterior corners**: Typically 1–2 blocks at each corner of the pyramid base (4 corners = up to 8 blocks)
4. **Base tier wall faces**: 1–2 blocks embedded at the center of each cardinal face
5. **Middle tier ledge**: Occasional scattered blocks on the ledge walkway

### Mining Priority

```
Priority 1: Treasure room gold blocks (you're already there)
Priority 2: Base tier exterior corners (highest concentration)
Priority 3: Base tier wall faces
Priority 4: Middle tier scattered blocks
```

### Fortune III Reminder

| Pickaxe | Gold per Block |
|---|---|
| No Fortune | 7–9 ingots |
| Fortune I | 7–9 ingots (avg. higher) |
| Fortune II | 7–9 ingots (avg. higher) |
| Fortune III | Always 9 ingots |

> [!TIP]
> If you have a Fortune III pickaxe, 9 gold blocks = 81 ingots = 81 barter attempts. At 4.73% pearl rate, that gives an **expected 3.83 pearl trades**, yielding ~15–30 Ender Pearls. Fortune III essentially guarantees your pearl goal from a well-stocked Treasure Bastion.

## Common Mistakes

These are the most frequent errors made by runners in Treasure Bastion:

1. **Landing on the magma top**: Taking continuous damage from magma hurts your health before the run really starts. Always avoid the top.

2. **Rushing the treasure room entrance without checking for brutes**: Walking straight into a brute's axe swing is a run-ender.

3. **Forgetting to mine treasure room gold blocks**: Players focus on the chest and forget the 2–4 gold blocks on the walls. This can mean 18–36 fewer ingots.

4. **Using the lava bucket carelessly near chests**: Lava destroys item drops and chest contents. Never place lava inside the treasure room itself.

5. **Not corralling before bartering**: Standing in the open and bartering one Piglin at a time is 5x slower than a proper corral setup.

6. **Leaving the bastion before checking pearl count**: Always count before you leave. Walking away with 9 pearls when you needed 12 costs a full fortress trip.

7. **Aggroing Piglins accidentally**: Breaking blocks near Piglins (other than Gilded Blackstone) will aggro them. Be careful which blocks you mine.

8. **Forgetting gold armor**: Arriving without a gold piece equipped means all Piglins are immediately hostile. You'll spend the entire run being attacked.

9. **Falling into the lava moat**: Sprint-jumping around the pyramid exterior without watching your footing. The lava moat is deep and lethal.

10. **Fighting every single Piglin Brute**: Brutes are time sinks. Only fight them when they absolutely block your mandatory path.

## Tips & Tricks

### Navigation

- **Use sound**: Piglin Brutes make unique grunting sounds when aggro'd. If you hear one you haven't seen, stop moving and locate it before proceeding.
- **Crouch in corridors**: Crouching prevents you from falling off edges and lets you peek around corners safely.
- **Remember that lava glow is visible through gaps**: The orange glow helps you locate lava moat openings and interior hazards.

### Efficiency

- **Pre-dig the barter hole** while Piglins are wandering — set it up BEFORE you go to the treasure room so it's ready when you return.
- **Sprint everywhere safe**: Every second counts. Only slow down near Piglin Brutes.
- **Batch your gold mining**: Mine all gold blocks in one continuous sweep rather than running back and forth.

### Combat

- **Sprint-critical hits**: Sprint toward a Piglin Brute, jump at the last second, and swing. The sprint provides extra knockback + the jump provides critical hit bonus — maximum damage per hit.
- **Use the staircase choke point**: If brutes are chasing you, retreat up the staircase. They bunch up in single-file and you can critical-hit them one at a time from above.

### Pearl Strategy

- **Throw gold in clusters**: Don't throw one ingot and wait. Throw 5–8 ingots to all corralled Piglins at once, then wait for all resolutions simultaneously.
- **Don't stop at 12**: If you have more gold after getting 12 pearls, keep bartering. Extra pearls mean more margin for error during Stronghold navigation.

> [!TIP]
> A great Treasure Bastion run (sub 2:00 from entry to exit with good loot) is one of the highest-skill achievements in early MCSR routing. Don't be discouraged by early runs — it takes 50+ attempts to get comfortable here.
