## Introduction

The **Hoglin Stables Bastion** is the only bastion type with **three ramparts** instead of two. This is its defining identifier and the first thing you should look for when approaching any bastion. Beyond the rampart count, Stables is characterized by its wide, **open layout** — much more spread out and horizontal than Treasure Bastion, and with far less enclosed interior space than Housing Bastion.

True to its name, the Stables bastion features:
- **Fenced enclosures** (the "stables") where Hoglins spawn and roam
- **Hay bales** scattered throughout (a surprisingly useful resource — more on that below)
- A **flatter, more walkable terrain** compared to other types
- Consistent **gold block clusters** beneath and around the stable pens

Despite its medium difficulty rating, many experienced runners develop a strong preference for Stables because:
- The gold blocks are **predictable in location** once you learn the layout
- The **open layout** means fewer blind corners and less risk of surprise Brute encounters
- Hoglin management, while important, is very learnable with a lava bucket
- The triple chest rampart at Y=72 is relatively easy to access once identified

The main challenge is the **Hoglin knockback**, which in combination with elevated ledges and lava below creates genuine danger. Mastering Hoglin avoidance is the key skill for this bastion type.

## Visual Identification

### From Above

- **Count the ramparts**: Stables has **THREE** visible ramparts protruding from the main body. This is the single most reliable identifier. No other bastion type has three ramparts.
- **Wide, flat layout**: The bastion spreads horizontally. It looks lower and wider than Treasure, and more uniform than Housing.
- **Fences visible**: From directly above, you can often see the **fence posts** of the hoglin pens — a grid of fence blocks forming enclosure outlines.
- **Hoglins moving**: Hoglins are large pink pig-like mobs and are visible from above, wandering within fenced areas.

### From a Distance

- The three-pronged rampart arrangement is visible from most angles — look for **three distinct elevated tower shapes** on the bastion body
- The structure tends to be **shorter in profile** than Treasure Bastion
- Unlike Bridge Bastion, there is no characteristic long walkway/bridge structure visible
- Unlike Housing Bastion, the ramparts are **not adjacent to each other** — they are spaced around the bastion body

### The Three Rampart Arrangement

In Stables, the three ramparts are not all in a line. Their arrangement varies slightly by seed, but typically they form an L-shape or triangular pattern around the bastion's upper body. From above:
```
[Rampart 1]
      [Rampart 2]
[Rampart 3]
```
The spacing between them is clearly noticeable — they do not touch each other.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Three ramparts = Hoglin Stables.** There are no exceptions. If you count three distinct rampart structures, you are at Stables Bastion every single time. This is the most reliable single-type identifier in the game.

## The Triple Rampart Identifier

While all three ramparts exist in Stables, they are **not equal**. Like other bastion types, Stables has:
- **One triple chest rampart** (1 single chest + 1 double chest)
- **Two single chest ramparts**

Your route is built around efficiently looting the triple chest rampart first, then the gold blocks, then the remaining single ramparts if time allows.

### Identifying the Triple Chest Rampart

The triple chest rampart in Stables is the **largest** of the three ramparts. Key identification methods:

1. **From above**: The triple chest rampart has a slightly larger footprint than the single chest ramparts
2. **Y-coordinate**: The triple chest rampart in Stables is located at approximately **Y=72** — the chests are accessible from roughly this height
3. **Block count**: The triple chest rampart has a noticeably taller or more built-up appearance
4. **Break a block and peek**: At head height on the rampart exterior, break one block and look inside — if you see chests, you've found the chest level

> [!NOTE]
> The Y=72 height for the triple chest rampart in Stables is slightly **lower** than Housing Bastion's triple chest rampart (Y=73). This 1-block difference, combined with the chest spacing difference (2 blocks between chests in Stables vs. 3 blocks in Housing), helps differentiate the types when you're uncertain.

## Triple Chest Rampart Details — Y=72

Knowing the precise layout of the triple chest rampart saves exploration time:

### Chest Configuration

- **Top level chest**: A single chest accessible from the top platform of the rampart
- **Lower level chests**: A double chest (two chests side by side) on the lower tier of the rampart, approximately **2 blocks below** the top single chest
- **Gap between chests**: The two chests in the double chest are separated by **2 blocks of wall** (compare to Housing's 3-block gap)

### Accessing the Triple Chest Rampart

```
[Top Platform] <- Single chest here
      |
  2 blocks down
      |
[Lower Level]  <- Double chest here (2-block gap between them)
```

- You can often access both levels by: (1) opening the top chest, (2) crouching off the edge and dropping one level, (3) finding the double chest
- Some seeds have a small internal staircase or ledge connecting the levels

### Loot in the Triple Chest Rampart

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Gold ingots | 4–15 per chest |
| Iron ingots/blocks | Common |
| Iron tools/armor | Occasionally enchanted |
| Spectral arrows | Common filler |
| Crossbows | Occasionally enchanted |
| String, saddles, leather | Common filler |
| Netherite Scrap | Very rare but possible |

## The Stables Area

The "stables" themselves — the fenced hoglin enclosures — occupy a significant portion of the bastion's lower interior. Understanding this space helps you navigate efficiently.

### Structure

- **Fenced pens**: Multiple rectangular areas enclosed by wooden fences and fence gates
- **Hay bales**: Scattered throughout the pens on the ground and occasionally on ledges
- **Crimson planks flooring**: The floor of the stables area uses crimson planks, giving it a distinctive reddish appearance
- **Hoglins**: Multiple Hoglins (typically 3–8 per seed) roam freely inside the pens and occasionally outside them
- **Open top**: Unlike the enclosed interiors of Housing or Treasure, the stables area is largely open to the Nether sky above

### Hay Bales — Underrated Resource

Hay bales in the stables area are craftable into **9 Wheat each**. In a speedrun context, the more immediate use is that hay bales act as **soft landing platforms** — they reduce fall damage by 80%. If you need to drop from a high point and your health is low, aim for a hay bale stack below you.

### Hoglin Behavior in the Stables

Hoglins in the stables behave like regular Hoglins with one important modifier: they are already in a fenced area that can act as natural containment. However, they can still aggro you through fence gaps and will charge if you enter their pen.

- Hoglins have a **large aggro radius** — expect them to charge whenever you enter the pens
- **Warped Fungus** repels Hoglins if you have any in your inventory/hand
- Hoglins will not follow you through Nether portals

## Route Strategy — Triple-to-Gap Route

The **Triple-to-Gap Route** is the standard Stables route. It prioritizes the triple chest rampart, then the gold blocks below the main structure (in the "gap" beneath the stables deck), then bartering. The "Gap" refers to the open sub-structure area beneath the stables where many gold blocks are concentrated.

### Pre-Entry

- Gold armor equipped ✅
- Lava bucket in hotbar slot 1 or 2 ✅
- Identify the triple chest rampart from above before landing ✅
- Note Hoglin positions — are they near your intended landing zone? ✅

---

### Step 1 — Approach and Identify

- Circle the bastion at altitude to count ramparts and identify the triple chest rampart (usually the largest)
- Plan your landing spot: top of the triple chest rampart if accessible, or the bastion roof near it

### Step 2 — Land and Loot the Triple Chest Rampart

- Land on the triple chest rampart top
- Open the single chest at the top and take all items
- Drop down to the lower level (controlled drop — use Shift to decelerate mid-air)
- Open both chests of the double chest
- Take everything

### Step 3 — Mine Rampart Gold Blocks

- Mine all visible gold blocks on the triple chest rampart (walls, floor, ceiling)
- Typical yield: 1–3 gold blocks here

### Step 4 — Navigate to the Bastion Roof

- Climb or jump back to the main bastion roof area
- Scan for Piglin Brutes — identify their positions before moving

### Step 5 — Loot Single Ramparts (if time permits)

- Visit the two single-chest ramparts if accessible from the roof
- Open each single chest and collect loot
- Mine any gold blocks on these ramparts
- Skip this step if the single ramparts are far away or blocked by Brutes — the time cost may not be worth it

### Step 6 — Descend to the Stables Body

- Move from the bastion roof down into the interior/body of the stables
- **This is where Hoglin danger is highest**: be alert as soon as you enter the stables area
- Move quickly but not recklessly — watch for Hoglin charge animations

### Step 7 — Manage Hoglins

- If Hoglins are in your path, use your **lava bucket** to clear them, or hop onto fence tops
- See the [Hoglin Management](#hoglin-management) section below

### Step 8 — Mine Gold Blocks Beneath the Structure

- The primary gold block concentration in Stables is **below and around the main stable pens**
- Look for gold blocks embedded in:
  - Walls beneath the elevated floor of the stables
  - Support pillars of the structure
  - Floor level of the sub-structure
- This "gap" area below the stables deck is often the richest gold mining zone

### Step 9 — Set Up the Barter Hole

- Find a flat, open area away from Hoglins and Brutes
- The area **outside the stables pens** at ground level is usually ideal
- Dig your barter hole (2×1, 2–3 blocks deep)
- Piglins wandering outside the stables area will be your barter partners — corral 5–8 of them

### Step 10 — Corral and Barter

- Lead Piglins to the barter hole
- Barter all available gold ingots
- Collect and count Ender Pearls

### Step 11 — Exit

- Count pearls — if 12+, exit immediately
- If short: check for any missed gold blocks or continue bartering if ingots remain
- Exit in the direction of the Nether Fortress

## Hoglin Management

Hoglins are the unique mob threat of Stables Bastion. Understanding them thoroughly is what separates competent Stables runners from beginners.

### Hoglin Stats

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| Health | 40 HP (20 hearts) |
| Damage per hit | ~2.5–5 hearts depending on difficulty |
| **Knockback** | Very high — can send you flying 5–10 blocks |
| Aggro trigger | Entering their territory / getting within ~6 blocks |
| Deterrent | Warped Fungus |

### Why Hoglins Are Dangerous in Stables

The knockback is the real danger. In a normal open area, Hoglin knockback is annoying. In Stables Bastion, it is **lethal** because:
- You are often on elevated ledges with **no railings**
- The bastion floor below is frequently **lava** or has lava adjacent
- Being knocked back even 3 blocks sideways can send you off a rampart or ledge
- Multiple Hoglins can chain-knockback you before you can recover

### Strategy 1 — Lava Bucket (Best Strategy)

- Keep the lava bucket in your **hotbar slot 1**
- When Hoglins aggro and charge, place lava in **front of them** as they approach
- Back up after placing — the lava will damage them as they walk through it
- Hoglins die from lava in ~3–4 seconds
- **Do NOT place lava** near: gold blocks you haven't mined yet, chest rooms, or anywhere you need to stand soon

### Strategy 2 — Fence Hopping

- Fence tops are exactly **1.5 blocks tall** — Hoglins cannot jump fences
- Hop onto a fence top to temporarily escape a charging Hoglin
- From the fence top, you can pillar up further or plan your next move safely

### Strategy 3 — Warped Fungus

- If you have **Warped Fungus** in your inventory or hand, Hoglins will flee from you
- Warped Fungus can be found in Warped Forest biomes
- Hold it in your hand and Hoglins will run away completely
- This is biome-dependent but extremely powerful when available

### Strategy 4 — Outrun and Ignore

- Hoglins are not particularly fast runners
- If a Hoglin aggros but you're in an open area, **sprint away** and put distance between you
- Hoglins will de-aggro after losing sight/range
- This is viable in the wide-open ground level of Stables where there's room to run

### Strategy 5 — Fight (Not Recommended)

- You CAN kill Hoglins with a sword — 40 HP means about 5 hits with a Diamond Sword
- However, **fighting wastes time** and risks getting knocked off ledges during the combat
- Only fight Hoglins if they are directly blocking your mandatory path AND you have full health

> [!WARNING]
> **Never let a Hoglin hit you while you're on the edge of a ledge or rampart.** Even 1 knockback hit can be fatal if the ledge drops into lava. Always position yourself against a solid wall when Hoglins are nearby on elevated ground.

## Gold Block Locations

### Stables Bastion Gold Block Map

| Location | Approx. Blocks | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Triple chest rampart walls/floor | 1–3 | High |
| Sub-structure support pillars | 2–4 | Very High |
| Below-deck walls and alcoves | 3–5 | Very High |
| Exterior bastion walls | 2–4 | Medium |
| Single chest rampart walls | 1–2 each | Low |

### Where to Look

1. **Below the main stables floor deck**: The sub-structure beneath the hoglin pens is the gold-richest area. You access it by descending from the pens level or from outside the bastion at the lower elevation.
2. **Support pillars and arches**: Blackstone pillar structures often have gold blocks embedded in them at various heights — check all 4 sides of each pillar.
3. **Exterior walls at mid-height**: Walk around the outside perimeter at Y=50–60 and look for gold block faces embedded in the blackstone.

> [!TIP]
> In Stables, the gold blocks are often **not visible from above**. They're hidden in the sub-structure below the stables deck. This is why descending into the "gap" area is critical — skipping it means leaving 30–50 gold ingots behind.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Not counting ramparts**: Mistaking Stables for Bridge (2 ramparts) or Housing (2 adjacent ramparts). Count carefully — THREE is the number.

2. **Ignoring Hoglins until they knockback you off a ledge**: Hoglins don't announce themselves loudly. Always check their positions before standing near ledge edges.

3. **Skipping the sub-structure gold**: The biggest gold loss in Stables runs is leaving without mining the gold blocks beneath the stables deck. This can mean 40–50 fewer ingots.

4. **Placing lava in the barter area**: If you use the lava bucket near your planned barter zone, you'll destroy Piglin drops. Place lava only where it won't interfere with bartering.

5. **Trying to fight Hoglins one at a time with a sword**: Multiple Hoglins chain their knockback. What looks like a safe 1v1 fight can turn into a 1v3 chain-knockback situation quickly.

6. **Not using the fence tops**: Fence hopping is an underutilized technique in Stables. It's free safety — use it whenever Hoglins are chasing you.

7. **Confusing Stables with Housing**: Both have complex layouts. The definitive check: Stables has THREE total ramparts; Housing has TWO adjacent ramparts.

8. **Bartering too close to Hoglins**: Hoglins near your barter hole will disrupt your corralled Piglins. Establish your barter area away from the stables pens.

## Tips & Tricks

### Approach

- **Always approach from above** and circle once to count ramparts. Stables identification should take under 2 seconds once you've practiced.
- Land on the **triple rampart top** directly — don't waste time landing on the main roof and walking over.

### Hoglin Counter-Intuition

- **Make noise intentionally in safe areas** to pre-discover Hoglin locations before you're on a ledge. Step into the stables area briefly to see where they are, then retreat, before committing to your route through the pens.
- **The fences are your friends**: Hoglin AI does not jump fences. Once you're on the other side of a fence from a Hoglin, you are safe.

### Gold Efficiency

- **Fortune III is more valuable in Stables** than most types because of the high gold block count in the sub-structure. If your run has Fortune III available, prioritize using it at Stables.
- **Don't forget hay bales** can soften falls — if you need to drop from a high point and are low on health, land on a hay bale stack if one is below you.

### Bartering

- Stables often has a **high Piglin density at ground level** around the bastion exterior — excellent for corralling large numbers quickly.
- The flat Nether ground outside the stables (if the bastion is at ground level) is the ideal barter spot — easy to dig a hole and plenty of Piglins wandering nearby.

> [!TIP]
> Hoglin Stables is a fantastic "middle ground" bastion for intermediate runners. It has more depth and danger than Bridge but is more structured and predictable than Treasure. Learning Stables properly opens up many competitive routing options.
