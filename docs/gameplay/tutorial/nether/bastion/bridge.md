# Bridge Bastion — Complete Guide

> [!TIP]
> Bridge Bastion is widely considered the **most beginner-friendly** bastion type. Its gold blocks are often exposed on the exterior, its layout is intuitive, and its Piglin Brute count is lower than Treasure or Housing. If you're new to MCSR Ranked, learning Bridge first is the recommended approach.

## Introduction

The **Bridge Bastion** is named for the long, exposed walkway that spans the structure's midsection — a bridge of blackstone extending between the bastion's main body and its outer rampart system. This open, elevated architecture makes it the most visually distinctive and one of the easiest bastions to navigate.

Key characteristics that make Bridge Bastion beginner-friendly:

- **Exposed gold blocks** on the exterior walls — easy to locate and mine without venturing deep inside
- **Lower Piglin Brute count** than Treasure or Housing — typically 2–4 total
- **Straightforward chest access** — rampart chests are accessible from above or the side without threading through dangerous interiors
- **Two well-defined routes** (DK and Cracksmoker) that cover every common rampart configuration
- **Clear barter setup area** — flat sections of the bridge deck make excellent corralling spots

The biggest decision you'll make at Bridge Bastion is which **route to use**, and that decision is entirely determined by which **rampart type** you have and which side it's on. This guide walks you through both the identification and the routing decision.

## Visual Identification

### The KEY Identifier: Separated Ramparts

The single most important visual feature of Bridge Bastion is that its **two ramparts are SEPARATED** — there is a visible gap of open air between them. This is what distinguishes it from Housing Bastion, which has two ramparts that are adjacent and touching.

```
BRIDGE BASTION RAMPARTS:
  [Rampart A]    [Rampart B]
       ^               ^
     GAP between them (open air)

HOUSING BASTION RAMPARTS:
  [Rampart A][Rampart B]
       ^
     TOUCHING (no gap)
```

If you see two ramparts with a clear gap between them, you are at a Bridge Bastion.

### Additional Visual Cues

- **The Bridge itself**: A long horizontal walkway or platform spans between the main body and the rampart(s), visible from above as a flat blackstone pathway extending outward
- **Open architecture**: Bridge Bastions feel more "open" than Housing Bastions — there are fewer enclosed rooms and more exposed ledges
- **Height profile**: Bridge Bastions are generally not as tall as Treasure Bastions; they spread horizontally rather than vertically
- **Underbridge area**: Below the main bridge deck, there is often an open void or Nether ceiling structure visible — useful for dropping down during certain route variations

### From the Air

1. Approach the bastion from above
2. Count the ramparts — if you see **2**:
   - Look for a gap between them → **Bridge**
   - Look for them touching → **Housing**
3. Confirm by looking for the characteristic bridge walkway extending from the main body

> [!TIP]
> Practice the gap vs. touching distinction. Look at both ramparts simultaneously and ask: "Could I build a 1-block pillar in the space between them?" If yes → Bridge. If no (they share a wall) → Housing.

## The Rampart System Explained

**Ramparts** are the elevated, tower-like structures that extend out from the main bastion body. In Bridge Bastion, there are exactly **two ramparts**, and they are always separated by a gap.

### What Ramparts Contain

Ramparts are the primary chest-bearing structures in Bridge Bastion. They contain:
- Either a **single chest** (single chest rampart) or a **triple chest** arrangement (1 single + 1 double chest)
- Gold blocks embedded in their walls and floor
- Piglins and occasionally Piglin Brutes patrolling the top

### How to Spot Ramparts from a Distance

- Ramparts appear as **taller, narrower towers** extending from the sides of the bastion body
- They typically rise **2–3 blocks higher** than the surrounding bastion roof
- Their tops are usually **flat or near-flat platforms** accessible from the bastion roof level
- From above, they appear as rectangular protrusions on the bastion's footprint

### Rampart Orientation — Left vs. Right

When approaching Bridge Bastion, the two ramparts are on **opposite sides** of the bridge structure. One will be on your left, one on your right (relative to your approach angle). Determining which side the **triple chest rampart** is on determines your route choice.

> [!NOTE]
> "Left" and "right" in route descriptions refer to **your left/right as you face the bastion from the standard approach angle**, which is usually from outside looking toward the main bridge. This can vary by seed, so always double-check which rampart has more chests before committing to a route.

## Triple vs. Single Chest Rampart

This is the most important decision point in Bridge Bastion routing. The two ramparts are **not equal** — one has more chests than the other.

### Triple Chest Rampart (Primary Target)

- Contains **3 chests total**: one single chest + one double chest (2-wide)
- The double chest is typically on a lower level of the rampart, accessible by breaking 1 block or looking through a gap
- **Much more loot** than a single chest rampart
- This rampart is your **primary loot target** — your route is planned around reaching it efficiently

**What's in the triple rampart chests:**
- Gold ingots (4–12)
- Iron ingots/blocks
- Iron sword/armor (occasionally enchanted)
- Spectral arrows
- Saddle
- Occasionally Netherite Scrap (rare)

### Single Chest Rampart (Secondary Target)

- Contains **1 chest** only
- Less loot, accessed the same way as the triple but worth visiting if time allows
- Usually looted last, after the triple rampart and gold blocks

### How to Check Which Rampart is Which

1. Approach the bastion and **gain a high vantage point** (pillar up 2–3 blocks if needed)
2. Look down onto each rampart top
3. The **triple chest rampart** will have a slightly larger or more elaborate structure — you can sometimes see the chest tops from directly above
4. **Break one block** at head level on the rampart exterior and peek in — if you see 2 chests side by side (a double chest), you've found the triple rampart

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Route selection depends entirely on the triple chest rampart's position.** If the triple is on the LEFT → use the **DK Route**. If the triple is on the RIGHT → use the **Cracksmoker Route**. Getting this wrong and running the wrong route wastes 15–30 seconds.

## The Two Main Routes

### Donkey Kong (DK) Route

**Use when:** The triple chest rampart is on the **left side** (or the terrain near the left rampart is buried/lower, requiring a different approach).

The DK Route is named after the classic arcade game due to the up-down-up movement pattern it requires — you climb, descend, and climb again in a manner reminiscent of navigating Donkey Kong's platforms.

#### When to Use DK Route
- Triple chest rampart is on the **left** side
- The left rampart is partially buried by Nether terrain (requiring you to mine in from above)
- When the standard approach naturally brings you to the left side first

#### DK Route — Step by Step (12 Steps)

**Step 1 — Approach and Land**
- Approach from above and land on the **main bastion roof** (the flat area between the two ramparts)
- Identify which rampart is triple (left) and which is single (right)
- Check for Piglin Brutes on the roof — note their positions

**Step 2 — Cross to the Left Rampart**
- Walk across the bastion roof toward the left rampart
- Crouch near edges to avoid falling
- If a Brute is patrolling the left side, time your crossing for when it faces away

**Step 3 — Access the Left Rampart Top**
- Step onto the top of the left rampart
- Quickly open the **single chest** on the top (the top-level chest in a triple rampart setup)
- Loot everything — don't sort now, just take it all

**Step 4 — Descend to the Lower Chest Level**
- Drop down one level on the left rampart (the double chest is on the lower tier)
- There is usually a small interior section or ledge — look for the double chest
- If needed, break 1 block to access the chest area

**Step 5 — Loot the Double Chest**
- Open both sides of the double chest and collect everything
- You now have all 3 chests looted from the triple rampart

**Step 6 — Mine Left Rampart Gold Blocks**
- Before leaving the left rampart, mine any visible **gold blocks** on its walls and floor
- Typically 1–3 gold blocks per rampart

**Step 7 — Return to the Roof**
- Pillar or climb back up to the main bastion roof

**Step 8 — Mine Roof and Exterior Gold Blocks**
- The main bastion body often has gold blocks embedded in its exterior walls
- Work your way around the bastion perimeter, mining all visible gold blocks
- Key spots: corners, central wall faces, the area beneath the bridge walkway

**Step 9 — Visit the Right (Single) Rampart**
- If time allows, drop down to the right (single chest) rampart
- Open the single chest and collect loot
- Mine gold blocks on this rampart too

**Step 10 — Descend to the Bridge Deck Level**
- Drop down to the main bridge walkway level (the actual "bridge" part of Bridge Bastion)
- This is typically a large flat area with good space for a barter hole

**Step 11 — Dig Barter Hole and Corral**
- Dig a 2×1 hole 2–3 blocks deep in a flat section of the bridge deck
- Walk near groups of Piglins to attract them; lead them to the hole
- Wait for 5–8 Piglins to fall in

**Step 12 — Barter and Exit**
- Right-click rapidly with gold ingots on all Piglins in the hole
- Collect drops when animations complete
- Count Ender Pearls — target 12+
- Exit the bastion in the direction of the Nether Fortress

---

### Cracksmoker (CS) Route

**Use when:** The triple chest rampart is on the **right side**.

The Cracksmoker Route is a more direct, linear approach that takes advantage of the right-side rampart's typical accessibility from a specific entry angle.

#### When to Use CS Route
- Triple chest rampart is on the **right** side
- This is arguably the faster route when applicable because the movement is more linear

#### CS Route — Step by Step (10 Steps)

**Step 1 — Approach from the Right Side**
- Instead of landing on the center roof, approach and land **directly above or beside the right rampart**
- This is the key difference from DK — you aim for the right rampart immediately rather than crossing the roof first
- Saves the roof-crossing time

**Step 2 — Land on Right Rampart Top**
- Touch down directly on the top of the right (triple chest) rampart
- Immediately scan for Piglin Brutes — there may be 1 patrolling the rampart top

**Step 3 — Open the Top Chest**
- Open the single chest at the top of the rampart
- Take all items

**Step 4 — Check Brute Position and Descend**
- Look down into the rampart's lower level — is a Brute there?
- If clear: drop down immediately
- If a Brute is present: pillar up 1 block, wait for it to walk away from directly below, then drop

**Step 5 — Loot the Double Chest**
- Access the double chest on the lower rampart level
- Take everything from both chest slots

**Step 6 — Mine Right Rampart Gold Blocks**
- Mine all gold blocks visible on the right rampart (walls, floor, ceiling)

**Step 7 — Bridge Across to the Main Body**
- Move from the right rampart across the bridge walkway to the main bastion body
- Watch your footing — the bridge may have gaps or irregular edges

**Step 8 — Mine Main Body and Left Rampart Gold Blocks**
- Work around the bastion exterior mining gold blocks
- Drop to the left (single chest) rampart if time permits to grab that chest
- Focus on the highest density gold block areas: corners and central wall faces

**Step 9 — Set Up Barter Hole**
- Find a flat section of the bridge deck or base area
- Dig barter hole, corral Piglins as described above

**Step 10 — Barter and Exit**
- Barter all available gold ingots
- Collect drops, count pearls
- Exit toward Fortress

## Gold Block Locations in Bridge

Gold blocks in Bridge Bastion are among the most accessible of any type — many are **on the exterior walls** and don't require entering dangerous interiors.

### Location Map

| Location | Gold Blocks (Approx.) | Accessibility |
|---|---|---|
| Left Rampart walls/floor | 1–3 | Easy |
| Right Rampart walls/floor | 1–3 | Easy |
| Main body exterior walls | 3–6 | Easy |
| Below bridge walkway | 1–3 | Moderate (need to drop down) |
| Bridge deck inlays | 0–2 | Easy |

### Mining Priority

```
Priority 1: Triple rampart (you're already there)
Priority 2: Main body exterior walls (most concentrated)
Priority 3: Single rampart
Priority 4: Under bridge (only if time allows)
```

> [!TIP]
> In Bridge Bastion, the **exterior walls are usually rich enough** that you don't need to go deep inside the structure at all. This is why Bridge is so beginner-friendly — you can get a great gold haul while mostly staying in the open air.

## Trading Hole Setup

Bridge Bastion offers several excellent locations for a barter hole due to its flat, open deck areas.

### Best Barter Locations

1. **The Main Bridge Deck**: The wide walkway connecting the main body to the rampart(s) is flat and usually has plenty of Piglins wandering on it. Dig your hole here.
2. **The Base Courtyard**: If there is a flat area at the base of the bridge pillars, this is also a strong barter location — often densely populated with Piglins.
3. **Avoid the Rampart Tops**: Too narrow and dangerous with potential Brutes nearby.

### Setup Steps

1. Find your chosen flat area
2. Dig a **2 blocks wide × 1 block long** hole, **2–3 blocks deep**
3. The hole needs to be deep enough that Piglins can't hop out
4. Walk circles near wandering Piglins to attract them
5. Lead them to the hole — they will follow and fall in
6. Barter as described in the Overview page

> [!WARNING]
> **Don't dig your barter hole directly under Piglins standing on elevated edges** — they may fall into lava instead of your hole. Choose a location with solid ground beneath.

## Common Mistakes

### Route Selection Errors

- **Choosing DK when you should use CS (or vice versa)**: Always verify triple chest rampart position BEFORE committing to a route. If you're unsure, take 3 seconds to count chests from above.
- **Not knowing the routes at all**: Using an improvised path in Bridge Bastion is much slower than following DK or CS properly. Memorize both routes before playing ranked.

### Positional Errors

- **Falling off the bridge**: The main bridge walkway has no railings. Sprint carefully, especially near edges. Crouching before dropping is essential.
- **Dropping too far during descent**: Overshooting a rampart drop and landing in open air. Practice controlled drops (release jump mid-air, hold Shift to slow yourself).

### Mob Errors

- **Accidentally aggroing a Piglin Brute while mining**: Know which blocks you're targeting. Mining Gilded Blackstone does not aggro Piglins, but reckless block-breaking near them can cause confusion.
- **Failing to verify Brute position before entering rampart lower levels**: Always peek before dropping into the lower rampart section.

### Loot Errors

- **Visiting the single chest rampart first**: Always prioritize the TRIPLE chest rampart. The single chest rampart is secondary.
- **Missing the double chest in the triple rampart**: Beginners often only find the top (single) chest and leave without finding the double chest below. Always descend into the rampart after the top chest.

## Tips & Tricks

### Approach Angle

- **Approach the triple chest rampart directly** — especially for CS Route. You save 3–5 seconds of roof-crossing time.
- **Scout from above before landing**: Circle the bastion at altitude for 2–3 seconds to count chests and identify rampart types before committing to a landing spot.

### Buried vs. Exposed Rampart

- Occasionally, one rampart will be partially **buried in Nether terrain** (surrounded by Netherrack on one or more sides). This changes how you access it.
- For a buried triple rampart (DK Route situation): you may need to **mine through 1–2 blocks of Netherrack** to access the lower chest. Have a spare pickaxe ready.
- For an exposed triple rampart (CS Route): straightforward drop-in access from the top.

### Rampart Recognition Speed

- With practice, you should be able to identify your rampart configuration (triple left, triple right, or both single) within **1–2 seconds** of getting a clear aerial view.
- Practice this in non-ranked games: just enter Nether, find bastions, and categorize them as fast as possible.

### Miscellaneous

- **Keep gold helmet equipped throughout**: Easy to forget during combat or inventory management.
- **Don't waste time fighting non-blocking Brutes**: If a Brute is not between you and your objective, ignore it completely.
- **Collect iron from barter even if you have a lot**: Iron nuggets from trading can be smelted/combined for tools and are never wasted.

> [!TIP]
> Bridge Bastion is a great type to run in practice mode to drill your barter timing. The open environment lets you focus on the bartering mechanics without as much survival pressure — perfect for beginners building confidence.
