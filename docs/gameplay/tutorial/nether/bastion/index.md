## What Is a Bastion Remnant?

A **Bastion Remnant** is a large Nether structure made primarily of **Blackstone**, **Polished Blackstone Bricks**, and **Gilded Blackstone**. Bastions spawn in all Nether biomes **except** Basalt Deltas, and they are one of the two major Nether structures alongside Nether Fortresses.

Bastions are inhabited by:
- **Piglins** — neutral mobs that barter with gold ingots when not provoked
- **Piglin Brutes** — always-hostile guards that will attack regardless of armor
- **Hoglins** (Stables type only) — hostile pig-like mobs that deal significant knockback

Every bastion contains **gold blocks** embedded in its structure and **chests** filled with valuable loot. Crucially, bastions contain **Ender Pearls** via the Piglin bartering mechanic, making them a core component of nearly every competitive speedrun route.

## Why Bastions Matter in Speedrunning

In MCSR Ranked (and Any% Glitchless runs broadly), the goal is to reach and defeat the Ender Dragon as fast as possible. Two resources gatekeep End access:

- **Blaze Rods** (for Blaze Powder → Eyes of Ender) — obtained at the Nether Fortress
- **Ender Pearls** (combined with Blaze Powder to make Eyes of Ender) — obtained via **Piglin bartering** in bastions

Because Ender Pearls are rare drops from Endermen and not reliably farmable in the Overworld during a speedrun, **bastions are the primary source of Ender Pearls**. Runners aim to collect enough gold (from the bastion's gold blocks and chests) to barter with Piglins until they accumulate **12 Ender Pearls** (enough to make 12 Eyes of Ender for the stronghold portal).

Beyond Ender Pearls, bastions also provide:
- **Iron ingots / iron nuggets** — useful for shield, tools, or trade padding
- **Netherite Scrap / Ancient Debris** — rare but possible in Treasure bastion
- **Obsidian** — useful for emergency portals or end platform
- **Gold ingots** — currency for bartering and crafting
- **Enchanted Books, Saddles, String** — lower priority but occasionally helpful

## The Four Bastion Types — Overview Table

There are exactly **four** distinct Bastion Remnant types in Minecraft. Each has a unique layout, difficulty profile, and routing strategy.

| Type | Difficulty | Key Feature | Primary Route | Approx. Gold Block Yield | Brute Count |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Treasure** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Hard | Stepped pyramid, highest loot, most brutes | Boomer Route | 8–12 blocks | 4–6 |
| **Bridge** | ⭐ Easy | Separated rampart, exposed gold, beginner-friendly | DK / Cracksmoker Route | 8–12 blocks | 2–4 |
| **Hoglin Stables** | ⭐⭐⭐ Medium | Three ramparts, open layout, Hoglin danger | Triple-to-Gap Route | 6–10 blocks | 2–4 |
| **Housing** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Hard | Two adjacent ramparts, maze-like interior | Top-Down / Manhunt Route | 6–10 blocks | 2–4 |

> [!NOTE]
> Gold block yield varies based on RNG generation. These figures represent typical ranges under normal conditions. Each gold block drops **9 gold ingots** when mined with a Fortune III pickaxe, making them extremely valuable.

## How to Identify Your Bastion Type

Correctly identifying your bastion type **before you land** saves enormous amounts of time. Here is how to read a bastion from the air or at a distance:

### Step 1 — Count the Ramparts

Ramparts are the elevated, balcony-like structures protruding from the bastion body. This is your **first and most important diagnostic step**.

- **3 ramparts visible** → **Hoglin Stables**
- **2 ramparts that are SEPARATED** (a gap exists between them) → **Bridge**
- **2 ramparts that are ADJACENT/TOUCHING** → **Housing**
- **0–1 ramparts, pyramid shape, magma at top** → **Treasure**

> [!TIP]
> Practice counting ramparts during your Nether entries. You'll develop an instinct for it within a few hours of playing. Use F3 to check coordinates and look for the bastion at Y=33 base (Treasure) or Y=27 base depending on type.

### Step 2 — Look at the Overall Shape

- **Treasure**: A large, stepped **pyramid** with a distinctive magma block cap at the top. The structure tapers inward as it rises. A deep **lava moat** surrounds the lower levels. Very tall and imposing.
- **Bridge**: Recognizable by a long **bridge-like walkway** spanning across an open section. The two ramparts are **visibly separated** with open air between them. Often one of the most "open" looking bastions.
- **Stables**: Has a **wide, flat, open** layout. Fences and fence-gated areas are visible from above. You can often see hoglins walking around inside. Three ramparts protrude clearly.
- **Housing**: Has a **blocky, apartment-building** aesthetic with many small rectangular rooms stacked side by side. Two ramparts sit **directly adjacent** to each other.

### Step 3 — Check Surrounding Terrain

Some generation clues can help:
- Treasure bastions often generate with a **lava moat** below them, making the surrounding terrain look like a basin.
- Bridge bastions, true to their name, will often appear to have their main body **raised over a gap or canyon**.
- Stables and Housing can be harder to distinguish purely from terrain, so rely more on rampart counting.

### Visual Cheat Sheet

```
TREASURE  → Pyramid shape, magma top, lava moat, no clear ramparts
BRIDGE    → Two ramparts with a GAP between them
STABLES   → THREE ramparts, wide/flat/open, fences visible
HOUSING   → Two ramparts TOUCHING each other, small block rooms
```

## Gold Armor — The Non-Negotiable Rule

> [!WARNING]
> **You MUST wear at least one piece of gold armor before approaching any Piglin.** Piglins will immediately turn hostile if you are not wearing gold armor. This aggro state persists and will prevent bartering entirely.

### The Rule

Any single piece of gold armor satisfies the requirement:
- Gold Helmet ✅
- Gold Chestplate ✅
- Gold Leggings ✅
- Gold Boots ✅

Most runners equip a **Gold Helmet** because it is the cheapest to craft (5 gold ingots) and leaves the other armor slots free for better protective gear (Iron, Diamond, etc.).

### Piglin Brutes Are Different

**Piglin Brutes do NOT follow the gold armor rule.** They will attack you regardless of what you are wearing, from the moment they spot you. There is no way to pacify a Piglin Brute through armor or trading. Your only options are:

1. **Avoid them entirely** — don't enter their line of sight
2. **Fight them** — they have 50 HP (25 hearts), deal ~7.5 hearts per hit on Java Edition
3. **Pillar up and shoot** — use height advantage with a bow
4. **Use a lava bucket** — instant kill, but wastes your lava bucket

## The Piglin Bartering System

Bartering is the act of throwing **gold ingots** at Piglins (or right-clicking them with a gold ingot in hand) to receive a random item from the barter loot table. This is the primary way to obtain **Ender Pearls** during a speedrun.

### How Bartering Works

1. Hold a gold ingot in your main hand (or have it in your inventory and right-click a Piglin)
2. Right-click on a Piglin — it will grab the ingot and begin inspecting it (takes ~6–8 seconds)
3. After the inspection delay, the Piglin tosses a random item on the ground
4. Pick up the item

### The Loot Table (Key Items)

| Item | Probability | Quantity |
|---|---|---|
| **Ender Pearl** | ~4.73% | 4–8 pearls per trade |
| Iron Nugget | ~10.18% | 10–36 nuggets |
| Iron Boots (Enchanted) | ~8.71% | 1 |
| Gravel | ~8.71% | 8–16 |
| Leather | ~8.71% | 4–10 |
| Nether Quartz | ~8.71% | 8–16 |
| Obsidian | ~8.71% | 1 |
| String | ~4.36% | 8–24 |
| Fire Charge | ~8.71% | 1 |
| Spectral Arrow | ~8.71% | 6–12 |
| Soul Sand | ~8.71% | 4–16 |
| Nether Brick | ~1.74% | 4–16 |
| Blackstone | ~1.74% | 8–16 |
| Crying Obsidian | ~8.71% | 1–3 |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The effective **Ender Pearl rate** is approximately **4.73% per trade**, which means you need roughly **21 gold ingots on average** to receive a single pearl trade. Since each trade gives 4–8 pearls, you generally need around **3 pearl trades** to secure 12 pearls. With Fortune III, a full set of gold blocks from a bastion (~8–12 blocks × 9 ingots = 72–108 ingots) is usually sufficient, but not guaranteed.

### Fortune III vs. No Fortune

Mining gold blocks with a **Fortune III** pickaxe yields **9 gold ingots per block** (the maximum). Without Fortune, you get 7–9 ingots. Always prioritize having Fortune III before mining bastion gold blocks if possible (a Fortune III iron pickaxe from an Overworld village chest is ideal).

## Corralling Piglins

**Corralling** is the technique of herding multiple Piglins into a small space (usually a 1×1 or 2×2 hole you dig in the floor) so that you can barter with many of them simultaneously, drastically reducing the time spent waiting for the barter animation.

### Basic Corralling Method

1. Find a flat section of floor inside or near the bastion
2. Dig a hole **2–3 blocks deep** (so Piglins fall in and can't escape)
3. Lure Piglins toward the hole by walking nearby — they will follow
4. Once multiple Piglins are in the hole, stand at the edge and right-click rapidly with gold ingots
5. All Piglins in the hole will barter simultaneously, multiplying your output rate

> [!TIP]
> You can pre-dig your barter hole before entering the main bastion body, near a safe area away from Piglin Brutes. Some routes designate a specific "barter spot" on the outer edge of the bastion where Piglins naturally congregate.

### Advanced — Mass Barter

With good corralling, runners can throw 20–30+ gold ingots to 5–8 Piglins simultaneously. The barter animations all resolve at roughly the same time, creating a burst of loot. Immediately after throwing, collect all drops and count your pearls. If you have 12+, exit the bastion immediately.
