# Housing Bastion — Complete Guide

> [!WARNING]
> Housing Bastion is the most complex layout of any bastion type. Its multi-room interior, adjacent rampart configuration, and varied Piglin Brute spawn positions create a higher navigation demand than Bridge or Stables. However, once you learn its patterns, Housing is a reliable source of gold and manageable within competitive run times.

## Introduction

The **Housing Bastion** is named after the distinct **"housing unit" rooms** that make up its interior — small, rectangular chambers stacked side by side and on top of each other, resembling a Nether apartment complex built from blackstone. Unlike Treasure Bastion's dramatic pyramid or Stables' open pen layout, Housing is a dense, **multi-room structure** that rewards careful navigation and punishes disorientation.

What makes Housing Bastion unique:

- **Two ADJACENT ramparts**: The two ramparts sit directly beside each other (touching or sharing a wall), as opposed to Bridge Bastion's separated ramparts. This is the primary visual identifier.
- **Complex interior**: Dozens of small "house" rooms with their own loot potential (crafting tables, small chests, barrels) create a maze-like feeling for first-time runners.
- **Missing staircase block**: A specific block is intentionally absent from the main staircase floor — a secondary identifier that distinguishes Housing from other types at close range.
- **High Piglin Brute count**: 2–4 Brutes in typical spawns, comparable to Treasure in danger level.
- **Triple chest rampart at Y=73**: Slightly higher than Stables' Y=72, with a 3-block gap between the double chest pair (vs. Stables' 2-block gap).

Housing is rated **hard** primarily because of navigation complexity — it's easy to get turned around inside and miss gold blocks or take unexpected Brute damage. The top-down route was developed specifically to minimize time spent in the confusing interior.

## Visual Identification

### Primary Identifier — Two ADJACENT Ramparts

The single most important visual cue for Housing Bastion is that its **two ramparts are adjacent** — they touch each other or are directly side by side with no gap between them.

```
HOUSING (correct):
  [Rampart A][Rampart B]
      TOUCHING -- no open air between them

BRIDGE (not this):
  [Rampart A]    [Rampart B]
      GAP -- open air between them

STABLES (not this):
  [Rampart 1]
      [Rampart 2]    <- Three total ramparts
  [Rampart 3]
```

When you see two ramparts with **no clear gap between them** — they share a wall or are immediately adjacent — that is Housing Bastion.

### From the Air

- **Two rampart structures side by side**: From directly above, the two ramparts appear as a **pair** rather than two isolated towers.
- **Dense rectangular rooms**: The bastion body below the ramparts has a very blocky, room-heavy appearance with many uniform rectangles visible.
- **No pyramid shape**: Ruling out Treasure Bastion.
- **Only two ramparts**: Ruling out Stables (which has three).

### From a Distance

- The adjacent ramparts give Housing a distinctive **double-tower silhouette** on one side
- The body is compact and blocky — many right angles visible
- Height is moderate — taller than Bridge's horizontal spread, shorter than Treasure's pyramid

> [!TIP]
> The adjacent rampart double-tower is your "fingerprint" for Housing. When approaching any bastion, look for that paired structure immediately. If you see two ramparts and they look like they're sharing a wall, say "Housing" and proceed with the Top-Down route.

## The Adjacent Rampart Identifier

To understand WHY the adjacent ramparts are so diagnostic, consider that:

- **Bridge** has separated ramparts because it needs both to serve as independent endpoints of its bridge structure
- **Stables** has three ramparts arranged around the stable pens
- **Treasure** has no ramparts at all — its pyramid design doesn't use them
- **Housing** places both ramparts **together** because they serve as the entrance towers to the housing complex

The adjacent placement means:
1. From above, both rampart tops are accessible from a single landing zone (no need to traverse the whole roof)
2. The Piglin Brutes spawning near the ramparts are likely both in the same area
3. Chest access requires dealing with a higher mob density in a smaller space

### Checking from Just Outside

If you're at ground level and unsure:
1. Face the rampart side of the bastion
2. You should see what looks like a **double tower or thick rampart block**
3. Try to walk along the rampart base — if you can go from one rampart to the second without crossing open space, they are adjacent (Housing)
4. If you have to cross a visible gap or open air section, they are separated (Bridge)

## The Missing Staircase Block Identifier

The **missing staircase block** is a secondary identifier specific to Housing Bastion. In the main interior staircase of the housing complex, **one block in the floor is intentionally missing** — creating a 1×1 hole in the staircase landing.

### What It Looks Like

- The main staircase in Housing descends in several L-shaped sections
- At one of the landing areas, a single block is absent from the floor
- This creates a small hole that a player can look through or accidentally fall through

### Why This Matters

1. **Identification**: If you see this missing block while navigating, you can confirm you're in Housing even if you were uncertain from the outside
2. **Navigation**: The missing block marks a reference point in the staircase — "I've passed the missing block, so the gold level is two more flights down"
3. **Danger**: Accidentally falling through it in a hurry can cause fall damage or disorient you mid-run

> [!NOTE]
> The missing block identifier is most useful once you're inside the structure as a navigation and confirmation tool. It is less useful for pre-entry identification (you'd need to be inside to see it), where rampart counting is your primary method.

## Triple Chest Rampart Details — Y=73

The triple chest rampart in Housing follows the same 1 single + 1 double chest configuration as Stables, but with two key differences:

### Height — Y=73

- The triple chest rampart in Housing is accessible at approximately **Y=73**
- This is **1 block higher** than Stables' triple rampart at Y=72
- While a 1-block difference sounds minor, it matters for quickly locating the chest level when you land

### Chest Gap — 3 Blocks

- In Housing's double chest pair on the lower rampart level, the two chests are separated by **3 blocks of wall**
- In Stables, the gap is **2 blocks**
- This means Housing's double chest pair has more wall between them — they're slightly further apart and you have to look around the room more carefully to find both

```
STABLES double chest:   [Chest][Block][Block][Chest]
                                  2-block gap

HOUSING double chest:   [Chest][Block][Block][Block][Chest]
                                    3-block gap
```

### Accessing the Triple Chest Rampart

- Land on the rampart top
- Open the single chest (top level)
- Drop down to the lower tier
- Navigate to find both chests of the double chest — they'll be separated by 3 blocks of wall, so look around the full room

## Stables vs. Housing Differentiation

This is one of the most commonly confused pairings in MCSR Ranked. Both types have a triple chest rampart with a single + double chest configuration. The following table clarifies all key differences:

| Feature | Hoglin Stables | Housing |
|---|---|---|
| **Rampart Count** | **THREE** | **TWO** |
| **Rampart Arrangement** | Spaced apart | **Adjacent/touching** |
| **Triple Rampart Y-Level** | **Y=72** | **Y=73** |
| **Double Chest Gap** | **2 blocks** | **3 blocks** |
| **Interior Type** | Open pens with fences | Enclosed room maze |
| **Hoglins Present?** | **YES** — many | No |
| **Staircase Missing Block?** | No | **YES** |
| **Gold Block Access** | Sub-structure below deck | Interior rooms + exterior |
| **Difficulty Rating** | Medium | Hard |

### The Quick Mental Check

If you see **three ramparts** → it's definitely **Stables**.
If you see **two ramparts touching** → it's definitely **Housing**.
If you see **two ramparts separated** → it's definitely **Bridge**.

The Stables/Housing confusion only arises when you can't clearly count or see the ramparts. In that case:

- **Are there Hoglins and fences?** → Stables
- **Are there small rectangular "house" rooms everywhere?** → Housing
- **Is the Y-level of the chest area ~72?** → Stables. **~73?** → Housing

> [!TIP]
> Drill the Stables vs. Housing differences in creative mode. Spawn bastions using `/locate` and walk through both types, specifically noting the rampart touching vs. separation, the Y-level of the triple rampart, and the chest gap size. 30 minutes of creative practice will eliminate confusion permanently.

## Route Strategy — Top-Down Approach

The **Top-Down Approach** is the standard optimized route for Housing Bastion. It uses the high vantage point advantage (landing on the rampart tops) to quickly access chests before descending through the housing interior to reach gold blocks and bartering.

### Philosophy

Instead of entering from the base of the structure and navigating upward through the complex interior (which causes disorientation), the Top-Down route has you start at the **highest accessible point** (the adjacent rampart tops) and work downward systematically. This keeps you moving in one direction and reduces the chance of getting lost.

---

### Step 1 — Approach and Identify

- Approach from above, circle once to identify:
  - Which of the two adjacent ramparts is the triple chest rampart (larger/taller one)
  - Piglin Brute positions on the rampart tops
  - Approximate Brute count in the visible interior
- Confirm this is Housing (two adjacent ramparts, rectangular rooms visible)

### Step 2 — Land on the Triple Chest Rampart

- Land directly on the **top of the triple chest rampart** (the larger of the two adjacent ramparts)
- Immediately check for Brutes on the rampart top — if one is there, decide: avoidance or fight
- Open the single chest at the top immediately; take all contents

### Step 3 — Access the Adjacent Single Chest Rampart

- Step across to the immediately adjacent (touching) second rampart
- Open its single chest
- This is easy in Housing because both ramparts are right next to each other — no traversal required

### Step 4 — Drop to Triple Rampart's Lower Level

- Return to the triple chest rampart and drop to its lower tier (controlled drop)
- Navigate to the double chest — remember, the two chests are separated by 3 blocks of wall
- Open both chests and collect all loot

### Step 5 — Mine Rampart Gold Blocks

- Mine all gold blocks visible on both adjacent ramparts — walls, floor, ceiling
- Both ramparts together typically yield 2–6 gold blocks

### Step 6 — Descend into the Housing Interior

- From the rampart lower level, look for the interior entrance to the housing body
- The main staircase descends from near the rampart level down through the housing units
- Begin descending the staircase carefully — Brutes may be patrolling interior landings

### Step 7 — Watch for the Missing Block

- As you descend the staircase, watch for the **missing floor block** — the 1×1 hole
- Don't fall through it accidentally; use it as a navigation reference point
- This marks roughly the middle section of the staircase

### Step 8 — Sweep the Housing Interior

- The interior housing units each have a small amount of loot potential (barrels, small chests)
- Focus on any **obvious chests visible while passing through** — don't deviate from your path to search every room
- Navigate toward the bottom level where gold blocks are concentrated

### Step 9 — Mine Interior and Lower-Level Gold Blocks

- The gold blocks in Housing are concentrated in **lower level walls and the sub-structure** of the housing body
- Mine all gold blocks you encounter on your way down
- Keep moving; don't get sidetracked by the maze of rooms

### Step 10 — Exit the Interior and Set Up Barter

- Once you've collected interior gold blocks, exit the housing body at the base level
- Find a flat area outside or at the base of the bastion
- Dig your barter hole (2×1, 2–3 deep)
- Corral Piglins from the surrounding area

### Step 11 — Barter and Count

- Barter all available gold ingots
- Collect drops and count Ender Pearls
- Target: 12+ pearls

### Step 12 — Exit Toward Fortress

- If pearl count is met, exit immediately
- If short, sweep for any missed gold blocks before leaving

## The Manhunt Route Alternative

The **Manhunt Route** is an alternative to the Top-Down approach, used in specific situations.

### How It Differs

Instead of starting at the top rampart and descending, the Manhunt Route:
1. Enters the bastion from a **middle entry point** (usually a window-like opening in the bastion exterior wall)
2. Navigates directly to the **middle chest** (the single chest in the triple rampart, accessed from inside rather than from above)
3. Collects the chest, then descends directly to the gold block level
4. Skips or defers the double chest if it requires excessive backtracking

### When to Use Manhunt

- When the rampart tops are heavily guarded by Brutes and landing on top is risky
- When the bastion is at a lower elevation and a middle-wall entry is naturally accessible
- When you're confident in Housing's interior layout and want the slightly faster routing for specific seed configurations

> [!NOTE]
> The Manhunt Route is considered an **intermediate-to-advanced** technique. Beginners should master the Top-Down approach first. The Manhunt route requires good knowledge of Housing's interior layout to avoid getting lost when entering from the middle.

## Housing Interior — The House Units

The defining visual feature of Housing Bastion is its interior "house" rooms. Understanding them helps navigation.

### What Are the House Units?

Each "house" is a small, enclosed room, approximately **5×5 to 7×7 blocks** in floor area, with a ceiling of 3–4 blocks. They are stacked vertically and arranged in rows horizontally. Each unit typically contains:

- **Crafting Table** (no loot, but confirms you're in a house unit)
- **Barrel** (occasionally — contains a small amount of loot)
- **Small Chest** (occasionally — contains loot similar to rampart chests but lower quantity)
- Piglins wandering through

### Navigating the Maze

The house units can disorient first-time runners. Tips for staying oriented:

- **Follow the staircase**: The main central staircase is always accessible — if you get lost, find the staircase and use it as an anchor
- **Use the missing block**: The missing staircase block marks a known point in the descent
- **Look for light**: Natural Nether light or lava glow often indicates openings and exits
- **F3 coordinate tracking**: Keep an eye on your Y-coordinate — you want to descend toward Y=30–40 where the gold blocks are concentrated

### House Unit Loot Value

In a time-pressured speedrun, individual house unit loot is **low priority**. The chests and barrels in house units are worth checking only if you're already passing by — don't deviate from your route to search each room.

## Gold Block Locations in Housing

### Location Priority Table

| Location | Approx. Blocks | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Triple rampart walls/floor | 1–3 | Very High |
| Single rampart walls/floor | 1–2 | High |
| Lower interior walls | 3–5 | Very High |
| Sub-structure support columns | 2–4 | High |
| Exterior bastion body walls | 2–4 | Medium |
| House unit interior walls | 0–2 | Low |

### Where to Look

1. **Rampart walls**: Already covered during the rampart looting phase — mine as you go.

2. **Lower interior walls**: These are the walls of the housing body at the **base tier** (approximately Y=35–50). Gold blocks are embedded in the wall faces here. This is the richest single zone.

3. **Support columns**: The large blackstone pillar structures that support the housing tiers often have gold blocks embedded partway up. Check all four sides of each column you pass.

4. **Exterior walls**: Walking around the outside perimeter at mid-height reveals several gold blocks on the wall faces. Do this sweep after exiting the interior.

5. **House unit walls**: Occasionally, a gold block appears in the wall of a house unit — visible as you pass through the room. Collect it opportunistically but don't hunt for it specifically.

> [!TIP]
> The **total gold block yield** in Housing (typically 6–12 blocks with Fortune III) is slightly lower on average than Bridge or Treasure, making efficient mining routes even more important. Don't leave any block behind.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Confusing Housing with Bridge**: Both have two ramparts. The key difference is whether they're touching (Housing) or separated (Bridge). Practice this distinction until it's automatic.

2. **Confusing Housing with Stables**: Stables has three ramparts; Housing has two. If you get to the bastion and are unsure, count again.

3. **Getting lost in the interior**: Housing's maze of rooms is the number one time waster for new runners. Learn the staircase route and stick to it — explore rooms only as you pass through, not as dedicated exploration.

4. **Missing the triple rampart double chest**: The 3-block wall gap between the double chests means they're not side by side — beginners often find one and think there's only one. Always look around the full lower rampart room for the second chest.

5. **Spending too long in house units**: Tempting to check every barrel and chest in every room. In a speedrun, this adds minutes of time for marginal loot gains.

6. **Falling through the missing staircase block**: Sprinting down the staircase without noticing the hole. Take one moment to identify it and step around it.

7. **Not using the adjacent rampart advantage**: Both ramparts are right next to each other — players sometimes don't realize they can loot both rampart tops within 5 seconds. Take advantage of the adjacency.

8. **Not accounting for Brute patrol routes**: Housing Brutes often patrol the staircase landings. Rushing down without checking each landing is a common source of unexpected damage.

9. **Bartering inside the bastion**: The enclosed interior makes it hard to corral Piglins and creates Brute risk. Always exit to an open area for bartering.

10. **Leaving before checking pearl count**: Count your pearls before leaving any bastion. Discovering you have only 8 pearls after you've already left Housing and started toward the Fortress is a major setback.

## Tips & Tricks

### Identification Speed

- **Develop a 1-second rampart check**: When approaching, immediately look for ramparts. Two touching = Housing. Don't spend 5 seconds thinking about it.
- **Use the block texture as backup**: Housing's interior has a characteristic crimson plank + blackstone brick combination that differs from Stables' more fence-heavy interior.

### Navigation

- **The staircase is always your anchor**: When you feel lost, stop moving, identify the staircase from sounds (footsteps echo differently on stairs vs. flat floors), and return to it.
- **Watch your Y-level**: Rampart chests at ~Y=73, gold blocks starting at ~Y=50 and lower, barter level at ground (Y=30–45).
- **Don't sprint in corridors**: Walking speed is safer in the tight housing interior — it gives you time to react to Brutes around corners.

### Combat

- **Brutes on staircase landings**: If a Brute is on a landing, you have a height advantage from the stairs above. Critical hits (jump + swing from above) deal maximum damage and the staircase choke point prevents the Brute from flanking you.
- **Shield blocking**: In Housing's tight corridors, a shield is extremely valuable. Brute attacks in corridors are difficult to dodge without a shield or precise movement.

### Efficiency

- **Pre-plan your descent**: Before dropping into the housing interior, know where you're going — rampart, then staircase, then lower interior gold blocks, then exit. Having this sequence memorized eliminates decision-making during the run.
- **Leave rooms with nothing obvious**: If you glance into a house unit and see no chest or barrel, don't enter. Move on immediately.
- **Gold mining pattern**: Mine gold blocks in a loop — ramparts → exterior walls → interior lower walls → support columns. One continuous loop is faster than back-and-forth.

> [!TIP]
> Housing Bastion rewards **structural knowledge** more than any other type. The more runs you do at Housing, the faster your routes become — not because of improved mechanics, but because you instinctively know where every block, chest, and Brute can be. Invest the time in learning its layout.
