# The Nether — Overview & General Navigation

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The Nether is the most RNG-dependent and mechanically demanding phase of an MCSR Ranked run. Mastering its navigation, structure routing, and gold economy will dramatically lower your finish times and death rate.

## 1. Introduction to the Nether Phase

The Nether is the second major phase of a Minecraft Speedrun Any% (Set Seed or Random Seed). After gathering resources in the Overworld and building or entering a ruined portal, you enter the Nether with one core objective: **collect the materials needed to craft Eyes of Ender** so you can locate and enter a Stronghold.

### Your Two Primary Goals

| Goal | Source | Why You Need It |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| **Blaze Rods** | Nether Fortress (Blaze spawners) | Crafted into Blaze Powder for Eyes of Ender |
| **Ender Pearls** | Bastion Remnant (Piglin barter) | Combined with Blaze Powder to make Eyes of Ender |

Both structures must be found and cleared efficiently. Neither goal is optional — you cannot finish the run without both.

### Bastion First vs. Fortress First

This is one of the most debated routing decisions in MCSR Ranked. Here is the breakdown:

**Bastion First (recommended for most runners)**
- You barter gold for Ender Pearls early, while you have full HP and resources
- Bastions often contain extra gold blocks that fund more trades
- Killing the Piglin Brute in the bastion makes the area safe for looting
- You enter the Fortress already knowing your pearl count, letting you judge if you need more Blaze Rods
- The most common approach in top-level play

**Fortress First**
- Useful if your nether spawn is directly adjacent to a Fortress
- Blaze farming is deterministic (unlike RNG barter), so you always know your powder count early
- Some runners prefer this when they find the Fortress before the Bastion on their pie chart
- Risk: you may waste time farming Blazes only to then barter poorly and need even more pearls

**General Rule of Thumb:**
> If both structures are roughly equidistant, go Bastion first. If the Fortress is clearly much closer (visible or very near on the pie chart), consider hitting it first. The goal is always to minimize total travel distance.

## 2. General Nether Goals

### Blaze Rod Count

Blaze Rods are obtained by killing Blazes at a Nether Fortress. Each Blaze drops **0–1 Blaze Rods** (average ~0.5 per kill without Looting).

**The math:**
- 1 Blaze Rod → crafts into **2 Blaze Powder**
- 1 Eye of Ender = **1 Blaze Powder + 1 Ender Pearl**
- Stronghold activation requires up to **12 Eyes of Ender** (for a fully empty portal)
- In practice, most portals have 0–3 eyes pre-filled, so you realistically need **9–12 Eyes**

| Blaze Rod Target | Blaze Powder Yield | Eyes Possible (with 12 pearls) |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| 7 rods (minimum) | 14 powder | 12 eyes (just enough) |
| 8 rods (safe minimum) | 16 powder | 12 eyes + 4 spare |
| 10 rods (comfortable) | 20 powder | 12 eyes + 8 spare |
| 12 rods (very safe) | 24 powder | 12 eyes + 12 spare |

> [!TIP]
> Aim for **10 Blaze Rods** as your target. This gives you a cushion for missing Eye throws on the stronghold hunt and losing eyes entering the End portal room. 7–8 is the hard minimum; below that you risk not having enough powder.

### Ender Pearl Count

Ender Pearls come from **bartering with Piglins** inside the Bastion Remnant. You throw gold ingots at Piglins and they toss back random loot — Ender Pearls are one of the possible barter results.

- Barter loot table: Ender Pearls have roughly a **~4.7% chance** per barter (Java Edition 1.16+)
- Each successful pearl barter gives **4–8 Ender Pearls**
- You need a minimum of **12 Ender Pearls** to make 12 Eyes of Ender

**Pearl targets:**
- **12 pearls** — absolute minimum for a perfect portal
- **14–16 pearls** — comfortable, leaves buffer for throwing at stronghold
- **20+ pearls** — very safe but usually costs too much gold/time at high-level play

> [!NOTE]
> Pearl bartering is **pure RNG**. You may barter 24 gold and get 20 pearls, or barter 24 gold and get 4. Always enter the Nether with more gold than you think you need. If pearls are very bad, remember that many portals spawn with 1–4 eyes already inserted — check before panicking.

### Gold Armor — Why It Is Mandatory

Piglins are neutral mobs: they will **not attack you** as long as you are wearing at least **one piece of gold armor**. The moment you remove all gold armor (or if you never put it on), every Piglin in the area becomes hostile.

- **Wear a gold helmet** — it is the cheapest piece (5 gold ingots) and provides full Piglin immunity
- Without it, Piglins will swarm you the moment you open a chest, pick up gold, or walk past them
- Piglin Brutes (found in bastions) are **always hostile** regardless of armor — they must be killed
- Wearing gold armor does NOT affect Hoglin aggression; Hoglins attack on sight regardless

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Craft or obtain a gold helmet **before** entering the Nether, or immediately upon entry if you find gold nearby. Never open bastion chests without it equipped — you will be swarmed and likely killed instantly.

## 3. Entering the Nether

### What the Nether Looks Like

The Nether contains several distinct biomes. Recognizing them quickly helps you orient yourself and predict where structures are:

| Biome | Appearance | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| **Nether Wastes** | Red/orange netherrack, lava lakes, ghasts | Most common; Fortresses and Bastions can spawn here |
| **Crimson Forest** | Dense red mushroom trees, crimson nylium floor | Bastions can spawn here; Hoglins are common |
| **Warped Forest** | Blue-green mushroom trees, endermen | No hostile mobs except Endermen; safe to traverse |
| **Soul Sand Valley** | Blue soul sand/soil floor, bone spires, skeleton spawning | Very dangerous; lots of ghasts and skeleton spawning |
| **Basalt Deltas** | Gray basalt columns, magma blocks, heavy magma cube spawning | **Bastions CANNOT spawn here**; avoid if looking for one |

### What NOT to Do Upon Entering

The Nether is extremely punishing for new players. Avoid these common mistakes:

- ❌ **Don't sprint toward lava** — Nether terrain often has sudden drops into lava lakes; look before you move
- ❌ **Don't ignore Ghasts** — a fireball can knock you off a ledge or into lava from full health
- ❌ **Don't walk near Hoglins without a warped fungus** — they deal significant knockback and can send you flying off cliffs
- ❌ **Don't open your inventory immediately** — orient yourself and mark your portal first
- ❌ **Don't break blocks** adjacent to a lava pool without checking your surroundings first

### Immediate Actions After Entering

Follow this checklist the moment you step through the portal:

1. **Mark your portal** — Place a distinctive block (dirt pillar, torches, or a uniquely colored block) on or next to the portal. The Nether all looks the same; you will get lost. Your portal is precious — losing it means losing your run.
2. **Equip gold armor** — Put on your gold helmet (or whatever gold armor piece you have). Do this before moving toward any Piglins.
3. **Assess your biome** — Look around. Are you in Nether Wastes? Crimson Forest? This tells you roughly what to expect nearby.
4. **Check your coordinates** — Press F3 and note your X and Z values. Your portal is at (approximately) these coordinates in the Nether. Write them down mentally or remember them.
5. **Begin pie chart scanning** — Use Shift+F3 to open the performance overlay pie chart to start identifying nearby structures.

## 4. The Two Key Structures

### Bastion Remnant

Bastions are large, multi-level fortress-like structures made of Blackstone, Polished Blackstone, and Gilded Blackstone. They are home to **Piglins** (your trading partners) and **Piglin Brutes** (aggressive guards).

There are four types of Bastions:
- **Treasure Bastion** — Has a central bridge and a large loot room filled with gold blocks; the most lucrative
- **Housing Bastion** — Multiple small rooms connected by corridors; moderate loot throughout
- **Stables Bastion** — Open lower stable areas with Hoglins; moderate chest loot
- **Bridge Bastion** — Long bridge structure crossing a ravine; moderate loot density

**What you do at a Bastion:**
- Kill the Piglin Brute(s) immediately to make the area safe for looting
- Loot chests for gold ingots, and mine any gold blocks present
- Barter gold with Piglins by dropping ingots near them (right-click or Q to throw)
- Collect Ender Pearls from successful barter results

> 📄 **See the full Bastion guide:** [Bastion Remnant Strategies](./bastion_remnant.md)

---

### Nether Fortress

Nether Fortresses are large bridge-and-corridor structures made of Nether Brick. They contain **Blaze spawners** — the source of Blaze Rods. They also contain Wither Skeletons and Blazes patrolling the corridors.

**What you do at a Fortress:**
- Navigate to a **Blaze spawner** (found in open, roofed spawner rooms along the outer bridge corridors)
- Kill enough Blazes to collect your target rod count
- Use the spawner efficiently — stand at the optimal position to trigger rapid mob spawns

> 📄 **See the full Fortress guide:** [Nether Fortress Strategies](./nether_fortress.md)

## 5. Navigation Tips

Navigating the Nether efficiently is a learnable skill. These are the core tools every runner uses.

### The Pie Chart (Shift+F3 Debug Overlay)

The **F3 pie chart** is the most important navigation tool in the Nether. It's a performance breakdown chart that experienced runners use to detect which direction a structure is loading.

**How to open it:**
- Press **Shift + F3** to toggle the pie chart overlay in the bottom-right corner of your screen

**How it works:**
- The pie chart shows how much time the game spends processing different subsystems
- When structures are generating or loading nearby, certain entries in the breakdown spike
- Runners use this to detect **which direction a structure is loading** by rotating and watching entries change

**Step-by-step pie chart navigation:**
1. Press **Shift+F3** to open the pie chart
2. Identify the relevant entries (usually under "Level" or entity processing sections)
3. Rotate your character slowly left and right while walking
4. When the relevant entry **grows larger or spikes**, you are generally facing toward that structure
5. Walk in that direction; the spike grows as you close the distance

> [!NOTE]
> Pie chart navigation is a core advanced technique that takes significant practice. Beginners should focus on visually scanning for structures first, then learn pie charting as they develop. Watch top runners use it to understand the visual cues.

---

### Render Distance Adjustment (F3 + F)

- Press **F3 + F** to **increase render distance** by 1 chunk
- Press **F3 + Shift + F** to **decrease render distance** by 1 chunk
- Temporarily raising render distance (e.g., from 8 to 12 or 16) helps you visually spot Bastions and Fortresses from much farther away
- Lower it again after spotting to restore framerate and game performance

**Tips for effective render distance scanning:**
- Climb to the highest point you can find (tall netherrack column, top of a cliff) before increasing render distance
- Bastions appear as large dark silhouettes of blackstone blocks
- Fortresses appear as red-brown nether brick bridges extending across the terrain
- Even at moderate render distances, the **distinctive outline** of either structure often peeks through Nether fog

---

### Biome Identification for Structure Hunting

Structures have specific biome restrictions in Java Edition 1.16+. Using this knowledge saves significant time during your run:

- **Bastions** can spawn in: Nether Wastes, Crimson Forest, Warped Forest, Soul Sand Valley
- **Bastions CANNOT spawn in**: Basalt Deltas — if you're surrounded by gray basalt columns and magma cubes, move on immediately
- **Fortresses** can spawn in any Nether biome, but tend to be easier to spot in Nether Wastes
- **Piglin natural spawning** happens in Nether Wastes and Crimson Forest — seeing groups of Piglins wandering may suggest a Bastion is not too far

**Practical biome navigation tips:**
- If you enter a Basalt Delta biome, head perpendicular to it to exit quickly
- Check the biome in the F3 debug screen (look for the **"Biome:"** line)
- Soul Sand Valley has a distinctive pale-blue fog — useful for orientation even from a distance
- Crimson Forest with its red mushroom trees is very recognizable; Bastions commonly spawn within or near them

---

### Ghast Awareness

Ghasts are large flying mobs that shoot explosive fireballs. They are common in Nether Wastes and Soul Sand Valley and pose a significant threat to both your health and your portal.

**Deflecting fireballs:**
- You can **hit a fireball back at a Ghast** by left-clicking (attacking) it while it's airborne
- Time your click for just before the fireball reaches you — the hitbox is generous
- A successfully returned fireball deals significant damage to the Ghast and often kills it outright
- This works with **any item**, including bare hands — you do not need a specific weapon

**When to fight vs. when to run:**
- **Fight:** When a Ghast is directly blocking your path or hovering over the entrance to the Fortress or Bastion you need
- **Run:** When a Ghast is off to the side and not directly threatening your movement — fighting it wastes precious seconds
- **Priority rule:** Never stop moving toward your objective because of a Ghast unless it is actively blocking you or about to push you into lava

> [!WARNING]
> Ghast fireballs can extinguish your Nether Portal. If you leave your portal unprotected and a Ghast shoots through the frame, the obsidian survives but the fire inside goes out. Always check that your portal is still lit when you return to it. Carry Flint and Steel.

## 6. Gold Management

Gold is the currency of the Nether. Managing it well is the difference between a smooth run and a failed barter session with no pearls.

### How Much Gold Do You Need?

Each barter with a Piglin costs **1 gold ingot**. The Ender Pearl barter result is random, so you need enough gold to statistically secure the pearls you need.

| Scenario | Gold Ingots Recommended |
|----------|------------------------|
| Minimum (desperate run) | 8–10 ingots |
| Standard / Comfortable | 20–24 ingots |
| Safe / High variance protection | 30–32 ingots |

- **8 ingots** may get you 0 pearls or 16 pearls — it's essentially a coinflip
- **24 ingots** gives you a reasonable statistical spread — on average you'll trigger ~1 pearl barter per 21 attempts, yielding ~5–6 pearl batches (roughly 20–36 pearls total from lucky runs)
- Top runners often carry **20–24 ingots** as their baseline, then top up with Bastion gold

> [!TIP]
> Always plan to supplement your Overworld gold with gold found inside the Bastion. Arrive with at least 10 ingots, then mine gold blocks and loot chests inside. Treasure Bastions in particular can net you 40+ additional gold ingots from their loot room.

### Where to Get Gold

**From the Overworld (pre-Nether):**
- **Ruined Portals** — The chest almost always contains gold ingots; occasionally has a gold block (9 ingots)
- **Village blacksmith chests** — May contain 1–3 gold ingots
- **Buried treasure** — Sometimes contains gold ingots as part of the loot table
- **Desert temple chests** — Occasionally contain gold ingots
- **Zombie Piglin farms** — Drop gold nuggets when killed; 9 nuggets = 1 ingot. Only worth it if they're literally in your path near the ruined portal

**Inside the Nether:**
- **Bastion Remnant chests** — Often contain 4–10 gold ingots per chest; Treasure Bastions have the most chests
- **Gold blocks in Treasure Bastions** — The central treasure room is lined with gold blocks; each yields 9 ingots and there can be 8–15 blocks in one room
- **Nether Gold Ore** — Naturally generates in netherrack; drops 2–6 gold nuggets per ore block. Very inefficient mid-run; ignore unless you have nothing else
- **Killing Zombie Piglins** — Drops gold nuggets; only do this if your gold count is critically low

### Gold Armor Crafting

You need **at least one piece of gold armor** at all times in the Nether when near Piglins. Here are your options ranked by gold cost:

| Armor Piece | Gold Ingots Required | Coverage |
|-------------|---------------------|----------|
| Boots | 4 ingots | Feet only — minimum viable |
| Helmet | 5 ingots | Head only — **recommended choice** |
| Leggings | 7 ingots | Legs — moderate protection |
| Chestplate | 8 ingots | Torso — highest protection, highest cost |

**Standard recommendation:** Craft a **gold helmet** (5 ingots) before entering the Nether.
- It is cheap and preserves the rest of your gold for bartering
- Provides full Piglin immunity (same as any other piece)
- Combined with iron armor underneath, gives adequate overall protection
- If you found a gold helmet from a Ruined Portal chest or bastion, you saved 5 ingots — use those for bartering

> [!TIP]
> Some runners craft a gold chestplate (8 ingots) if they have surplus gold, as it provides better protection against Blazes and Piglin Brutes. However, for budget runs, the helmet is always sufficient.

## 7. Portal Relighting

Nether Portals can be **extinguished** by several causes:
- Ghast fireballs striking the inside of the obsidian frame
- Accidentally hitting the inner fire while navigating near the portal
- Certain server or plugin interactions (less relevant for solo runs)

If your portal goes dark (the purple swirling particle effect disappears), you **cannot leave the Nether** until it is relit.

### How to Relight a Portal

**Method 1: Flint and Steel (preferred and most reliable)**
- Crafted from 1 iron ingot + 1 flint (flint drops from gravel)
- You should **always carry one in your hotbar** during a Nether run — this is non-negotiable
- Right-click any block inside the obsidian frame to relight the portal instantly
- Flint and Steel has durability; it lasts many uses before breaking, so one is enough per run

**Method 2: Fire Charge**
- Occasionally found in Bastion Remnant chests, Ruined Portal chests, or Nether Fortress chests
- Right-click the inside of the obsidian frame — functions identically to Flint and Steel for this purpose
- A useful backup if you somehow lost your Flint and Steel during the run
- Single use per Fire Charge, so carry multiple if relying on them

**Method 3: Deflect a Ghast Fireball into the Frame**
- If a Ghast happens to be shooting at you while your portal is unlit, you can aim a deflected fireball into the frame to relight it
- This is an extremely situational trick — it requires a Ghast to be nearby, shooting at the right angle, at the right time
- It has saved runs in tournament play, but should never be your plan
- Treat this as a happy accident rather than a strategy

### Prevention Tips

- Build your Nether Portal in a **sheltered or enclosed area** where Ghasts cannot see it
- After passing through your portal, **glance back** to confirm the purple swirl is still active
- If you're spending a long time at a distant structure, the risk to your portal is lower — but always verify on return

> [!CAUTION]
> Entering the Nether without Flint and Steel is a run-ending mistake if your portal ever gets extinguished. Always carry one. It weighs nothing, costs almost nothing, and is absolutely critical insurance.

## 8. Leaving the Nether

Knowing **when to leave** is just as important as knowing what to do inside. Many beginners over-farm trying to get perfect counts; top runners leave the moment they have enough.

### The Exit Checklist

Before stepping back through your Nether Portal, verify the following:

**Blaze Rods:**
- [ ] Do you have at least **7 Blaze Rods** (hard minimum)?
- [ ] Ideally, do you have **10 Blaze Rods** (comfortable target)?
- [ ] 12+ Blaze Rods? You are very safe — leave now

**Ender Pearls:**
- [ ] Do you have at least **12 Ender Pearls**?
- [ ] If not, estimate expected portal pre-fills: most portals average 3 pre-filled eyes, meaning you may only need 9 Eyes of Ender — so 9 pearls might be acceptable
- [ ] If you're at 8–9 pearls and barter was terrible, mentally calculate if the run is still viable before staying longer

**Resources and Inventory:**
- [ ] Flint and Steel is in your hotbar
- [ ] You have food remaining for Overworld travel
- [ ] Remove gold helmet if you want (no longer needed)
- [ ] Drop excess Nether debris or worthless loot to free inventory space

### Making the Call: When Is "Enough"?

| Situation | Decision |
|-----------|----------|
| 10+ rods + 14+ pearls | ✅ Leave immediately, excellent shape |
| 10 rods + 12 pearls | ✅ Leave — clean exit |
| 10 rods + 10 pearls | ✅ Leave — most portals cover the gap |
| 7 rods + 12 pearls | ⚠️ Acceptable, leave now — powder will be tight |
| 7 rods + 9 pearls | ⚠️ Risky, consider staying for a few more rods |
| 7 rods + 8 pearls or fewer | ❌ Stay — too tight; farm more rods or try more barters |
| 12+ rods + 12+ pearls | ✅ Leave immediately — don't over-farm |

> [!TIP]
> A common runner mistake is **farming too long for comfort**. Every extra minute in the Nether is a minute not spent traveling to the Stronghold. Once you hit the acceptable threshold, leave. Speed wins races, not perfect inventory counts.

### After Leaving — Immediate Next Steps

Once back in the Overworld, do these in order:

1. **Craft Blaze Powder** — Open your crafting table (or 2x2 grid) and convert all Blaze Rods into Blaze Powder (1 rod → 2 powder)
2. **Craft Eyes of Ender** — Combine Blaze Powder + Ender Pearls (1:1 ratio) to make Eyes of Ender
3. **Throw an Eye of Ender** — Right-click with it in your hand; it flies toward the nearest Stronghold then either returns to you or shatters (1 in 5 chance of shattering)
4. **Triangulate the Stronghold** — Throw 2–3 Eyes from different positions to narrow down the exact location
5. **Dig into the Stronghold** — Find the End Portal room, insert remaining Eyes into any empty frame slots, and jump through
